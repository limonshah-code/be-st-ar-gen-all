### Step-by-Step Guide to Routine Cleaning

Routine cleaning keeps your Simplehuman trash can fresh. This process is simple and quick. Do this every week or every other week. This stops dirt and odors from building up. It keeps your kitchen smelling good.

Start by removing the trash bag. Wipe the inside of the liner. Use a damp cloth with a little dish soap. Then, wipe down the outside. Pay attention to the lid and pedal area. These parts get touched often. This routine cleaning prevents tough messes.

This method does not take much time. It helps maintain a clean home. It also makes deep cleaning less frequent. A regular clean keeps your bin hygienic. It makes your Simplehuman trash can a cleaner part of your home.

#### Emptying and Initial Rinse

First, remove the trash bag from the can. Take out the inner liner if your model has one. Carry the liner to a sink or outdoor area. A utility sink or a garden hose works well. Rinse the liner with warm water. This removes loose debris and food particles.

Check the bottom of the main can body for any spills. Sometimes bags leak. Wipe up any liquids or crumbs with a paper towel. This first step gets rid of the major mess. It prepares the surfaces for actual cleaning. This makes the next steps more effective.

#### Wiping Down the Exterior

Mix a small amount of mild dish soap with warm water in a bowl. Dip a soft microfiber cloth into the soapy water. Wring out the cloth so it is damp, not dripping. Carefully wipe down all exterior surfaces of your Simplehuman trash can. This includes the lid, body, and pedal.

Pay close attention to areas with fingerprints or smudges. Stainless steel models often show these marks. Wipe in the direction of the grain on stainless steel. This helps avoid streaks. Use a clean, damp cloth to wipe off any soap residue. Finally, dry the exterior with a clean, dry cloth. This prevents water spots and keeps the finish shiny.

### Deep Cleaning Your Simplehuman Trash Can

Sometimes, routine cleaning is not enough. Your trash can might have lingering odors. It might have tough stains or sticky residue. This means it is time for a deep clean. Deep cleaning gets rid of built-up grime. It also helps to sanitize the bin. This process makes your trash can truly fresh again.

Deep cleaning involves more than just wiping surfaces. You will focus on the inner liner. You will also use stronger cleaning agents. This method addresses odors at their source. It ensures thorough disinfection. Plan to deep clean your Simplehuman trash can once a month. You might do it more often if you throw away a lot of food waste.

This extra effort makes a big difference. It helps maintain the hygiene of your home. It keeps your Simplehuman trash can in top condition. You will appreciate a truly clean and fresh bin.

#### Addressing the Inner Liner

The inner liner holds most of the trash. It gets the dirtiest. Take the liner outside or to a large utility sink. Use warm water and mild dish soap. If the liner is very dirty, you can fill it with warm, soapy water. Let it soak for 15-30 minutes. This helps loosen stuck-on grime.

After soaking, scrub the inside and outside of the liner. Use a soft brush or a non-abrasive sponge. Pay attention to corners and edges where dirt collects. Rinse the liner thoroughly with clean water. Make sure all soap residue is gone. Prop the liner upside down to drain and air dry completely. Drying prevents mold and mildew.

#### Deodorizing Techniques

Bad smells often come from residual odors in the plastic liner or main bin body. White vinegar is excellent for deodorizing. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Spray the inside of the clean liner and the interior of the main can. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes. Vinegar neutralizes odors.

Baking soda is another great odor absorber. Sprinkle a generous amount of baking soda into the dry liner. Let it sit overnight. The baking soda will absorb any lingering smells. Then, just dump it out. For a powerful duo, you can learn how to clean with vinegar and baking soda for maximum freshness. You can also place a small sachet of baking soda at the bottom of your bin after cleaning for ongoing odor control.

#### Special Care for Stainless Steel

Simplehuman stainless steel trash cans look sleek. They need specific care to avoid scratches and streaks. Always use a soft cloth. Never use abrasive cleaners or scrub pads. These will damage the finish. Mild dish soap or a dedicated stainless steel cleaner works best.

When wiping, follow the grain of the steel. This helps to prevent streaks. After cleaning, use a dry microfiber cloth to buff the surface. This brings back the shine. Some people use a small amount of olive oil on a cloth. Wipe it onto the stainless steel. Then buff it. This creates a protective barrier. It also helps repel fingerprints.

### Tackling Stubborn Odors and Stains

Sometimes, odors and stains persist even after deep cleaning. This can happen from spills, pet waste, or spoiled food. Do not worry. There are stronger methods for these tough problems. You can get rid of these issues with specific cleaning agents. This section helps you target those extra challenging messes.

Dealing with these problems promptly helps. It stops them from becoming permanent. It also prevents attracting pests. Knowing these tricks keeps your Simplehuman trash can truly fresh. You can solve almost any mess inside your bin.

#### Eliminating Lingering Smells

For very stubborn odors, a bleach solution can be used. Mix 1/2 cup of bleach with one gallon of water. Pour this solution into the cleaned liner. Let it sit for about 30 minutes. This will disinfect and kill odor-causing bacteria. Rinse thoroughly multiple times with clean water. Make sure no bleach residue remains. Air dry completely outdoors if possible. For alternative strong mold and odor solutions, you can also explore how to clean mold with bleach.

Another option for strong odors is activated charcoal. Place a bag or bowl of activated charcoal inside the empty bin overnight. It absorbs odors without adding any scent. Coffee grounds can also work as a temporary odor absorber. Spread fresh coffee grounds in the bottom of the liner. Leave them for a few hours. Then, discard them.

#### Dealing with Mold and Mildew

Mold and mildew thrive in damp, dark environments. Trash cans offer such conditions. If you see black or green spots, you have mold. White vinegar is effective for mild mold. Spray full-strength white vinegar onto the moldy areas. Let it sit for an hour. Then, scrub with a brush. Rinse well. You can find more specific guidance on how to clean mold with vinegar.

For severe mold, a bleach solution is more powerful. Mix 1 cup of bleach with a gallon of water. Apply it to the moldy spots. Let it sit for 15 minutes. Scrub, then rinse very well. Always wear gloves and ensure good ventilation when using bleach. Make sure the can dries completely after treatment.

#### Removing Tough Stains

Food spills can leave stubborn stains. A paste made of baking soda and a little water works well. Apply the paste to the stain. Let it sit for 15-30 minutes. Scrub gently with a non-abrasive sponge. The baking soda provides a gentle abrasive action. This helps lift the stain.

For very tough, greasy stains, a degreasing dish soap can be effective. Apply it directly to the stain. Let it sit for a few minutes. Then, scrub and rinse. For extreme biological messes, such as maggots, a thorough cleaning is vital. You can find detailed instructions on how to clean maggots from trash can, which often involves strong cleaning and sanitizing steps. Always ensure surfaces are dry after cleaning to prevent future stains and odors.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid When Cleaning

Cleaning your Simplehuman trash can is important. But using the wrong methods can cause damage. Some cleaning products or tools are too harsh. They can scratch finishes or ruin sensors. Knowing what to avoid is as important as knowing what to do.

Avoid practices that might harm the bin's material. Also, steer clear of shortcuts that do not truly clean. These mistakes can reduce your trash can's lifespan. They can also make it look worse. Proper care helps your Simplehuman bin last for many years.

#### Harsh Abrasives

Never use abrasive cleaners or scrubbing pads. These include steel wool, scouring powders, or rough sponges. Simplehuman cans, especially stainless steel ones, scratch easily. Scratches ruin the sleek finish. They also create tiny grooves where dirt can hide. Always opt for soft cloths or non-abrasive sponges.

If a spot is stubborn, use a paste of baking soda and water. This offers gentle scrubbing power. Rub it lightly. Then rinse well. Harsh abrasives can also damage plastic liners. They can make them dull or weak over time. Stick to gentle methods for all parts of your bin.

#### Neglecting Drying

Many people clean their trash cans well but forget to dry them. Leaving your trash can damp is a big mistake. Moisture encourages mold and mildew growth. It also leads to rust on metal parts. Bad odors often start from damp conditions. Always dry your bin thoroughly after washing.

Use a clean, dry cloth to wipe all surfaces. Then, let the can air dry completely. Prop the lid open. Place the inner liner upside down. This allows air to circulate. Drying your trash can completely is a simple step. It prevents many problems. It keeps your bin fresh and clean longer.

### FAQ Section

**How often should I clean my Simplehuman trash can?**
Clean the exterior weekly with a quick wipe. Deep clean the interior and liner monthly. If you dispose of a lot of food waste, deep clean more often. Regular cleaning prevents odors and germ buildup. This keeps your home fresh.

**Can I use bleach on my Simplehuman stainless steel trash can?**
Use bleach with caution. Bleach can cause discoloration or damage to stainless steel if not used properly. For stainless steel exteriors, use mild soap and water or a stainless steel cleaner. For the plastic liner, a diluted bleach solution is okay for deep sanitizing, but rinse thoroughly.

**What causes bad odors in my trash can even after emptying?**
Lingering odors often come from absorbed smells in the plastic liner or the main can body. Small food particles or liquid spills can also hide in crevices. Deep cleaning with vinegar or baking soda helps. Ensuring the can is completely dry after washing prevents new odors.

**How do I remove fingerprints from my Simplehuman stainless steel trash can?**
Use a microfiber cloth with warm water and a drop of dish soap. Wipe in the direction of the grain of the steel. For stubborn marks, use a stainless steel cleaner. Some people use a small amount of olive oil on a cloth, then buff for a streak-free shine.

**Can I put my Simplehuman trash can liner in the dishwasher?**
Most Simplehuman trash can liners are too large for a standard dishwasher. Even if it fits, handwashing is usually better. Dishwashers might use very hot water or harsh detergents. These can warp plastic or dull finishes over time. Handwashing allows for more controlled cleaning.

**Are there natural alternatives for cleaning my trash can?**
Yes, white vinegar and baking soda are excellent natural cleaners. Vinegar disinfects and deodorizes. Baking soda absorbs odors and gently scrubs. You can use them separately or together for a powerful cleaning solution. Lemon juice can also help with odors and leave a fresh scent.
