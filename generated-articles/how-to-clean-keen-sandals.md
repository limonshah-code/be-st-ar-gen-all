## Keep Your Keen Sandals Clean and Fresh

Keen sandals are popular for adventure. They offer durability and comfort. Many people wear them for hiking, water sports, or daily activities. These sandals often get dirty from mud, sweat, and general use. This guide will show you how to clean Keen sandals properly. We will cover different methods to keep your footwear looking good and smelling fresh.

Cleaning your Keen sandals helps them last longer. It also stops bad odors from forming. I have used Keen sandals for years. I know how important it is to clean them often. This article provides simple steps. You will learn about materials, essential tools, and specific cleaning methods. Get ready to refresh your favorite adventure footwear!

### Takeaway

*   **Hand wash often:** This is the safest way to clean Keen sandals.
*   **Use mild soap:** Harsh chemicals can damage materials.
*   **Air dry completely:** Never use direct heat, which can warp your sandals.
*   **Target odors:** Use baking soda or specialized cleaners for smells.
*   **Protect materials:** Apply water repellent to fabric models.

### How do you clean Keen sandals?

You clean Keen sandals by hand washing them with mild soap and cold water. Scrub the footbed and straps gently. Rinse them thoroughly to remove all soap. Allow them to air dry completely away from direct heat or sunlight. This method protects the materials and keeps your sandals in good shape.

Some Keen sandals have leather or nubuck accents. These materials need special care.

Polyester webbing is strong and dries fast. It can hold onto dirt and sweat. Rubber parts are easy to wipe clean. The EVA footbed is comfortable but can absorb odors. Leather or nubuck needs gentle cleaning to prevent damage. You should never soak leather. Using the right cleaning method for each material type extends the life of your Keen sandals. This section explains how different materials behave. It helps you choose the best cleaning approach.

When you clean your Keen sandals, consider their specific components. For instance, the closed-toe design of many Keen models is famous. This design protects your feet, but it can also trap dirt inside. The footbed is a common place for odors to start. This happens because sweat and bacteria build up there. If your sandals have special waterproof treatments, harsh cleaners can strip them away. Always read any care tags on your specific Keen model. This ensures you use the correct method for the materials present. My own pair of Keens has a mix of webbing and rubber. I find that a soft brush works well on both.

### Step-by-Step Guide to Hand Washing Keen Sandals

Hand washing is the best method for most Keen sandals. It lets you control the process. This prevents damage to sensitive materials. You can focus on dirty areas specifically. This method is also gentle on colors and finishes. I always hand wash my Keen sandals. This keeps them looking new for longer.

#### Pre-Cleaning Steps

First, remove any loose dirt. Take your sandals outside. Bang them together gently to shake off mud and debris. You can also use a dry brush to scrub away dry dirt. Pay attention to the deep grooves in the sole. Small stones and grit often get stuck there. Use a pick or a stick to dislodge larger pieces. This initial step makes the main washing much easier. It also prevents dirt from scratching the sandal's surface during washing.

#### Washing the Exterior and Interior

Now, get your cleaning solution ready. Fill a sink or bucket with cool water. Add a small amount of mild soap. Mix it until some suds appear. Do not use too much soap. Too much soap leaves residue. Submerge your Keen sandals in the soapy water. Let them soak for a few minutes. This helps loosen dirt.

Next, start scrubbing. Use your soft-bristled brush. Gently scrub all surfaces of the sandal. Focus on the webbing straps. Dirt often hides in the woven fibers. Clean the rubber toe bumper and sides. Pay special attention to the footbed. This area accumulates sweat and grime. Scrub the textured surface thoroughly. For hard-to-reach spots, use an old toothbrush. You can find more tips on cleaning sandal footbeds here: [How To Clean Footbed Of Sandals](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-footbed-of-sandals).

#### Rinsing and Drying Properly

After scrubbing, rinse your sandals completely. Hold them under running cool water. Make sure all soap residue washes away. Soap left behind can attract more dirt. It can also irritate your skin. Keep rinsing until the water runs clear. The sandals should not feel slippery.

Once rinsed, shake off excess water. Do not wring or twist your sandals. This can damage their shape. Blot them with a clean towel. Press the towel gently into the straps and footbed. This removes a lot of moisture. Then, place your sandals in a well-ventilated area to air dry. Do not put them in direct sunlight or near a heat source. High heat can shrink or warp the materials. It can also crack rubber components. Air drying ensures your sandals retain their shape and durability. Drying can take several hours, or even a full day. Patience is key for proper drying.

### Tackling Stubborn Stains and Odors in Keen Sandals

Keen sandals are for outdoor use. They often pick up stubborn stains and develop odors. Dirt, grass, and even food spills can leave marks. Sweat and bacteria cause unpleasant smells. Dealing with these issues requires specific methods. This section provides tips for both stain removal and odor elimination. My sandals sometimes get muddy after a hike. I know how important it is to clean them well.

#### Stain Removal Techniques

For minor stains, the hand washing method works well. However, some stains need more attention. For mud or dirt stains, let them dry first. Then, brush off as much dry dirt as possible. Wetting fresh mud can push it deeper into the fabric. For grease stains, apply a small amount of a gentle degreaser, like dish soap, directly to the spot. Rub it in with your finger or a soft brush. Let it sit for a few minutes. Then, proceed with the general hand washing steps.

Grass stains can be tricky. You can try a mix of white vinegar and water. Dab it onto the stain with a cloth. Then, gently scrub. For general discoloration, a paste of baking soda and a little water can help. Apply the paste to the stained area. Let it dry completely. Then, brush it off. Always test any new cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first. This ensures it does not damage or discolor the sandal material.

#### Eliminating Lingering Odors

Keen sandals can become smelly over time. This happens due to bacteria growth. The moisture from sweat creates a good environment for bacteria. The footbed is a common culprit. To fight odors, start with a thorough cleaning as described above. If the smell persists, you need to target the bacteria.

One effective method is using baking soda. Sprinkle a generous amount of baking soda inside each sandal. Focus on the footbed. Let the baking soda sit overnight, or even for 24 hours. Baking soda absorbs odors naturally. After sitting, shake out the powder. You can also vacuum any remaining powder. Another option is a white vinegar soak. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a basin. Submerge only the fabric and rubber parts of your sandals for about 30 minutes. Do not soak leather parts. Vinegar kills bacteria and neutralizes odors. After soaking, rinse the sandals very well. Then, let them air dry completely. Damp sandals will just develop new odors.

### Machine Washing Keen Sandals: Is It Safe?

Many people wonder if they can machine wash Keen sandals. The short answer is yes, but with important caveats. Keen, as a brand, often states that most of their fabric and webbing sandals are machine washable. However, you must follow specific instructions. Machine washing can be harsh. It risks damaging the sandal's shape or materials. I prefer hand washing my own Keens. But machine washing can save time if done correctly.

If you choose to machine wash, use a gentle cycle. Select cold water only. Hot water can cause materials to shrink or warp. Always place your sandals in a mesh laundry bag. This protects them from hitting the inside of the machine. It also prevents straps from getting tangled. Do not wash your Keen sandals with other clothes. This prevents dye transfer and protects your garments.

Use a small amount of mild detergent. Avoid bleach or harsh chemicals. These can strip protective coatings or fade colors. After the wash cycle finishes, remove your sandals immediately. Do not put them in a dryer. High heat from a dryer is very damaging to Keen sandal materials. It can melt glues, crack rubber, and shrink straps. Always air dry your Keen sandals. Place them in a well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight. Ensure they are completely dry before storing them. This prevents mold and mildew growth. Machine washing is a convenience. However, it requires careful steps to protect your sandals.

### Special Care for Leather Keen Sandals

Some Keen sandal models feature leather or nubuck elements. These materials need different care than synthetic webbing. Leather is a natural material. It can dry out, crack, or stain easily if not treated right. Never machine wash leather Keen sandals. Soaking leather in water can cause irreversible damage. It can stiffen the leather and remove its natural oils. I have ruined leather shoes before by not caring for them properly.

For leather portions, use a damp cloth. Gently wipe away surface dirt. You can use a very small amount of mild leather cleaner. Apply it to a cloth first, not directly to the leather. Rub it into the leather in small circles. If your leather is stained, avoid harsh scrubbing. A specialized leather eraser can help with scuffs on nubuck. After cleaning, wipe away any excess cleaner with a clean, damp cloth.

It is important to condition leather after cleaning. Leather conditioners restore moisture. They keep the leather soft and flexible. Apply a small amount of leather conditioner following the product's instructions. Buff it gently with a soft cloth. This step prevents drying and cracking over time. For suede or nubuck parts, use a suede brush to restore the nap. Never use water directly on suede unless it's a specific suede cleaner for water-based stains. Proper leather care extends the life and appearance of your Keen sandals.

### Drying and Maintaining Your Keen Sandals for Longevity

Proper drying is as important as washing. It stops odors from forming again. It also keeps your Keen sandals in good condition. Incorrect drying can lead to damage. After cleaning, the way you store and protect your sandals also matters. These steps ensure your sandals last through many adventures.

#### Proper Drying Methods

After rinsing your Keen sandals, remove as much excess water as possible. Shake them vigorously. You can also press them with a clean, dry towel. This helps absorb a lot of moisture. Then, place your sandals in a spot with good air circulation. A shaded outdoor area is ideal. If drying indoors, choose a room with open windows or a fan. You can also place them upside down. This helps water drain from the footbed.

Never use a clothes dryer. Avoid placing sandals near direct heat. This includes radiators, heaters, or direct sunlight. High temperatures can cause the rubber soles to melt or warp. They can also shrink the webbing or crack leather. It might take 24 to 48 hours for sandals to dry completely. Ensure they are bone dry before wearing or storing them. Damp sandals become breeding grounds for mold and mildew. This leads to bad smells and material breakdown.

#### Storage Tips

When your Keen sandals are completely dry, store them properly. Keep them in a cool, dry place. Avoid storing them in damp basements or hot garages. These environments can encourage mildew growth. You can store them in their original box. Alternatively, place them in a breathable shoe bag. Do not store them in sealed plastic bags. This traps moisture and odors. Proper storage prevents dust buildup and maintains the sandal's shape. It also keeps them ready for your next outing.

#### Waterproofing and Protection

Many Keen sandals are water-friendly. However, their materials can benefit from additional protection. For webbing and synthetic materials, you can apply a fabric protector spray. These sprays create a barrier against water and stains. Choose a product suitable for outdoor gear. Follow the product instructions carefully. Reapply the spray periodically, especially after cleaning.

For leather parts, use a leather conditioner or a waterproofing spray designed for leather. This helps repel water and prevent stains. It also keeps the leather supple. Regular protection helps your Keen sandals withstand various weather conditions. It also makes future cleaning easier. By drying and protecting your sandals well, you extend their life significantly.

### FAQ Section

#### How often should I clean my Keen sandals?
Clean your Keen sandals whenever they look dirty or start to smell. If you use them often for outdoor activities, clean them regularly. This could be once a month or after every few major outings. Regular cleaning prevents dirt and odors from building up.

#### Can I use bleach on my Keen sandals?
No, do not use bleach on Keen sandals. Bleach can damage the materials. It can also cause colors to fade. Stick to mild soaps and gentle cleaning solutions. Harsh chemicals can weaken the fabric and rubber.

#### What if my Keen sandals still smell after cleaning?
If your Keen sandals still smell, try a baking soda soak overnight. Sprinkle baking soda generously inside. Alternatively, use a white vinegar and water solution (equal parts). Soak fabric and rubber parts. Rinse thoroughly and dry completely. Persistent odors usually mean bacteria still exists.

#### Can I put my Keen sandals in the washing machine?
You can machine wash some fabric Keen sandals. Use a gentle cycle with cold water. Place them in a mesh laundry bag. Never use hot water or a dryer. Always air dry your sandals completely afterward. Check the Keen website or care tag for your specific model first.

#### How do I remove black marks from my Keen sandal soles?
For black scuff marks on rubber soles, try rubbing alcohol on a cotton ball. Gently rub the mark until it lifts. A magic eraser can also work. Test a small, hidden area first to ensure it does not damage the material.

#### How long does it take for Keen sandals to dry?
Keen sandals typically take 24 to 48 hours to air dry completely. Drying time depends on humidity and air circulation. Ensure they are fully dry before storing to prevent mildew and odors. Avoid direct heat sources as they can damage the sandals.
