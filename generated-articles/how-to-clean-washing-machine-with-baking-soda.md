## How To Clean Washing Machine With Baking Soda

You might notice a strange smell coming from your washing machine. Perhaps your clothes do not smell as fresh as they should. These signs mean your washing machine needs a good cleaning. Learning how to clean washing machine with baking soda is a simple, effective, and natural solution. This guide walks you through cleaning your machine using a common household item. We will discuss why your machine gets dirty, what baking soda does, and step-by-step instructions for different machine types. You will also find tips for keeping your washer fresh.

### Takeaway

*   Regularly clean your washing machine to prevent odors and buildup.
*   Baking soda is a natural deodorizer and gentle abrasive for cleaning.
*   Clean both the inside and accessible parts like gaskets and dispensers.
*   Follow specific steps for top-load and front-load machines.
*   Maintain cleanliness with simple habits after each wash.

### How to Clean Your Washing Machine with Baking Soda

To clean your washing machine with baking soda, add one cup of baking soda directly to the drum. Run a hot water cycle without clothes. This simple process helps neutralize odors, break down residue, and refresh your appliance. For more stubborn areas, a paste of baking soda and water can scrub surfaces.

## Why Your Washing Machine Needs Cleaning

Your washing machine works hard. It cleans countless loads of laundry. Over time, dirt, detergent residue, and minerals build up inside the machine. This buildup is not always visible. It can lead to unpleasant odors.

The constant damp environment inside a washing machine also encourages mold and mildew growth. These growths thrive in dark, moist places. They cause that musty smell you sometimes notice. This smell can transfer to your clean clothes.

Buildup can also affect your machine's performance. It might make your washer less efficient. Your clothes might not get as clean as they should. Cleaning your machine ensures it runs well. It also keeps your laundry smelling fresh.

Regular cleaning prevents these issues. It extends the life of your appliance. A clean machine means cleaner clothes for you. Ignoring cleaning leads to bigger problems over time.

## The Power of Baking Soda for Appliance Cleaning

Baking soda is a versatile cleaning agent. It is a natural compound with many uses around the home. Its scientific name is sodium bicarbonate. People use it for baking, cleaning, and deodorizing.

Baking soda works wonders in a washing machine. It neutralizes odors. This happens because baking soda reacts with acidic and alkaline odor molecules. It brings them to a more neutral pH level. This process eliminates bad smells instead of just covering them up.

Baking soda also acts as a mild abrasive. It helps scrub away grime and residue. Yet, it is gentle enough not to scratch surfaces. This makes it safe for washing machine drums. It can loosen sticky detergent buildup and hard water deposits. This makes them easier to rinse away.

Using baking soda is also an eco-friendly choice. It is a non-toxic cleaner. It does not harm the environment. It is safe for septic systems. You avoid harsh chemicals by choosing baking soda. This is good for your home and the planet.

Many people combine baking soda with vinegar for a powerful cleaning duo. Vinegar is acidic, and baking soda is alkaline. Together, they create a fizzing reaction. This reaction helps lift dirt and grime. This combination is highly effective for deep cleaning.

### Baking Soda's Deodorizing Action

Baking soda is excellent at absorbing smells. It does not just mask odors. It removes them. Think of it like a sponge for bad smells. It pulls the odor molecules in.

In a washing machine, smells come from mold, mildew, and stale water. Laundry detergents also leave residue. This residue can trap odors. Baking soda tackles these sources directly.

When you run a cycle with baking soda, it circulates through the machine. It reaches all parts of the drum and hoses. It neutralizes the odors it encounters. This leaves your machine smelling clean and fresh.

This deodorizing power is why baking soda is popular. It is simple, effective, and safe. You will notice a big difference in how your machine smells. Your clothes will smell better too.

### Baking Soda's Gentle Abrasive Quality

Baking soda has a slightly gritty texture. This texture gives it abrasive properties. However, it is a very fine grit. It is much softer than common abrasive cleaners. This quality makes it safe for delicate surfaces.

In your washing machine, baking soda gently scrubs away buildup. It helps remove soap scum and mineral deposits. These deposits cling to the drum and agitator. They can make surfaces feel sticky.

The abrasive action helps loosen these stubborn residues. Once loosened, the water can wash them away. This leaves the inside of your machine smoother and cleaner. It helps improve water flow.

It is important to remember baking soda is not harsh. It will not scratch metal or plastic parts. This gentle action cleans without causing damage. It is a perfect choice for maintaining appliance surfaces.

## Step-by-Step Guide: How to Clean a Top-Load Washing Machine with Baking Soda

Top-load washing machines are common. They are easy to clean with baking soda. This method helps remove odors and buildup. Follow these steps for a fresh machine.

### Pre-Cleaning Steps

Before you start the main cleaning cycle, do a few things. First, make sure your washing machine is empty. Remove any clothes or items from the drum. Check pockets for forgotten items.

Next, wipe down the exterior of your machine. Use a damp cloth to clean the top and sides. This removes dust and spills. Pay attention to the control panel. This prepares the machine for the internal clean.

You can also wipe down the inside of the lid. Use a mild soap solution if needed. Rinse it well. A clean lid prevents dirt from falling back into the drum. This simple preparation helps achieve a better overall clean.

### Baking Soda Wash Cycle

Now, it is time to clean the inside of the machine. This step uses baking soda to deodorize and scrub. It is simple to do.

1.  **Add Baking Soda:** Pour one cup of baking soda directly into the washing machine drum. Do not add it to the detergent dispenser.
2.  **Select Hot Water:** Choose the hottest water setting on your washing machine. High temperatures help baking soda dissolve and work effectively.
3.  **Choose a Large Load Cycle:** Select the largest load size and the longest wash cycle. This ensures the baking soda solution circulates thoroughly.
4.  **Start the Cycle:** Begin the wash cycle. Let it run for a few minutes to mix the baking soda with water.
5.  **Pause and Soak (Optional):** If your machine allows, pause the cycle after a few minutes. Let the baking soda solution soak in the drum for at least 30 minutes to an hour. This helps loosen stubborn grime.
6.  **Complete the Cycle:** Restart the machine and let the cycle finish completely. The hot water and baking soda will work to clean and deodorize the drum.

This cycle targets the hidden buildup. It cleans the drum and water lines. Your machine will smell much better after this.

### Manual Cleaning of Components

After the baking soda cycle, clean accessible parts manually. These areas often harbor gunk and mildew. Cleaning them by hand ensures a thorough job.

1.  **Detergent and Fabric Softener Dispensers:** These can get gummy. Remove them if possible. Wash them in your sink with warm, soapy water. Use a small brush to scrub away residue. Rinse well and let them air dry before putting them back. If they are not removable, wipe them thoroughly with a damp cloth. You can use a toothbrush for tight spots.
2.  **Agitator (for top-loaders):** Wipe down the agitator or impeller. Use a damp cloth. You can also make a paste of baking soda and a little water. Apply the paste to any dirty spots. Let it sit for a few minutes. Then scrub with a brush or cloth and wipe clean.
3.  **Inside of the Drum Rim:** For top-loaders, lift the lid and look under the rim of the drum. This area can accumulate residue. Wipe it down with a damp cloth. If you see mildew, use a baking soda paste. Rub it on the affected area. Rinse thoroughly.
4.  **Lid and Hinges:** Do not forget the lid itself and its hinges. Dust and dirt can settle here. Wipe them clean with a damp cloth. This completes the cleaning of your top-load washing machine. Your machine is now ready for fresh laundry.

## Step-by-Step Guide: How to Clean a Front-Load Washing Machine with Baking Soda

Front-load washing machines are known for efficiency. However, their design can lead to specific cleaning challenges. The door gasket is a common spot for mold and odors. Learning how to clean a front-load washing machine with baking soda is essential. This guide shows you how to tackle these areas.

### Pre-Cleaning Steps

Just like with top-loaders, some initial steps are helpful. First, ensure the washing machine drum is empty. Look for any small items left behind. These could be coins or lint.

Next, give the exterior a quick wipe. Use a clean, damp cloth. Wipe the door, control panel, and sides. This removes surface dust and grime. It prepares the machine for its deep clean.

Also, examine the detergent dispenser drawer. Pull it out if possible. Check for old detergent or fabric softener residue. This area often gets sticky. You will clean it more thoroughly later.

### Baking Soda Wash Cycle

This cycle targets the main drum and internal plumbing. It uses hot water and baking soda to sanitize and deodorize. This step is crucial for overall cleanliness.

1.  **Add Baking Soda:** Add one cup of baking soda directly into the washing machine's detergent dispenser. If your dispenser has separate compartments, place it in the main wash compartment.
2.  **Select Hot Water Setting:** Choose the hottest water temperature available. High heat improves baking soda's effectiveness.
3.  **Choose a Heavy Duty or Tub Clean Cycle:** Select a heavy duty or a specific "Tub Clean" cycle if your machine has one. These cycles use more water and run longer. This ensures a thorough cleaning.
4.  **Start the Cycle:** Begin the cycle. Allow it to run fully. The baking soda will circulate, breaking down residue and neutralizing odors inside the machine.
5.  **Optional Pause and Soak:** Some people like to pause the cycle after water fills and baking soda dissolves. Let it soak for 30 minutes to an hour. This can help with very stubborn buildup. Then, restart the cycle.

This automated cycle handles the majority of the internal cleaning. You will notice a fresher smell from your machine afterward. For a general guide on keeping your washer spotless, consider reading [how to clean washing machine](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-washing-machine).

### Manual Cleaning of Components (Gasket, Dispenser)

Front-load washers have unique spots needing manual attention. The rubber gasket and detergent dispenser are prime examples. They often collect mold and residue.

#### Cleaning the Detergent Dispenser

The detergent dispenser can become a breeding ground for mold and soap scum. This is due to constant dampness. Cleaning it improves performance and prevents odors.

1.  **Remove the Drawer:** Most front-load dispensers pull out completely. Consult your machine's manual if unsure.
2.  **Wash Separately:** Take the drawer to your sink. Wash it with warm, soapy water. Use an old toothbrush or a small cleaning brush. Scrub all compartments and crevices. Pay attention to any mold or sticky residue.
3.  **Clean the Dispenser Housing:** While the drawer is out, look inside the dispenser housing on the machine. Use a damp cloth or sponge. Wipe away any residue. A spray bottle with a baking soda solution can help here. Spray, let sit, then wipe clean.
4.  **Rinse and Dry:** Rinse the dispenser drawer thoroughly. Make sure no soap residue remains. Let it air dry completely before putting it back. This prevents new mold growth.

#### Cleaning the Rubber Gasket/Door Seal

The rubber gasket around the door is notorious for trapping water, lint, and mold. This area is critical to clean regularly.

1.  **Examine the Gasket:** Pull back the folds of the rubber seal. You will likely find grime, lint, and dark spots of mold or mildew.
2.  **Prepare Cleaning Solution:** Make a paste of baking soda and a little water. You can also use a mixture of equal parts water and white vinegar in a spray bottle.
3.  **Apply and Scrub:** Apply the baking soda paste directly to moldy or dirty areas. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes. For a spray solution, spray the entire gasket liberally.
4.  **Wipe and Rinse:** Use a clean cloth or paper towel to wipe away the grime and paste. Pay close attention to all the folds. You might need to scrub with an old toothbrush for stubborn spots. After wiping, dampen a clean cloth with water and wipe the gasket thoroughly to remove all residue.
5.  **Dry:** Leave the washing machine door ajar after cleaning. This allows the gasket to air dry completely. This is vital to prevent immediate re-growth of mold.

Cleaning these parts manually completes the thorough cleaning process. Your front-load washing machine will now be fresh and clean inside and out. ## Addressing Common Issues: Mold, Mildew, and Hard Water Stains

Washing machines face specific challenges beyond general dirt. Mold, mildew, and hard water stains are common. Baking soda can help address these problems. Knowing how to tackle them keeps your machine in top condition.

### Tackling Mold and Mildew

Mold and mildew thrive in damp, dark environments. Your washing machine, especially the gasket and dispenser, provides perfect conditions. They cause unpleasant, musty odors. They can also transfer to your clothes.

1.  **Identify Affected Areas:** Look for black or gray spots. These often appear on the rubber gasket of front-load washers. They can also be in the detergent dispenser or around the drum rim.
2.  **Create a Baking Soda Paste:** Mix baking soda with a small amount of water. Make a thick paste. This paste concentrates the cleaning power.
3.  **Apply the Paste:** Apply the paste directly to the moldy areas. Use a cloth or an old toothbrush. Make sure the paste covers the mold completely.
4.  **Let it Sit:** Allow the paste to sit for at least 15-30 minutes. For severe mold, let it sit for an hour or even longer. This gives the baking soda time to break down the mold.
5.  **Scrub and Wipe:** Scrub the areas gently with a brush or cloth. The abrasive nature of baking soda helps lift the mold. Wipe away the residue with a clean, damp cloth.
6.  **Run a Rinse Cycle:** After manual scrubbing, run a short hot rinse cycle. This helps flush away any remaining mold spores or baking soda residue.

Regularly cleaning these areas prevents mold return. Always dry the gasket and leave the door ajar after each use.

### Removing Hard Water Deposits

Hard water contains minerals like calcium and magnesium. These minerals can leave behind white, chalky deposits. These deposits reduce your machine's efficiency. They also make surfaces look dirty.

1.  **Identify Deposits:** Look for white, crusty buildup on the inside of the drum. You might also see it around the detergent dispenser.
2.  **Baking Soda Paste Application:** Create a paste using baking soda and a small amount of water.
3.  **Apply and Soak:** Apply the paste directly to the hard water stains. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes. For very stubborn deposits, you can let it sit for a few hours.
4.  **Scrub Gently:** Use a non-abrasive scrubber or a stiff brush. Gently scrub the affected areas. The baking soda helps to break down the mineral bonds.
5.  **Wipe Clean:** Wipe the areas with a clean, damp cloth. You may need to repeat the process for very heavy deposits.
6.  **Run a Hot Cycle:** After manually cleaning, run a hot water cycle. This helps to rinse away any loosened deposits and baking soda residue.

For particularly tough hard water stains, especially combined with mold, a mixture of baking soda and vinegar can be more effective. The fizzing reaction helps lift and dissolve stubborn grime. Addressing these issues ensures your washing machine stays efficient and clean.

## Maintaining Your Washing Machine for Lasting Cleanliness

Cleaning your washing machine with baking soda is a great start. However, regular maintenance keeps it fresh. Simple habits can prevent future buildup and odors. These practices extend your machine's life.

### Regular Maintenance Schedule

Consistency is key for a clean washing machine. Set a schedule for cleaning. This prevents dirt and grime from accumulating.

*   **Monthly Deep Clean:** Aim to do a full baking soda cleaning cycle once a month. This includes the hot water cycle and manual wiping. For heavy use or hard water, consider doing this every two to three weeks.
*   **Weekly Wipe-Down:** Every week, take a few minutes to wipe down accessible areas. This includes the detergent dispenser, the drum rim, and the door gasket (for front-loaders). Use a damp cloth. This prevents mold and mildew from settling in.
*   **Immediate Spill Cleanup:** If any detergent or fabric softener spills, wipe them immediately. This prevents sticky residue from forming. These residues can trap dirt and grow mold.

Following this schedule keeps your machine consistently clean. It reduces the need for intense cleaning sessions. It also ensures your clothes always come out fresh.

### Best Practices for Laundry Day

Small changes to your laundry routine make a big difference. These practices minimize buildup and moisture. They help maintain your machine's cleanliness between deep cleans.

*   **Use Proper Detergent Amounts:** Do not use too much detergent. Excess detergent creates more suds and residue. This residue can stick to the machine's interior. Follow the detergent manufacturer's recommendations.
*   **Remove Clothes Promptly:** Take clothes out of the washer as soon as the cycle finishes. Leaving wet clothes inside creates a damp environment. This encourages mold and mildew growth.
*   **Leave the Door Ajar:** After each wash, leave the washing machine door open. For front-loaders, also pull out the detergent drawer slightly. This allows air to circulate inside. It helps the machine's interior dry out completely. This is the best way to prevent mold and musty odors.
*   **Clean Lint Filter (If Applicable):** Some washing machines have a lint filter that needs regular cleaning. Consult your manual to locate and clean it. A clogged filter can affect drainage and machine efficiency.
*   **Use High-Efficiency (HE) Detergent for HE Machines:** If you have an HE washer, use only HE detergent. These detergents are low-sudsing and designed for less water. Regular detergent creates too many suds in HE machines. This leaves residue and can damage the machine.
*   **Check Pockets:** Always check pockets before washing. Small items like coins, tissues, or keys can get stuck in the drain pump. This can cause blockages. If you ever have issues with your washing machine's drainage, you can also learn [how to clean drain in washing machine](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-drain-in-washing-machine).

By adopting these habits, you contribute to a cleaner, healthier washing machine. It will perform better and last longer. Your clothes will always come out smelling clean and fresh.

## FAQ Section

### How often should I clean my washing machine with baking soda?

You should clean your washing machine with baking soda monthly. If you do many loads or live in a hard water area, clean it every two to three weeks. Regular cleaning prevents bad odors and residue buildup. This keeps your machine efficient and your laundry fresh.

### Can baking soda damage my washing machine?

No, baking soda is safe for washing machines. It is a non-abrasive and non-corrosive substance. It does not harm metal or plastic parts. Baking soda is a gentle, natural cleaner. It helps remove grime and odors without causing any damage to your appliance.

### Should I use baking soda and vinegar together in my washing machine?

Yes, combining baking soda and vinegar is highly effective. You can run a baking soda cycle first, then a vinegar cycle. Or, add baking soda to the drum and vinegar to the dispenser during the same cycle. The fizzing reaction helps deep clean and deodorize. Just do not mix them directly outside the machine, as they neutralize each other quickly.

### What causes a washing machine to smell bad?

A washing machine smells bad due to moisture, detergent residue, and mold. Lint, dirt, and body oils also accumulate. These factors create a damp environment. This environment encourages bacteria and mildew growth. Regular cleaning with baking soda removes these odor-causing elements.

### Can I clean a brand-new washing machine with baking soda?

Yes, you can clean a new washing machine with baking soda. New machines might have manufacturing residues or a plastic smell. A baking soda cycle helps to rinse these out. It also deodorizes the machine before its first laundry load. This ensures your new clothes start fresh.

### What if my washing machine still smells after cleaning?

If your machine still smells, you might have severe mold or a clogged drain. Repeat the cleaning process, focusing on manual scrubbing of the gasket and dispenser. Consider using a stronger solution like baking soda and vinegar. Check the drain filter for blockages. If problems persist, consult your appliance manual or a professional.
