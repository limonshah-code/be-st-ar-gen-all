## Clean Track Spikes: A Complete Guide

Track spikes are important for runners. They give you grip and speed on the track. After hard workouts or races, your spikes get dirty. Mud, dirt, and sweat build up. Knowing how to clean track spikes properly keeps them working well. It also makes them last longer. This guide will show you how to clean your track spikes, step by step. I will cover everything from simple dirt removal to deep cleaning and proper storage.

#### Takeaway:
*   Always clean your track spikes after each use to remove dirt and mud.
*   Use simple tools like a stiff brush, old toothbrush, and mild soap for effective cleaning.
*   Proper drying is essential to prevent odor and material damage.
*   Store your spikes correctly to maintain their shape and extend their lifespan.

To clean track spikes, first remove all visible dirt and mud with a stiff brush or old toothbrush. Then, unscrew the spikes and clean the spike plate and shoe upper with mild soap and water. Rinse thoroughly, air dry completely, and store in a cool, dry place.

### Why Clean Your Track Spikes? Boost Performance and Extend Life

Cleaning your track spikes is more than just making them look nice. It has real benefits for your running. Dirt and mud can weigh down your shoes. This extra weight slows you down. Dirty spikes also lose their grip on the track. You need good grip to run fast and stay safe.

Dirty spikes can also cause problems for the shoe itself. Mud and moisture break down materials over time. They can make the shoe stiff or cause parts to tear. Regular cleaning prevents this wear and tear. It keeps the fabric, plastic, and metal parts in good shape. This helps your expensive track spikes last for many seasons. It means you spend less money buying new shoes often.

Proper care also helps prevent bad odors. Sweat and dirt mix, creating a smell. Cleaning removes the bacteria that cause this odor. Your feet and your gear will thank you. Cleaning is a simple habit that makes a big difference.

### Gather Your Cleaning Supplies: What You Need

Before you start to clean track spikes, gather your tools. Having everything ready makes the job easier. You probably have most of these items at home already. This is a list of what I use to get my spikes spotless.

First, you need a stiff brush. An old toothbrush also works great for small spots. A soft cloth or sponge is good for wiping surfaces. You will also need a bucket or a sink filled with water. A mild soap is important. Dish soap or a gentle laundry detergent works well. Do not use harsh chemicals.

You might want a spike wrench. This tool helps you remove the metal spikes. If your spikes are stuck, a wrench makes it easy. I also recommend a small bowl for mixing soap and water. Gloves can protect your hands from dirt. Finally, have old newspapers or towels ready for drying. These supplies ensure a smooth cleaning process.

*   Stiff brush (a cleaning brush or a dedicated shoe brush)
*   Old toothbrush
*   Soft cloth or sponge
*   Bucket or sink
*   Mild soap (dish soap, laundry detergent, or dedicated shoe cleaner)
*   Warm water
*   Spike wrench (optional, but helpful)
*   Small bowl
*   Gloves (optional)
*   Old newspapers or towels

Having these items ready helps you clean your track spikes effectively. It saves time and ensures a thorough clean.

### Step-by-Step: Removing Loose Dirt and Debris

The first step to clean track spikes is to remove loose dirt. This prevents mud from spreading when you use water. It makes the deep cleaning process much easier later. I always start by doing this outside or over a trash can.

First, bang the soles of your spikes together gently. This knocks off large clumps of mud. You will see a lot of dirt fall away. Next, use your stiff brush. Brush off any remaining dry dirt from the upper, sole, and spike plate. Get into the grooves of the sole. Pay attention to areas where dirt likes to hide.

Use an old toothbrush for tighter spots. The small bristles can reach into small gaps. Brush around the base of each spike. Also clean the area where the sole meets the fabric. This pre-cleaning gets rid of most surface grime. It prepares the shoes for a more thorough wash. This step is crucial for effective cleaning.

*   **Knock off large clumps:** Hold the shoes by the heels and gently hit the soles together. This removes most of the caked-on mud.
*   **Use a stiff brush:** Take a stiff-bristled brush and scrub the outsole, midsole, and upper to remove dry dirt. Work systematically across all surfaces.
*   **Target small areas with a toothbrush:** For stubborn dirt caught in crevices, around the spike plate, or in stitching, use an old toothbrush. Its small head reaches tiny spaces.
*   **Remove visible debris:** Pick out any grass, small stones, or leaves stuck in the sole patterns. You can use a stick or your fingers for this.

This initial dry cleaning makes the next steps much more effective. It stops you from turning mud into messy sludge. It is an important part of how to clean track spikes well.

### Deep Cleaning the Spikes and Plate: Restoring Grip

After removing loose dirt, it is time for a deep clean. This part focuses on the spike plate and the actual spikes. These parts are key for your grip. They often get the most mud. Getting them truly clean helps your performance.

First, use your spike wrench to remove all the metal spikes. Place them in a small bowl. This allows you to clean the plate thoroughly. It also lets you clean the spikes separately. If your spikes are stuck, try twisting them firmly. Some might need a little extra effort.

Mix warm water with a few drops of mild soap in a separate bowl. Dip your old toothbrush into this soapy water. Scrub the spike plate vigorously. Clean around each spike hole. Make sure to remove all dirt and grime. This restores the plate's surface. Next, scrub the individual metal spikes. Remove any dirt stuck in their threads. You can also place the removed spikes in a small mesh bag and wash them if they are very dirty.

Rinse the spike plate and the metal spikes under clear water. Make sure no soap residue remains. This prevents corrosion. Let them air dry completely before reattaching. You can learn more about general footwear cleaning by reading our guide on [how to clean shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-shoes). Proper cleaning of these parts maintains maximum grip and performance.

*   **Remove the spikes:** Use a spike wrench to unscrew all individual metal spikes from the shoe. Put them aside.
*   **Clean the spike plate:** Dip an old toothbrush in soapy water. Scrub the entire spike plate area, paying close attention to the spike wells. Remove all dirt and debris.
*   **Clean individual spikes:** Place the removed spikes in the soapy water. Use the toothbrush to scrub each spike. Make sure to clean the threads. This ensures easy re-insertion and prevents rust.
*   **Rinse thoroughly:** Rinse both the spike plate and the individual spikes under clean running water. Remove all soap residue.
*   **Dry completely:** Lay the spike plate and metal spikes on a towel or newspaper to air dry. Ensure they are completely dry before putting them back on.

This deep cleaning process ensures your spikes give you the best grip. It helps them perform at their peak. It is a vital step in how to clean track spikes effectively.

### Cleaning the Shoe Upper and Sole: Fabric and Rubber Care

Once the spike plate is clean, turn your attention to the rest of the shoe. The upper and the main sole also need care. These parts get dirty from track dust, sweat, and general use. Cleaning them makes your entire shoe look new again.

Mix fresh, warm water with a small amount of mild soap in a bucket. Dip a soft cloth or sponge into the soapy water. Gently wipe down the entire upper part of the shoe. This includes the mesh, synthetic leather, or fabric. Do not rub too hard, especially on delicate materials. Work in small sections to lift dirt.

Next, clean the rubber sole. Use the stiff brush or sponge with soapy water. Scrub the bottom and sides of the sole. Get into any remaining treads or patterns. This removes any leftover dirt and discolors the rubber. Rinse your cloth or sponge often to keep it clean.

After scrubbing, wipe down the entire shoe with a clean, damp cloth. Remove all soap residue. Make sure no soap is left on the fabric or rubber. Soap left behind can attract more dirt later. This step ensures a clean finish for your track spikes. This comprehensive approach helps maintain the shoe's overall look and feel.

*   **Prepare soapy water:** Mix a small amount of mild soap with warm water in a clean bucket.
*   **Clean the upper:** Dip a soft cloth or sponge into the soapy water. Gently wipe down the entire fabric or synthetic upper of the shoe. Do not saturate the material.
*   **Address stubborn stains:** For tougher marks on the upper, use the old toothbrush with soapy water. Scrub lightly in a circular motion.
*   **Clean the sole:** Use the stiff brush or sponge with soapy water to scrub the rubber outsole. Pay attention to the treads and edges.
*   **Rinse thoroughly:** Take a clean, damp cloth and wipe down the entire shoe to remove all soap residue. Make sure no suds remain on any part of the shoe.

Cleaning the upper and sole is important. It completes the look of your clean track spikes. It also removes sweat and dirt that can damage materials.

### Drying Your Track Spikes: Essential for Longevity and Freshness

Drying your track spikes properly is just as important as cleaning them. If you do not dry them well, they can develop bad smells. Mold and mildew can also grow. This damages the materials and makes your shoes unhealthy to wear. Never put your track spikes in a dryer. High heat can melt or shrink the materials.

After rinsing, gently shake excess water from the shoes. Remove the insoles if they are removable. You can wash and dry the insoles separately. Stuff the inside of each shoe with old newspapers or paper towels. This helps absorb moisture quickly. It also helps the shoe keep its shape while drying. Change the paper every few hours if the shoes are very wet.

Place your spikes in a well-ventilated area. A warm, dry room is best. Avoid direct sunlight, as it can fade colors or damage materials. Let them air dry completely. This can take 24 to 48 hours. Ensure they are bone dry before storing them away. This step ensures your track spikes stay fresh and last longer. Proper drying is a key part of how to clean track spikes effectively.

*   **Remove insoles:** Take out the insoles from your track spikes. Wash them separately if needed and air dry them flat.
*   **Stuff with newspaper:** Crumple up old newspapers or plain paper towels and stuff them inside the shoes. This absorbs moisture and helps the shoes keep their shape. Change the paper every few hours.
*   **Air dry:** Place the spikes in a well-ventilated area. Avoid direct sunlight or heat sources like radiators. A fan can help speed up the drying process.
*   **Allow ample time:** Let the shoes air dry completely. This can take up to 24-48 hours depending on how wet they are and the humidity. Ensure they are thoroughly dry before moving to the next step.

Drying properly prevents a lot of common shoe problems. It keeps your track spikes ready for your next run.

### Proper Storage for Track Spikes: Maintaining Shape and Spikes

Once your track spikes are completely dry, proper storage is crucial. How you store them impacts their shape and the condition of the spikes. Storing them well helps them last longer. It also ensures they are ready for your next race or practice.

Before storing, re-insert the clean, dry metal spikes into the spike plate. Screw them in firmly with your spike wrench. Make sure they are snug but do not overtighten. This prevents them from falling out. It also keeps the spike holes clean.

Store your track spikes in a cool, dry place. A shoe bag or a breathable mesh bag is ideal. This protects them from dust and dirt. It also allows air to circulate, preventing moisture buildup. Avoid storing them in damp basements or hot attics. Extreme temperatures can damage materials. Do not store them squashed in a backpack or tightly packed in a box. This can deform the shoe's shape.

Keeping your spikes in their original box, if you still have it, is also a good option. Make sure the box is clean and dry. Proper storage makes a difference. It ensures your hard work cleaning them pays off. It helps your track spikes stay in top condition. This is the final step in how to clean track spikes and maintain them.

*   **Re-insert spikes:** Once both the shoes and the metal spikes are completely dry, carefully screw the spikes back into the spike plate using your spike wrench. Ensure they are secure.
*   **Use a breathable bag:** Store your track spikes in a breathable shoe bag or a mesh bag. This protects them from dust while allowing air circulation.
*   **Choose a cool, dry location:** Place the bag in a closet or on a shelf in a cool, dry room. Avoid damp areas like basements or places with direct sunlight.
*   **Avoid crushing:** Do not stack heavy items on top of your spikes. This can deform their shape. Store them in a way that maintains their natural form.

Good storage protects your investment. It means your track spikes will be ready to perform when you need them.

### Ongoing Maintenance Tips for Spike Longevity

Cleaning your track spikes after each use is a great start. But a few ongoing maintenance tips can help them last even longer. These small actions prevent major problems down the line. They save you time and money. I always recommend these practices to my fellow runners.

First, check your spikes regularly. After every few runs, inspect the metal spikes. Look for any that are loose, bent, or worn down. Replace worn-out spikes promptly. Running with dull or damaged spikes reduces grip. It also puts more strain on your shoe. Keep a small supply of replacement spikes handy.

Keep your spike wrench with your shoes. This makes it easy to tighten or change spikes quickly. If you run in very muddy conditions, consider a quick rinse. Even if you do not do a full clean, rinsing off heavy mud prevents it from drying and caking on. Let them air dry right after.

Store your spikes away from direct sunlight and heat. These elements degrade shoe materials over time. Just like you might clean your patio door tracks to keep them smooth, giving your track spikes regular attention keeps them performing. Regular, small cleaning efforts extend the life of your running gear. This ongoing care makes sure your track spikes perform their best for many seasons.

*   **Inspect spikes regularly:** After each use, check the metal spikes for wear, bending, or looseness. Replace any damaged or excessively worn spikes immediately.
*   **Keep a spike wrench handy:** This tool allows for quick tightening or replacement of spikes as needed, ensuring optimal grip.
*   **Brush off loose dirt promptly:** If a full wash is not possible, at least brush off caked-on mud and dirt immediately after your run. This prevents it from hardening and becoming difficult to remove.
*   **Avoid extreme temperatures:** Do not leave your track spikes in a hot car or direct sunlight for extended periods. This can damage the materials. Store them in a cool, dry place.
*   **Air out after every use:** Even if you do not wash them, always remove them from your bag and let them air out to dry sweat and moisture. This prevents odors and mildew.

These simple habits will keep your track spikes in excellent condition. They ensure you get the most out of your running investment.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Cleaning Track Spikes

### How often should I clean my track spikes?
You should clean your track spikes after every use, especially if they are muddy or wet. For lighter dirt, brushing off debris after each run is often enough. A deep clean with soap and water is good every few uses or when they look very dirty. Regular cleaning prevents damage and keeps them ready for races.

### Can I put track spikes in the washing machine?
No, do not put track spikes in the washing machine. The harsh cycles and detergents can damage the delicate materials, glues, and spike plates. It can also cause the shoes to lose their shape or shrink. Hand washing is always the safest and most effective method for cleaning track spikes.

### What should I do if my track spikes smell bad?
If your track spikes smell bad, it is often from sweat and bacteria. First, give them a thorough clean, including the insoles. After washing, ensure they dry completely in a well-ventilated area. You can also sprinkle baking soda inside the shoes and let it sit overnight to absorb odors.

### How do I prevent rust on metal spikes?
To prevent rust on metal spikes, ensure they are completely dry after cleaning before re-inserting them into the shoes. You can also apply a very thin layer of petroleum jelly or a silicone-based lubricant to the spike threads before screwing them in. Store your spikes in a dry environment.

### Can I use household cleaners like bleach?
No, avoid using harsh household cleaners like bleach or strong chemical detergents on your track spikes. These products can damage the synthetic materials, discolor fabrics, and weaken glues. Stick to mild soap, like dish soap or a gentle laundry detergent, mixed with water.

### What if my spikes are stuck and won't unscrew?
If your spikes are stuck, try applying a penetrating oil or a few drops of WD-40 to the base of the spike where it meets the shoe. Let it sit for 15-30 minutes. Then, use your spike wrench to try unscrewing them again. Sometimes, a gentle tap with a hammer on the wrench handle can help break the seal.
