### Miele Dishwasher Not Draining Water: Solutions

One of the most frequent Miele dishwasher problems is water not draining. You open the door after a cycle. A pool of dirty water sits at the bottom. This is a clear sign of a drainage issue. Several things can cause this. I will explain the steps to fix it.

First, check the filter system. Miele dishwashers have excellent filters. They can get clogged with food particles. Remove the filter. Rinse it under running water. Use a brush to clean away any debris. A clean filter allows proper drainage. This is often the quickest fix. For more detailed instructions on cleaning your filter, you can refer to our guide on [how to clean filter in dishwasher](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-filter-in-dishwasher).

Next, inspect the drain hose. The drain hose connects the dishwasher to your sink's plumbing. It can become kinked or blocked. Pull the dishwasher out carefully if possible. Check the hose for bends. Straighten any kinks you find. Food debris or grease can also build up inside the hose. Disconnect the hose from the sink drain. Look inside with a flashlight. Clear any blockages. For severe clogs, you might need to use a drain snake. Learn more about maintaining this part in our article on [how to clean dishwasher drain](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-drain).

The air gap is another part to check. If your dishwasher has one, it is usually on the countertop. This small cylinder prevents dirty water from flowing back into the dishwasher. It can get clogged. Remove its cap. Clear any debris inside. This blockage can prevent proper draining. Clearing it helps your Miele dishwasher drain correctly. Lastly, the drain pump might be faulty. If the above steps do not work, the pump could be the problem. A humming sound without water movement suggests pump trouble. You might need professional help for a pump replacement. However, sometimes the pump impeller is just jammed. For insights on cleaning this component, check our guide on [how to clean dishwasher pump](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-pump).

### Dishes Not Clean: Improving Miele Dishwasher Performance

Are your dishes coming out dirty? This is frustrating. A Miele dishwasher should deliver sparkling results. If not, several factors might be at play. We will go through the steps to get your dishes clean again. This is a common Miele dishwasher troubleshooting area.

First, check the spray arms. Miele dishwashers have rotating spray arms. They jet water onto your dishes. Food particles can clog the small holes. Remove the spray arms. Use a toothpick or small wire to clear the holes. Ensure they spin freely after reinstallation. Blocked spray arms cannot properly clean dishes. This simple check often resolves poor cleaning.

Next, consider your detergent. Are you using the right type and amount? Miele recommends specific detergents. Using too little detergent leaves dishes dirty. Too much can cause excess suds and residue. Make sure the detergent dispenser opens fully. Sometimes, it gets blocked by a tall dish. Also, check your rinse aid dispenser. Low rinse aid levels lead to water spots and streaks. Refill it as needed.

Water temperature also matters. Your dishwasher needs hot water. The water heater setting should be at least 120°F (49°C). Run your kitchen faucet for a minute before starting the dishwasher. This ensures hot water fills the machine quickly. Cold water does not clean grease and food effectively. Proper water temperature is crucial for good results.

Overloading is another common mistake. Do not cram too many dishes into the racks. Water and detergent need to reach all surfaces. Refer to your Miele manual for proper loading techniques. Also, scrape large food particles off dishes before loading. This prevents filters from clogging too quickly. Sometimes, a general cleaning of the dishwasher helps. You can use a specific dishwasher cleaner or try natural methods. For example, cleaning your dishwasher with vinegar can break down grease and mineral deposits. Find out more at [how to clean dishwasher with vinegar](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-dishwasher-with-vinegar).

### Miele Dishwasher Error Codes: Deciphering and Fixing

Miele dishwashers use error codes. These codes appear on the display. They tell you what is wrong. Understanding them helps you fix the issue faster. Each code points to a specific problem area. I will list common codes and their meanings.

For example, a common code is "FXX." This usually indicates a water intake or drainage issue. An "F11" might mean a drain pump fault. An "F12" or "F13" suggests a water intake problem. If you see "F12," check the water supply. Make sure the water tap is fully open. Check the water inlet hose for kinks. Also, inspect the water inlet filter for clogs. A dirty filter can restrict water flow.

Other codes might relate to temperature sensors or heating elements. For instance, "F68" often indicates a problem with the circulation pump or heater. These issues are more complex. They might require a technician. Always consult your Miele user manual first. The manual provides a complete list of error codes. It also gives specific troubleshooting steps for each code.

After checking the relevant components, try resetting the dishwasher. Turn off the power at the circuit breaker for a few minutes. Then turn it back on. This can clear temporary glitches. If the code persists, or if it is a complex issue like a pump or heater failure, contacting Miele service is best. Do not try to repair electrical or internal component issues if you are not experienced.

### Miele Dishwasher Not Starting or Power Issues

Your Miele dishwasher might not start. This is frustrating. It can indicate a simple power problem or something more complex. I will guide you through checks for these Miele dishwasher problems.

First, check the power supply. Is the dishwasher plugged in? This seems obvious, but it is easy to overlook. Is the circuit breaker tripped? Go to your home's electrical panel. Look for the breaker labeled for the kitchen or dishwasher. If it is tripped, flip it off, then back on. Sometimes, a temporary power surge can trip a breaker.

Next, check the dishwasher door. Miele dishwashers have safety features. The door must latch completely for the cycle to start. Push the door firmly closed. You should hear a click. If the latch is broken, the dishwasher will not start. Inspect the latch mechanism for damage. If it is visibly broken, you might need a replacement part.

The control panel itself can have issues. Is the display working? Are buttons responding? Try pressing the power button firmly. If the panel seems unresponsive, it might need a reset. Unplug the dishwasher from the wall outlet. Wait for about 5 minutes. Then plug it back in. This hard reset can clear electronic glitches. If the display is completely blank, there might be a wiring issue or a main control board failure. These require professional service. Always ensure the machine receives power before looking for deeper issues.

### Addressing Noisy Miele Dishwasher Operation

Miele dishwashers are known for quiet operation. If yours starts making unusual noises, something is wrong. Common Miele dishwasher problems include grinding, humming, or knocking sounds. I will help you identify the cause.

A grinding noise often means something is stuck in the drain pump. Small items like broken glass or food debris can get into the pump impeller. Unplug the dishwasher first for safety. Remove the lower spray arm and filter. You might see the pump impeller below. Use pliers or gloved hands to carefully remove any foreign objects. Be cautious of sharp items. Clearing the impeller often stops grinding noises.

A humming sound, especially without water movement, suggests a drain pump motor issue. The motor might be trying to run but cannot circulate water. This could be due to a blockage (as mentioned above). Or, the pump motor itself might be failing. If clearing blockages does not work, the pump might need replacement. A qualified technician should handle this.

Knocking or thumping noises usually come from the spray arms. This happens when dishes block the spray arm's rotation. Taller items or utensils can hit the arm as it spins. Rearrange your dishes to ensure clear paths for the spray arms. Make sure no items hang below the racks. This simple adjustment often stops the knocking sound. It ensures effective water distribution. Check that the spray arms are properly seated on their mounts. A loose spray arm can also cause knocking.

Another source of noise can be the water inlet valve. A vibrating or buzzing noise during the water fill cycle can indicate a faulty inlet valve. If the noise is consistently present during water intake, the valve might need replacement. This is usually a job for an appliance repair person. Regularly inspecting the inside of your dishwasher for loose items also helps prevent noise.

### Miele Dishwasher Leaking Water: Causes and Cures

A leaking dishwasher is a serious Miele dishwasher problem. Water on your floor can damage cabinetry and flooring. You need to find the source quickly. I will explain common reasons for leaks and how to fix them.

First, check the door seal (gasket). The rubber seal around the dishwasher door can crack or become brittle over time. Food debris can also get stuck in it. Inspect the seal closely. Look for tears, cracks, or hardened spots. Clean it thoroughly with a damp cloth. If the seal is damaged, it needs replacement. You can usually order a new one from Miele. Replacing it is often a simple process.

Next, examine the hose connections. Water hoses connect to the water inlet valve and the drain. These connections can loosen over time. Check both the supply hose and the drain hose at the back of the dishwasher. Also, check where the drain hose connects to your sink's plumbing. Tighten any loose connections. Look for signs of corrosion or drips around the fittings. A small leak at a connection point can quickly become a big problem.

Overfilling is another possibility. This usually happens if the water inlet valve is faulty or if the water level sensor malfunctions. If the dishwasher fills with too much water, it can overflow. A faulty inlet valve might not shut off the water supply completely. A professional can diagnose and replace a faulty water inlet valve or water level sensor. If you suspect overfilling, watch the dishwasher during its fill cycle.

Lastly, cracks in the tub or pump housing can cause leaks. These are less common but more serious. If you have checked everything else and water still leaks, there might be a structural issue. Look inside the dishwasher tub for any visible cracks. Inspect the area under the dishwasher for water coming from components like the pump or motor housing. These types of leaks often require a qualified technician for repair or even appliance replacement. Always unplug the dishwasher before attempting any leak diagnosis.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: Why does my Miele dishwasher have a lingering smell?**
A: A lingering smell often comes from food particles trapped in the filter or drain. Regularly clean your dishwasher filter. You can also run a hot cycle with a cup of white vinegar on the top rack. This helps remove grease buildup and odors. Ensure the drain hose is clear as well.

**Q2: My Miele dishwasher doesn't dry dishes well. What should I check?**
A: Poor drying can result from low rinse aid levels. Check and refill your rinse aid dispenser. Also, ensure the hot water supply is sufficient; higher water temperature aids in drying. Overloading the dishwasher can also prevent proper air circulation, leading to wet dishes.

**Q3: Why is my Miele dishwasher making a rattling noise?**
A: Rattling usually indicates dishes are hitting the spray arms or each other. Rearrange your dishes, ensuring adequate space for water circulation and spray arm rotation. Check that smaller items are secured. Sometimes, a loose utensil in the bottom can also cause rattling.

**Q4: How do I reset my Miele dishwasher?**
A: To reset your Miele dishwasher, first try holding down the "Start/Stop" button for a few seconds. If this does not work, you can perform a hard reset. Unplug the dishwasher from its power source or turn off the circuit breaker for 5-10 minutes. Then restore power.

**Q5: What does the "Water Intake/Drainage" error mean on my Miele?**
A: This error means the dishwasher is not getting enough water or cannot drain it. Check the water tap is fully open. Inspect the water inlet hose for kinks. Clean the filter and check the drain hose for blockages. Clear any debris from the air gap.

**Q6: Can I use regular dish soap in my Miele dishwasher?**
A: No, you must use automatic dishwasher detergent. Regular dish soap produces too many suds. This can cause leaks and damage your dishwasher. Always use detergents specifically designed for automatic dishwashers to protect your appliance and ensure proper cleaning.
