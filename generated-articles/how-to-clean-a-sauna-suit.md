## How To Clean A Sauna Suit: Your Essential Guide

Sweat suits, often called sauna suits, help you increase body temperature during workouts. They are popular for boosting sweat production. However, all that sweat means your suit collects a lot of moisture, salt, and grime. Proper care is vital. Knowing **how to clean a sauna suit** ensures it remains hygienic and lasts longer.

Ignoring cleaning can lead to bad odors and fabric damage. Your suit can become a breeding ground for bacteria. This guide will show you simple, effective ways to keep your sauna suit fresh. We will cover hand washing, spot cleaning, and proper drying techniques. Follow these steps to maintain your gear effectively.

### Takeaway

*   **Hand Wash Often:** Use mild soap and cool water for daily cleaning.
*   **Spot Clean Quickly:** Address small stains and dirt right away.
*   **Air Dry Fully:** Always hang your suit to air dry completely, away from direct heat.
*   **Avoid Machines/Harsh Chemicals:** Protect suit material from damage.

Cleaning a sauna suit involves simple hand washing with mild soap and cool water immediately after each use. You must rinse it thoroughly and then air dry it completely, turned inside out, to prevent odor and material breakdown. Avoid machine washing or harsh detergents.

### Understanding Your Sauna Suit Material

Sauna suits are often made from specific materials. These materials help trap heat and promote sweating. Common types include PVC, nylon, neoprene, and vinyl. Each material needs careful handling. Knowing your suit's fabric helps you clean it properly.

PVC and vinyl suits are lightweight and affordable. They are not breathable. Neoprene suits are thicker and more durable. They offer good insulation. Nylon suits are often layered with a special coating. This coating creates the sauna effect. Different materials react differently to water and cleaners.

Always check the care label on your specific sauna suit. The manufacturer's instructions are always the best guide. Some suits may have special coatings or seams. Incorrect cleaning can damage these features. You want to extend the life of your suit. Treating it right helps you do this.

Many suits are not machine wash friendly. High heat from a dryer can melt or shrink certain materials. Strong detergents can break down the fabric's integrity. Always choose mild cleaning agents. Avoid bleach or fabric softeners. They harm the material and reduce the suit's effectiveness.

### Why Regular Sauna Suit Cleaning Is Essential

Regular cleaning of your sauna suit is not just about smell. It is about hygiene and durability. When you sweat, your body releases salts, oils, and bacteria. These substances transfer onto your suit. Leaving them there can cause several problems.

First, persistent odors become an issue. The bacteria thrive in warm, damp environments. They break down sweat components. This process creates unpleasant smells. A dirty suit can smell bad even when dry. Nobody wants to wear stinky workout gear.

Second, material degradation can occur. The salts and oils from sweat are corrosive. They can break down the suit's fabric over time. This leads to cracks, tears, and a loss of the heat-trapping ability. Regular cleaning removes these harmful residues. This protects the material. Proper cleaning can make your suit last longer.

Third, skin irritation and infections are possible. Bacteria left on the suit can transfer to your skin. This may cause rashes, acne, or fungal infections. Maintaining a clean suit protects your skin. It ensures a healthy workout experience. Cleanliness promotes better health.

Finally, a clean suit simply feels better to wear. It contributes to a more positive workout experience. You can focus on your fitness goals, not on a dirty, smelly suit. Think of it like any other piece of workout attire. Just as you would clean your gym shorts, you must clean your sauna suit. ### The Basic Steps: Hand Washing Your Sauna Suit

Hand washing is the safest and most effective method for cleaning most sauna suits. It prevents damage from washing machines. It also ensures a thorough clean. I always hand wash my suit right after each use. This prevents odors from setting in.

Here is a simple step-by-step guide:

1.  **Rinse Immediately:** As soon as you finish your workout, turn your sauna suit inside out. Rinse it thoroughly under cool or lukewarm water. This removes most of the sweat and loose dirt. Do this in a shower or a large sink. This initial rinse is very important.

2.  **Prepare Cleaning Solution:** Fill a basin or tub with cool or lukewarm water. Add a small amount of mild liquid soap. You can use baby shampoo, a gentle dish soap, or a mild detergent. Avoid harsh chemicals or laundry detergents with strong enzymes. These can damage the suit's material. Mix the soap into the water until it creates a light suds.

3.  **Submerge and Swish:** Place your sauna suit into the soapy water. Gently swish it around. You want the soapy water to reach all parts of the suit. Pay extra attention to areas that collect more sweat. These areas include the armpits and crotch. Do not scrub harshly or wring the suit. Rough handling can stretch or tear the fabric.

4.  **Gentle Scrub (If Needed):** If there are visible spots or dirt, use a soft cloth or sponge. Gently wipe the affected areas. Again, avoid abrasive materials or brushes. They can scratch or damage the suit's surface. Most dirt should come off easily with gentle rubbing.

5.  **Rinse Thoroughly:** Drain the soapy water. Refill the basin with clean, cool water. Rinse the suit repeatedly. Keep rinsing until all soap residue is gone. Squeeze the suit gently to remove water. Do not twist or wring it out. Any leftover soap can attract dirt or irritate your skin.

6.  **Prepare for Drying:** After rinsing, gently press out excess water. Lay the suit flat on a clean, dry towel. Roll the towel and suit together. Press down to absorb more water. This step helps speed up drying. It also protects the suit's shape. You can also hang the suit on a plastic hanger. Avoid wire hangers as they can leave marks.

### Spot Cleaning Your Sauna Suit for Minor Messes

Sometimes, your sauna suit might only have a small dirt spot or a light stain. For these minor issues, spot cleaning is a quick and effective solution. It saves you from doing a full hand wash. Spot cleaning is good for maintaining the suit between deep cleans.

First, identify the stained area. Do not let the stain sit for too long. Fresh stains are always easier to remove. I always keep a small spray bottle with a mild cleaning solution ready. This helps me tackle spots right away.

Hereâ€™s how to spot clean your sauna suit:

1.  **Gather Supplies:** You will need a clean, soft cloth or sponge. You also need a small bowl of cool water and a very mild soap. A small spray bottle with a diluted soap solution works well too. Make sure your soap is gentle and colorless. Colored soaps can leave new stains.

2.  **Test in an Inconspicuous Area:** Before applying any cleaner to the stain, test it. Choose a small, hidden part of the suit. Apply a tiny amount of your cleaning solution. Wait a few minutes. Check for any discoloration or damage. This step prevents ruining your suit. It is a good practice for any new cleaning product.

3.  **Apply Solution to Cloth:** Do not apply the soap directly onto the suit. Instead, dampen your clean cloth with cool water. Put a tiny drop of mild soap onto the damp cloth. Work the soap into the cloth to create a light foam. You want very little soap.

4.  **Blot the Stain:** Gently blot the stained area with the soapy cloth. Start from the outside of the stain and work your way inward. This prevents spreading the stain. Do not rub vigorously. Rubbing can push the stain deeper into the fabric. It can also damage the suit's surface.

5.  **Rinse and Blot Dry:** Once the stain is gone, dampen a fresh, clean cloth with plain cool water. Use this cloth to blot the area again. This removes any soap residue. Continue blotting until no soap remains. Finally, use a dry part of the cloth to blot the area dry. You want to remove as much moisture as possible.

Spot cleaning is excellent for quick fixes. This method helps keep your suit looking neat between full washes. Always remember to air dry the spot-cleaned area completely before storing or wearing the suit again.

### Machine Washing Considerations for Sauna Suits

Most manufacturers do not recommend machine washing sauna suits. The harsh agitation and high temperatures of a washing machine can damage the material. It can cause tears, cracks, or a breakdown of the heat-trapping properties. If your sauna suit is made of neoprene, it might be more durable. However, caution is still advised.

Some specific types of modern sauna suits might be machine washable. Always check the care label first. If the label explicitly states "machine wash," follow those instructions carefully. If it does not, assume hand washing is the safest option. For my suits, I have never risked machine washing.

If your suit is indeed machine washable, here are key considerations:

*   **Use Cold Water:** Always select a cold water cycle. Hot water can damage the material, especially PVC and vinyl. Cold water is gentler on synthetic fabrics. It helps preserve the suit's shape and integrity.
*   **Gentle Cycle:** Choose the most gentle cycle available on your washing machine. A "delicate" or "hand wash" cycle is best. This minimizes agitation. Less agitation means less stress on the seams and fabric.
*   **Mild Detergent:** Use a very small amount of mild, liquid detergent. Avoid powders, bleach, or fabric softeners. Powders may not dissolve completely. They can leave residue. Bleach and fabric softeners can degrade the material.
*   **Wash Separately:** Wash your sauna suit by itself. Do not wash it with other clothes. Zippers or buttons from other garments can snag or tear the suit. This also prevents color transfer, though sauna suits rarely bleed color.
*   **Use a Mesh Laundry Bag:** Place your sauna suit inside a large mesh laundry bag. This provides an extra layer of protection. It reduces friction against the washing machine drum. It also keeps the suit from stretching.

Even with these precautions, machine washing carries a risk. Hand washing offers the best control and protection for your sauna suit. If you opt for machine washing, remember to never put your sauna suit in a dryer. Always air dry it completely.

### Drying and Storing Your Sauna Suit Properly

Proper drying and storage are as important as cleaning. Incorrect drying methods can damage your sauna suit. They can also lead to mildew and lingering odors. I have learned that patience in drying saves me from buying new suits.

Here are the best practices for drying and storing your sauna suit:

1.  **Never Use a Machine Dryer:** This is the most crucial rule. The high heat from a tumble dryer can melt, shrink, or crack the material of your sauna suit. It can permanently damage the suit's heat-trapping properties. Even low heat is too risky for most synthetic materials. Your suit will not last if you put it in a dryer.

2.  **Air Dry Inside Out:** After washing, gently squeeze out excess water. Do not wring or twist the suit. Turn the suit inside out. Hang it on a sturdy plastic hanger. Plastic hangers are better than wire hangers. Wire hangers can crease the material. Hanging it inside out helps the interior dry faster. This prevents moisture buildup inside.

3.  **Choose a Well-Ventilated Area:** Hang your suit in a spot with good airflow. A bathroom with an open window, a laundry room, or an outdoor clothesline are good choices. Avoid direct sunlight. Intense UV rays can degrade certain materials over time. They can also cause fading. A shady, airy spot is ideal.

4.  **Ensure Complete Dryness:** Allow plenty of time for your suit to dry completely. This might take several hours or even overnight. It depends on the humidity and temperature. Touch the suit to check for any damp spots. Pay attention to seams and inner linings. Even a slightly damp suit can develop mildew and odors in storage.

5.  **Store Properly:** Once your sauna suit is bone dry, store it in a cool, dry place. Fold it neatly or hang it in your closet. Avoid stuffing it into a gym bag or a drawer while still damp. This traps moisture and promotes bacterial growth. Keep it away from extreme temperatures. Do not store it under heavy items. This prevents creases and damage.

Following these drying and storage tips will significantly extend the life of your sauna suit. It will also ensure it stays fresh and ready for your next workout. These simple steps make a big difference.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid When Cleaning Sauna Suits

Cleaning a sauna suit might seem straightforward, but common mistakes can shorten its life. Avoiding these errors ensures your suit remains effective and hygienic. I have made some of these mistakes myself, learning valuable lessons.

Here are the key mistakes to avoid:

1.  **Using Hot Water:** Hot water is a major enemy of most sauna suit materials. It can cause PVC, vinyl, and even some neoprene suits to melt, warp, or crack. Always use cool or lukewarm water for washing and rinsing. This preserves the material's integrity and shape.

2.  **Machine Washing (Unless Specified):** As discussed, most sauna suits are not designed for washing machines. The vigorous agitation can tear seams, stretch fabrics, or damage protective coatings. Stick to hand washing unless the care label clearly says otherwise. When in doubt, hand wash.

3.  **Using Harsh Detergents or Bleach:** Regular laundry detergents often contain strong chemicals, enzymes, or brighteners. These can break down the synthetic materials of your suit. Bleach is even more corrosive. It will weaken the fabric and ruin the suit. Always opt for a mild, gentle soap, like baby shampoo.

4.  **Using Fabric Softeners:** Fabric softeners leave a residue that can clog the pores of the suit's material. This can reduce its breathability (if applicable) and its heat-trapping efficiency. They also may make it harder to rinse out odors completely. Skip the fabric softener entirely.

5.  **Wringing or Twisting:** After washing, it is tempting to wring out excess water forcefully. Do not do this. Wringing can stretch the fabric. It can also damage the seams. Gently press the water out, or roll the suit in a towel. This preserves the suit's shape.

6.  **Using a Tumble Dryer:** This is perhaps the most damaging mistake. The high heat of a dryer will almost certainly ruin your sauna suit. It can cause irreversible damage like melting, shrinking, or making the material brittle. Always air dry your suit.

7.  **Storing When Damp:** Putting a damp sauna suit into a gym bag or closet creates a perfect environment for mildew and bacteria. This leads to persistent, foul odors that are hard to remove. Always ensure your suit is completely dry before storing it. This also applies to general clothing care, as learning [how to wash a suit](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-wash-a-suit) for everyday wear emphasizes proper drying.

By avoiding these common pitfalls, you will significantly extend the lifespan of your sauna suit. You will keep it clean, fresh, and ready for your next intense workout.

### Dealing with Odor: Advanced Cleaning Tips

Even with regular cleaning, some sauna suits can develop stubborn odors. This often happens if the suit was left damp for too long. Bacteria thrive in these conditions. Removing set-in odors requires a bit more effort.

Here are some advanced tips for tackling persistent sauna suit smells:

1.  **Baking Soda Soak:** Baking soda is a natural odor absorber.
    *   Fill a basin or tub with cool water.
    *   Add half a cup to a full cup of baking soda. Stir to dissolve.
    *   Submerge your sauna suit completely in the baking soda solution.
    *   Let it soak for at least 30 minutes, or even a few hours for strong odors.
    *   After soaking, remove the suit and rinse it thoroughly with cool, clean water.
    *   Air dry completely.

2.  **Vinegar Rinse:** White vinegar is another excellent natural deodorizer and mild disinfectant.
    *   After your regular hand wash, prepare a rinse solution.
    *   Mix one part white vinegar with two parts cool water in a basin.
    *   Submerge your clean, rinsed suit in this vinegar solution for 15-20 minutes.
    *   Remove the suit and rinse it again very thoroughly with plain cool water. You want to remove all vinegar smell.
    *   Air dry completely. The vinegar smell will dissipate as it dries.

3.  **Enzymatic Cleaner (Use with Caution):** Some sports gear cleaners contain enzymes. These enzymes break down odor-causing bacteria.
    *   Choose an enzymatic cleaner specifically designed for athletic wear.
    *   Always dilute it significantly more than recommended for regular laundry.
    *   Test it on an inconspicuous area of your suit first.
    *   Follow the product's instructions for soaking or spot treating.
    *   Rinse extremely well afterward. These cleaners can be strong, so use sparingly.

4.  **Sunlight (Indirect Exposure):** While direct sunlight is harsh, indirect sunlight or a well-lit, airy outdoor spot can help. UV rays have some sanitizing properties.
    *   Hang your suit outside on a cloudy but breezy day.
    *   Ensure it is in the shade or dappled sunlight, not direct, intense sun.
    *   Allow the fresh air to circulate through the suit.
    *   Do this only after the suit is completely clean and rinsed.

The best defense against odor is immediate and consistent cleaning. Do not let sweat dry into the fabric. If odors persist even after these steps, it might indicate permanent damage to the material's pores. In such cases, replacing the suit might be the only option. However, these methods usually work well for most odor issues. Knowing how to [spot clean a suit](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-spot-clean-a-suit) with these odor-fighting agents can also target specific stinky areas.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: How often should I clean my sauna suit?**
You should clean your sauna suit after every single use. Sweat, oils, and bacteria accumulate quickly. Immediate cleaning prevents odors from setting in. It also stops material degradation. This regular care extends the suit's life and keeps it hygienic for your next workout session.

**Q2: Can I put my sauna suit in the washing machine?**
No, generally you should not put your sauna suit in a washing machine. The harsh agitation and high temperatures can damage the material. This includes PVC, vinyl, and even neoprene. Always check the care label first. If it does not explicitly say "machine wash," hand washing is the safest method.

**Q3: What type of soap is best for cleaning a sauna suit?**
The best type of soap is a mild, gentle liquid soap. Baby shampoo, a gentle dish soap, or a specialty sports apparel wash work well. Avoid harsh detergents, bleach, or fabric softeners. These can degrade the suit's material and leave residues that reduce its effectiveness.

**Q4: How do I remove stubborn odors from my sauna suit?**
For stubborn odors, try soaking your suit in a solution of cool water and baking soda for 30 minutes to a few hours. Alternatively, after washing, rinse the suit in a solution of one part white vinegar to two parts cool water. Always rinse thoroughly with plain water afterward and air dry completely.

**Q5: Can I dry my sauna suit in a dryer?**
Absolutely not. You should never put your sauna suit in a machine dryer. The high heat will melt, shrink, or crack the material. It will permanently damage the suit's heat-trapping properties. Always air dry your sauna suit by hanging it in a well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight.

**Q6: What is the best way to store a sauna suit?**
After ensuring your sauna suit is completely dry, store it in a cool, dry place. You can hang it on a sturdy plastic hanger or fold it neatly. Avoid stuffing it into a gym bag or a confined space while damp. Proper storage prevents mildew and helps maintain the suit's shape and material integrity.
