## Build Your Own Air Purifier: A Home DIY Guide

Are you looking for an effective and affordable way to improve the air you breathe at home? Learning how to make an air purifier at home offers a practical solution. Many commercial air purifiers can be expensive. However, you can build a simple yet effective air cleaning device using common household items. This article guides you through the process. We will cover the materials you need, step-by-step instructions, and tips for maintaining your homemade air purifier.

**Takeaway**
*   Create an affordable air purifier using a box fan and MERV-rated filters.
*   Improve indoor air quality by reducing dust, pollen, and pet dander.
*   Follow simple steps to assemble and maintain your DIY air cleaner.

**Clear, Concise Answer:**
You can make an air purifier at home by combining a standard box fan with high-efficiency air filters, such as MERV-13 or HEPA filters. This setup effectively draws air through the filters, capturing airborne particles and improving your indoor air quality with minimal cost.

### Essential Materials for Your Homemade Air Purifier

Building your own air purifier requires only a few key items. These materials are easy to find. You can purchase them at hardware stores or online. The main components are a box fan and air filters. The type of filter you choose is important. It directly affects the purifier's ability to capture particles. Gathering your materials first makes the assembly process smoother.

Here are the essential materials you need:
*   **Box Fan:** A standard 20-inch box fan works best. Choose one with multiple speed settings. A fan that moves a good volume of air is ideal. The 20-inch size perfectly matches common filter sizes. This ensures a tight seal.
*   **MERV-13 or HEPA Filters:** You will need four filters. Each filter should be 20x20 inches. MERV-13 filters are good for general use. They capture small particles. HEPA filters offer even better filtration. They trap very fine particles like pet dander and smoke. Consider the MERV rating carefully; higher ratings mean better filtration but can restrict airflow slightly.
