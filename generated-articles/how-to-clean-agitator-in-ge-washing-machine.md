## How To Clean Your GE Washing Machine's Agitator

Have you noticed your laundry not smelling as fresh as it should? Perhaps your GE washing machine has a musty odor. The agitator inside your top-loader GE washer often harbors dirt, detergent residue, and even mold. Learning how to clean the agitator in your GE washing machine is important for both fresh clothes and a long-lasting appliance.

This article shows you the clear steps to clean your GE washing machine's agitator. We will discuss why it needs cleaning, what supplies you need, and the simple process. You will learn how to remove, clean, and reinstall the agitator. We will also share tips to keep your washer smelling clean always. Let's make your GE washer work its best again.

### Takeaway

*   Regularly clean your GE washing machine agitator to remove residue and prevent odors.
*   Gather basic cleaning supplies like white vinegar, baking soda, and brushes.
*   Always disconnect power before working on your washing machine for safety.

### Main Query Answer

To clean the agitator in your GE washing machine, first disconnect the power. Remove the agitator by unscrewing its cap and bolt. Use a brush with a vinegar and baking soda solution to scrub away buildup. Rinse, dry, and then reinstall the agitator.

This gunk can become a breeding ground for mold and mildew. Mold creates that unpleasant, musty smell you sometimes notice on "clean" laundry. It can also transfer dirt back onto your clothes. A dirty agitator affects how well your washer cleans your clothes. It can also cause problems with how the agitator moves. This means your machine may not clean as well as it should.

### Gathering Your Cleaning Supplies and Tools

Before you start cleaning your GE washing machine agitator, gather all your supplies. Having everything ready makes the cleaning process smooth. You likely have most of these items already in your home. We will focus on simple, effective, and natural cleaning solutions. This helps protect your appliance and your family.

First, you will need some white vinegar. Vinegar is a natural disinfectant and powerful degreaser. It cuts through soap scum and mineral deposits well. Next, grab some baking soda. Baking soda is a gentle abrasive and a great odor absorber. It helps scrub away grime without scratching surfaces. Together, vinegar and baking soda create a powerful cleaning team.

You also need a few cleaning tools. A stiff-bristled brush is essential for scrubbing. An old toothbrush can reach small cracks and crevices on the agitator. You will also want a clean cloth or sponge for wiping. A bucket or large bowl is good for soaking smaller agitator parts. Finally, you might need a screwdriver, often a flat-head or Phillips, to remove the agitator cap and bolt. Some GE models have a specific agitator bolt wrench, but a regular socket wrench may work. Always check your GE washer's manual first. Having the right tools saves you time and frustration. It helps you do the job correctly. Knowing how to clean an agitator in any washing machine helps. ### Safety Steps Before You Start Cleaning Your GE Washer

Safety is the most important step before you start any appliance maintenance. Working with water and electricity can be dangerous if you are not careful. Always make sure your GE washing machine is completely safe before touching any internal parts. Do not rush this part of the process. Taking a few moments for safety prevents accidents.

Your first and most important step is to disconnect the power. Go to the wall outlet where your washing machine is plugged in. Simply pull the power cord out from the socket. Do not just turn off the machine at the control panel. This does not fully stop the electrical current. Unplugging it completely removes any risk of electric shock. Make sure the cord is out of the way so no one plugs it back in by mistake.

Next, turn off the water supply to the washing machine. Look behind your washer for two water hoses. These hoses connect to faucets on your wall. Each faucet usually has a knob or lever. Turn both knobs until the water flow stops completely. This prevents water from unexpectedly flowing into the machine while you work. It also helps avoid any accidental leaks. A clear workspace is also good. Remove any laundry from the drum. Make sure the area around the washer is dry and well-lit. Following these safety steps protects you and your home. They ensure a safe cleaning experience for your GE washer's agitator. Always prioritize safety first when doing appliance maintenance.

### Removing the Agitator from Your GE Washing Machine

Removing the agitator is a key step to clean it well. Most GE top-loader washing machines have a similar agitator removal process. It is a simple task once you know where to look. Take your time and follow the steps carefully. You will not need a lot of force to remove it.

First, open the lid of your GE washing machine. Look at the very top of the agitator. You should see a cap. This cap often hides a bolt that holds the agitator in place. Some caps just snap off. Others may require a small flat-head screwdriver to gently pry them open. Insert the screwdriver into a small slot or seam and lift the cap up. Be gentle to avoid breaking any plastic parts. Once the cap is off, you will see a bolt underneath.

This bolt holds the agitator to the transmission shaft. You will need a socket wrench or a screwdriver to loosen this bolt. The size of the socket depends on your specific GE model. It is usually a large bolt. Turn the bolt counter-clockwise to loosen it. Keep turning until it is completely unscrewed. Place the bolt and cap in a safe place so you do not lose them. After removing the bolt, grasp the agitator firmly with both hands. Pull it straight up and out of the washer drum. It might be a little stiff if it has not been removed before. Wiggle it slightly if needed, but avoid tilting it. Pulling it straight up helps prevent damage to the shaft. Now the agitator is free, ready for a deep clean. If you are ever unsure about removing parts, consult your GE washer's user manual. It provides specific instructions for your model. Knowing how to clean your GE washing machine is simple once you master these steps.

### Deep Cleaning Your GE Agitator

Now that you have removed the agitator, it is time for the deep clean. You will be surprised at how much grime can hide on this part. This cleaning step is important to remove all the buildup that causes odors and reduces washing efficiency. We will use our gathered supplies to make it shine again.

Place the agitator in a large utility sink, bathtub, or a large basin. Start by rinsing it with warm water. This removes loose dirt and lint. Next, make a cleaning solution. In a bucket, mix equal parts white vinegar and warm water. For tough stains or mold, you can also sprinkle baking soda directly onto the agitator. Focus on the base and the inner parts. These areas often collect the most residue. Many people forget about the bottom sections.

Use your stiff-bristled brush to scrub the entire surface of the agitator. Pay special attention to the vanes, the fins that move the clothes. Scrub inside any openings and underneath any removable parts. If your agitator has a fabric softener dispenser or other components, clean those separately. An old toothbrush works well for getting into tight spots. For stubborn mold or scrud, which is a waxy buildup, you can let the agitator soak in the vinegar solution for 30 minutes. This helps loosen the grime. After scrubbing, rinse the agitator thoroughly with clean water. Make sure all the cleaning solution and loosened dirt are gone. You want it sparkling clean. ### Cleaning the Agitator Housing and Washer Drum

With the agitator removed, you have a clear view of the washer drum and the agitator housing. This is the area where the agitator sits. This space also collects a lot of dirt, mold, and residue. Cleaning these parts is just as important as cleaning the agitator itself. It ensures a truly clean washing environment.

Start by looking into the empty drum. You will likely see scum lines or residue on the inner walls. The agitator housing, which is the center column, can also be very dirty. You might even spot mold growth in hidden areas. This is a common issue, especially if you live in a humid area or use too much detergent. Mold can quickly spread in damp, dark places. For general cleaning of the drum, you can spray a vinegar and water solution onto the surfaces. You can also use a mixture of baking soda and a little water to form a paste. Apply this paste to the dirty areas.

Use your stiff brush or a sponge to scrub the drum interior. Pay attention to the bottom of the tub and around the agitator shaft. For any mold, a solution of white vinegar is often effective. You can also use a small amount of bleach if needed, but always ventilate the area well. Remember to rinse thoroughly if you use bleach. You can learn more about how to clean mold from your washing machine [here](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-mold-from-washing-machine). After scrubbing, wipe down all surfaces with a clean, damp cloth. Make sure no residue is left. A clean drum works better and smells fresher. You should also wipe the outside of the machine. This step makes the whole washer feel new again.

### Reinstalling the Agitator and Final Steps

Once your GE agitator and the washer drum are sparkling clean, it is time to put everything back together. Reinstalling the agitator is simple. It follows the reverse steps of removal. Make sure all parts are dry before you put them back. This helps prevent new mold growth.

First, carefully lower the clean agitator back into the washer drum. Line up the splines (grooves) on the bottom of the agitator with the shaft in the center of the washer tub. You might need to gently wiggle the agitator until it slides down onto the shaft. It should sit flush with the bottom of the washer drum. Do not force it down. If it does not go in easily, check for any obstruction or misalignment. Once the agitator is in place, reinsert the bolt into the top.

Use your socket wrench or screwdriver to tighten the bolt. Turn it clockwise until it is snug. Do not overtighten, as this could strip the bolt or damage the agitator. Just make sure it is firm and secure. Finally, snap or push the agitator cap back into place on top of the bolt. Ensure it clicks or sits firmly. Once the agitator is reinstalled, turn on the water supply faucets again. Then, plug your GE washing machine back into the electrical outlet. Your machine is now ready to use. It is a good idea to run a clean cycle without clothes after putting it back together. This rinses out any remaining cleaning solution. It also ensures everything works correctly. ### Maintaining a Clean GE Agitator: Tips for Longevity

Cleaning your GE washing machine agitator is not a one-time event. Regular maintenance helps keep your machine performing its best. It also prevents dirt and odors from building up again. A few simple habits can extend the time between deep cleans. They also ensure your clothes always come out fresh.

First, always use the right amount of detergent. Too much detergent creates excess suds. These suds leave behind residue that sticks to the agitator and drum. Check your detergent instructions for the correct dosage. Also, consider using high-efficiency (HE) detergent if your GE washer is an HE model. These detergents are low-sudsing and designed for HE machines. You can also vary your detergent type. Sometimes, switching brands helps.

Second, run a monthly cleaning cycle. Many GE washing machines have a "Clean Washer" cycle. If yours does not, simply run a hot water cycle with white vinegar or a washing machine cleaner. Add two cups of white vinegar to the detergent dispenser or directly into the tub. This helps flush out residue and kill mold spores. You can also use baking soda and vinegar together. This method is effective for maintaining freshness. You can find more details on how to clean a washing machine with baking soda and vinegar [here](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-washing-machine-with-baking-soda-and-vinegar). After each wash, leave the washing machine lid open for a while. This allows air to circulate inside the drum. It helps dry out moisture. This step prevents mold and mildew from growing. Following these simple tips keeps your GE agitator and entire washing machine clean and fresh. It also helps your appliance last longer.

### Addressing Common Agitator Issues and Advanced Cleaning

Sometimes, cleaning the agitator might reveal other issues. Or, you might face challenges during the cleaning process. Knowing how to handle these common problems can save you time and frustration. We will cover a few scenarios beyond the basic cleaning steps.

**Stuck Agitator Bolt:** If the bolt holding your agitator is very stiff, it might be corroded. Try applying a small amount of penetrating oil to the bolt. Let it sit for 15-30 minutes. This can help loosen rust or old gunk. Use the correct size socket wrench for better leverage. Avoid using too much force. Forcing it can strip the bolt head.

**Agitator Won't Come Off:** After removing the bolt, the agitator might still be stuck. Mineral deposits or soap scum can "glue" it to the shaft. Try pouring warm water mixed with white vinegar around the base of the agitator. Let it soak for 10-15 minutes. Then, try pulling it up again, wiggling gently. Sometimes, two people pulling can help.

**Persistent Odors After Cleaning:** If odors remain after cleaning the agitator and drum, the source might be elsewhere. Check the dispenser drawers. These can also build up mold and residue. Also, inspect the drain hose for clogs or buildup. A clogged hose can trap dirty water and cause smells. Consider cleaning your washing machine's drain hose if the smell persists. Cleaning the entire washing machine drum is also a good idea. You can find more tips on how to clean the washing machine drum [here](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-washing-machine-drum).

**When to Call a Professional:** If you encounter serious issues, like a damaged agitator or a bolt that simply won't budge, it is best to call a GE appliance technician. Do not risk damaging your machine further. Professional help ensures the issue is fixed correctly. Sometimes, replacing a part is necessary. These advanced tips help you tackle more stubborn cleaning challenges. They also guide you on when to seek expert help.

### FAQ Section

**How often should I clean my GE washing machine agitator?**
You should aim to deep clean your GE washing machine agitator every 3 to 6 months. This frequency depends on how often you use your washer and your water hardness. Regular maintenance washes, like a monthly cycle with vinegar, can extend the time between full agitator removals. Look for signs like odors or visible residue to know when it is time.

**What causes my GE agitator to get dirty and smelly?**
Dirt, lint, hard water minerals, and leftover detergent are the main causes. These residues build up over time. They create a damp environment inside the agitator and drum. This humid space is perfect for mold and mildew to grow. Using too much detergent or fabric softener also makes the problem worse.

**Can I use bleach to clean my GE agitator?**
Yes, you can use a diluted bleach solution to clean your GE agitator for tough mold. Mix one part bleach with one part water. Apply it to moldy areas. Always ensure good ventilation when using bleach. Rinse the agitator very well afterward to remove all bleach residue. This prevents damage to clothes in future washes.

**Do all GE washing machines have agitators?**
No, not all GE washing machines have agitators. Many newer GE top-loader models are "high-efficiency" (HE) washers that do not have a traditional agitator. Instead, they use an impeller at the bottom of the tub. If your GE washer does not have a central post, it is an impeller model. The cleaning process for these machines is different. Learn more about cleaning a GE top-loader without an agitator [here](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-ge-washing-machine-top-loader-no-agitator).

**What if the agitator bolt is stripped?**
If the agitator bolt is stripped, you will not be able to loosen or tighten it with a wrench. You might need to use specialized tools, like a bolt extractor, to remove it. If you are not comfortable doing this, it is best to contact a professional appliance repair technician. They have the right tools and expertise.

**Is it safe to run my washing machine with a dirty agitator?**
While running your washing machine with a dirty agitator is possible, it is not ideal. A dirty agitator can transfer odors and residue onto your "clean" clothes. It can also affect the washing machine's efficiency. Persistent mold and grime can also cause damage to parts over time. Cleaning it prevents these issues.
