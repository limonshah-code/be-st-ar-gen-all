## How To Clean APL Shoes: A Complete Guide

Your APL shoes are more than just footwear. They are a statement of performance and style. Keeping them clean helps them look great and last longer. Knowing how to clean APL shoes properly is important. These shoes often use advanced materials. These materials need specific cleaning methods. This guide will help you understand the cleaning process. We will cover tools, techniques, and drying steps. You will learn how to care for your APL shoes.

Keeping your APL sneakers pristine can feel like a challenge. Their unique fabrics and sleek designs require special attention. I often hear people ask about the best ways to keep these high-tech shoes looking new. This article explains how to tackle dirt and grime safely. We will look at cleaning specific parts of your APL shoes. We will also discuss how to protect them for future wear.

### Takeaway

*   Gather essential cleaning tools like mild soap and soft brushes before you start.
*   Clean APL shoe uppers with gentle hand washing techniques.
*   Address midsoles and outsoles with targeted scrubbing to remove grime.
*   Refresh shoe interiors to eliminate odors and dirt.
*   Always air dry APL shoes away from direct heat or sunlight.
*   Practice regular preventative care to keep your APLs clean longer.

You can clean APL shoes effectively by hand. Use a mild soap, warm water, and a soft brush. Focus on one section at a time. This gentle approach protects the unique materials. Always air dry your APL shoes completely. This prevents damage and helps them keep their shape.

### Understanding APL Shoe Materials: Why Specialized Care Matters

APL (Athletic Propulsion Labs) shoes use special materials. These materials make the shoes light and strong. They offer comfort and performance. Common materials include TechLoom knit, mesh, and various synthetic overlays. Each material reacts differently to water and cleaning products. We must understand these differences. This helps us clean the shoes without causing damage.

TechLoom knit is a popular APL material. It is lightweight and breathable. It requires gentle cleaning. Harsh brushes or strong chemicals can damage the knit. Other APL shoes feature synthetic leather or rubber accents. These parts may need different cleaning methods. For instance, rubber parts can withstand more scrubbing. Leather parts need specific leather cleaners. Knowing your shoe's specific build is the first step.

The unique structure of APL shoes also plays a role. They often have seamless designs. This means less stitching where dirt can hide. However, it also means delicate cleaning is necessary. You want to preserve the shoe's integrity. Using the wrong products can shrink fabrics. It can also cause colors to fade. Some materials might absorb water differently. This can affect drying times.

It is wise to treat APL shoes with care. They are an investment. Proper cleaning extends their life. It maintains their look and performance. We do not want to risk damaging them. This understanding forms the foundation for successful cleaning. [Cleaning shoes correctly](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-shoes) prevents wear. It helps your shoes stay in top condition.

### Gathering Your Cleaning Supplies: What You Need

Before you start cleaning your APL shoes, gather your tools. Having everything ready makes the process smooth. You do not need many special items. Most of these things are already in your home. I find that preparation saves time and hassle. This step is crucial for an effective cleaning session.

First, you need a mild cleaning solution. Dish soap is a common choice. Mix a small amount with warm water. You can also use a dedicated shoe cleaner. Avoid harsh detergents or bleach. These can damage shoe materials. I always use a gentle, pH-neutral soap. This keeps the colors safe.

Next, get a few soft brushes. An old toothbrush works well for small areas. A soft-bristle brush is good for the upper parts. For soles, a stiffer brush can remove tough dirt. You will also need a few clean microfiber cloths. These are great for wiping and drying. I keep a separate cloth just for shoes.

A small bowl of water is essential. This helps you rinse brushes and cloths. Paper towels are also useful. You can use them to stuff shoes for drying. This helps them keep their shape. Some people like shoe trees for this purpose. These simple items will help you clean your APL shoes effectively.

### Step-by-Step Guide to Cleaning APL Uppers (TechLoom/Knit)

Cleaning the upper part of your APL shoes needs a gentle touch. Many APL shoes feature TechLoom or other knit materials. These fabrics can be delicate. I always start with a dry brush. This removes loose dirt first. This step prevents rubbing dirt into the fabric.

Begin by taking out the laces and insoles. Wash these separately. You can machine wash laces in a mesh bag. I often hand wash insoles. Then, use your soft-bristle brush. Gently brush away any loose dirt or debris from the upper. Work slowly and carefully. Make sure you get all the surface dirt off.

Next, prepare your cleaning solution. Mix a small amount of mild soap with warm water in your bowl. Dip your soft brush into the solution. Do not make the brush too wet. Excess water can soak into the shoe. Lightly scrub the upper in small sections. Use circular motions. Apply very little pressure. The goal is to lift dirt, not push it in.

For stubborn spots, use an old toothbrush. Dip it in the solution. Gently work on the stain. Be extra careful with colors. Some dyes might bleed if scrubbed too hard. After scrubbing, wipe the area with a clean, damp microfiber cloth. Make sure to remove all soap residue. Repeat these steps until the upper looks clean. This method is similar to [how to clean canvas shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-canvas-shoes). It protects the material.

### Cleaning APL Midsoles and Outsoles: Restoring Grip and Shine

The midsoles and outsoles of your APL shoes get the most direct contact with the ground. They collect a lot of dirt, mud, and scuff marks. Cleaning these parts brings back the fresh look of your sneakers. I find that this step makes a huge difference in the overall appearance. It is also important for maintaining good grip.

For midsoles, which are often white or light-colored, scuffs and dirt are very visible. Dip your stiffer brush into the mild soap solution. Scrub the midsole vigorously. White midsoles may need more effort. Focus on any dark marks. You can also use a magic eraser for tough scuffs. However, use it gently. It can be abrasive.

The outsole, or bottom of the shoe, collects pebbles and grime. Use your stiffer brush to clean the treads. Dig deep into the grooves. Remove any trapped debris. A toothpick can help dislodge small stones. Rinse your brush often in clean water. This prevents spreading dirt around. Make sure the outsole is free of all dirt.

After scrubbing, wipe the midsoles and outsoles with a clean, damp microfiber cloth. Remove all soap residue. Pay attention to the edges where the sole meets the upper. This area often collects dirt. A clean sole looks sharp. It also provides better traction during your activities. [Cleaning white shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-white-shoes) applies well to APL midsoles.

### Tackling APL Insides: Odor and Grime Removal

The inside of your APL shoes can become a source of odor and bacteria. Sweat and dead skin cells build up over time. Cleaning the interior is just as important as cleaning the exterior. It helps maintain hygiene and comfort. I always make sure to address this part of the shoe. It ensures a truly fresh pair.

Start by removing the insoles. Most APL insoles are removable. You can hand wash them separately. Use the mild soap solution and a soft brush. Gently scrub both sides of the insole. Pay attention to the arch area. Rinse the insoles thoroughly under cool water. Squeeze out excess water. Set them aside to air dry completely. Never put them in a dryer.

For the shoe lining, use a damp microfiber cloth. Dip the cloth into the soap solution. Wring out most of the water. You want the cloth to be damp, not soaking wet. Wipe down the entire interior lining. Pay extra attention to the heel and toe areas. These spots tend to accumulate more grime. If there are tough spots, a toothbrush can help. Gently scrub.

To fight odors, a baking soda sprinkle can help. After cleaning, let the inside air dry a bit. Then, sprinkle a small amount of baking soda inside the shoes. Let it sit overnight. The baking soda absorbs smells. Shake out the powder the next day. This process helps your shoes feel and smell fresh. ### Drying Your APL Shoes Safely: Avoiding Damage

Proper drying is a critical step in cleaning your APL shoes. Incorrect drying can cause damage. It can shrink materials or ruin their shape. It can also lead to mildew growth if shoes stay wet too long. I always prioritize safe drying methods. This protects my shoes and ensures they last.

After cleaning, gently pat the shoes with a clean microfiber cloth. Remove as much surface moisture as possible. Do not twist or wring the shoes. This can stretch or deform the materials. You want to absorb excess water without stressing the shoe's structure.

Next, stuff the shoes with paper towels. White, plain paper towels work best. Avoid newspaper. The ink can transfer onto your shoes. The paper towels absorb remaining moisture from the inside. They also help the shoes keep their original shape as they dry. Change the paper towels every few hours. This speeds up the drying process. Continue replacing them until they come out dry.

Place your APL shoes in a well-ventilated area. Choose a spot away from direct sunlight. Avoid direct heat sources like heaters or radiators. High heat can damage glues and synthetic materials. It can also cause cracking or shrinking. Air circulation is key for natural drying. A fan can help if needed. Allow shoes to dry completely for at least 24-48 hours. Make sure they are totally dry before wearing them again. This helps prevent odors and mildew.

### Preventative Care for APL Shoes: Keep Them Cleaner Longer

Cleaning your APL shoes after they get dirty is important. But preventative care is even better. Taking steps to protect your shoes helps them stay clean. It also reduces how often you need to do deep cleaning. I believe a little prevention goes a long way. This approach saves time and extends shoe life.

First, consider using a protective spray. Many brands offer water and stain repellent sprays for athletic shoes. These sprays create a barrier on the shoe's surface. This makes it harder for dirt and liquids to stick. Apply the spray to clean, dry shoes. Follow the product instructions. Reapply it periodically, especially after cleaning. This step is a great defense.

Wipe down your APL shoes regularly. Do this after each wear, if possible. Use a damp cloth to wipe off surface dust and light dirt. This prevents grime from building up. Small stains are easier to remove when fresh. Do not let dirt sit on the shoes for too long. A quick wipe can make a big difference. This routine is simple to incorporate.

Store your APL shoes properly. Keep them in a cool, dry place. Avoid leaving them in direct sunlight or damp environments. If you have shoe bags, use them. They protect shoes from dust. If your shoes get wet from rain, clean and dry them quickly. Do not leave them wet. Good storage habits keep shoes in better condition. This proactive approach helps keep your APLs looking new. ### FAQ Section

#### Can I put APL shoes in the washing machine?
No, machine washing APL shoes is not recommended. The delicate materials and construction can be damaged. Washing machines can cause shrinking, unraveling, or glue weakening. Hand washing is the safest and most effective method. It protects your APLs and helps them last.

#### How often should I clean my APL shoes?
Clean your APL shoes as needed. If you wear them often, a light wipe-down after each use is good. Deep clean them when they show visible dirt or stains. This could be every few weeks or months. Regular care keeps them looking fresh.

#### What should I avoid when cleaning APL shoes?
Avoid harsh chemicals like bleach or strong detergents. Do not use stiff brushes on the knit uppers. Do not put them in a washing machine or dryer. Avoid direct sunlight or high heat for drying. These things can damage the shoe materials.

#### Can I use bleach on white APL shoes?
No, do not use bleach on white APL shoes. Bleach can weaken the fabric. It can also cause yellowing over time. For white areas, use a mild soap solution. A magic eraser can help with stubborn scuffs. Always test any cleaner in a small, hidden spot first.

#### How do I remove tough stains from APL shoes?
For tough stains, create a paste with baking soda and water. Apply it to the stain. Let it sit for 15-20 minutes. Then, gently scrub with an old toothbrush. Wipe away with a damp cloth. Repeat if necessary. Act fast on new stains.
