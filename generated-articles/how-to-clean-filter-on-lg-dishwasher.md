### Step-by-Step Guide: Removing Your LG Dishwasher Filter

Removing your LG dishwasher filter is a simple process. It typically takes only a few minutes. You do not need complex tools. Most LG dishwashers share a similar filter design and removal method. Follow these steps carefully to access your filter.

First, open your dishwasher door completely. Pull out the bottom dish rack. This gives you clear access to the bottom of the dishwasher tub. Look for the cylindrical filter assembly. It is usually in the center or slightly to one side of the lower spray arm. Some LG models have a coarse filter and a fine filter that fit together.

Grasp the cylindrical part of the filter. It might have an arrow indicating "lock" and "unlock" positions. Twist the filter counter-clockwise to unlock it. Once unlocked, lift the filter straight up and out. Some LG models might have a two-piece filter. The cylindrical coarse filter lifts out first. Then, a flat fine mesh filter underneath also lifts out. Be gentle when removing these parts. They are plastic and can break if forced.

If your filter feels stuck, check for any clips or tabs. Sometimes, food debris can make it a little difficult to turn. A gentle wiggle or a slight increase in pressure might be needed. ### Thorough Cleaning Techniques for Your LG Dishwasher Filter

Now that you have removed your LG dishwasher filter, it is time to clean it. This step is where you remove all the trapped food particles and grease. A thorough cleaning ensures your filter works its best. It brings back sparkling dish results.

Start by rinsing the filter parts under warm running water. Use a strong stream to wash away loose food debris. You will be surprised how much grime comes off. After the initial rinse, apply a few drops of liquid dish soap to a soft brush or old toothbrush. Gently scrub all surfaces of the filter parts. Pay close attention to the mesh screens and crevices. These areas often trap stubborn residue. You might see a slimy film or greasy build-up. The brush helps break this down.

For heavily soiled filters, soaking can help. Fill a small bowl with warm water and a few drops of dish soap. Place the filter parts into the soapy water. Let them soak for 10-15 minutes. This softens the stuck-on grime. After soaking, scrub again with the brush. Rinse all parts thoroughly under running water until no soap suds remain. Make sure all visible food particles are gone.

Avoid using abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals. These can damage the plastic filter parts. They can also leave residues that affect your dishwasher's performance. Stick to dish soap and warm water. For extra cleaning power, especially if you have mold or lingering odors, consider using white vinegar. A solution of warm water and vinegar can help dissolve mineral deposits and combat smells. Once clean, let the filter parts air dry or wipe them with a clean towel. Ensure they are completely dry before reinstallation. This prevents any immediate moisture-related issues. A clean filter is vital for a clean dishwasher and sparkling dishes.

### Reinstalling Your LG Dishwasher Filter Correctly

After cleaning your LG dishwasher filter, the next step is to put it back. Proper reinstallation is just as important as cleaning. An incorrectly installed filter can lead to poor wash results or even damage to your dishwasher. This step is quick and easy.

First, ensure all filter parts are completely dry. Place the fine mesh filter back into its designated spot at the bottom of the dishwasher tub. It should sit flat and flush with the tub floor. There is usually a specific cutout for it. Next, align the cylindrical coarse filter with the opening in the fine filter. Lower it down until it sits securely.

Twist the cylindrical filter clockwise. You should feel it lock into place. Many LG filters have arrows or markings to guide you. Line up the unlock arrow with a marker. Then, twist it to the lock position. You might hear a small click when it locks correctly. This click confirms the filter is secure. The filter needs to be snug. If it is loose, food particles can bypass it. This leads to dirty dishes. It can also cause problems for the drain pump.

Visually inspect the installed filter. It should not wobble or move easily. It must fit snugly into its position. Once the filter is secure, slide the bottom dish rack back into place. Close the dishwasher door. You are now ready to run a wash cycle.

An improperly reinstalled filter can cause several issues. Dishes may still come out dirty because debris bypasses the filter. The dishwasher might make unusual noises. Water might not drain correctly. Taking a moment to ensure correct reinstallation prevents these problems. This simple step ensures your LG dishwasher runs efficiently. It also keeps your dishes spotless.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should I clean my LG dishwasher filter?
You should aim to clean your LG dishwasher filter at least once a month. If you use your dishwasher daily or wash many heavily soiled dishes, cleaning it every two to three weeks is better. Regular checks for visible food debris or odors can help determine if it needs cleaning sooner. This routine prevents performance issues.

### Can I put my LG dishwasher filter in the dishwasher?
No, you should not put your LG dishwasher filter in the dishwasher. The high heat and strong detergents in a dishwasher can warp or damage the plastic filter parts. Always clean the filter by hand using warm water, dish soap, and a soft brush. Hand washing ensures the filter maintains its shape and effectiveness.

### What if my LG dishwasher smells bad after cleaning the filter?
If your LG dishwasher still smells bad after cleaning the filter, the odor likely comes from other areas. Check the drain hose, the spray arms, or the dishwasher's interior for hidden food particles, grease build-up, or mold. Running a cycle with white vinegar or a specialized dishwasher cleaner can help eliminate lingering odors.

### Why are my dishes still dirty after cleaning the filter?
If dishes are still dirty, check the spray arms for clogs. Food bits can block the holes, preventing proper water spray. Ensure you are loading the dishwasher correctly, avoiding overcrowding that blocks water flow. Also, check your detergent dispenser to confirm it is releasing soap. Water temperature and hard water can also affect cleaning performance.

### Do all LG dishwashers have a removable filter?
Most modern LG dishwashers have a removable, manual-clean filter system. Older models might have a self-cleaning filter that grinds food particles. However, even with self-cleaning models, it is beneficial to periodically check for debris. Always consult your specific LG dishwasher model's user manual to confirm its filter type and cleaning instructions.
