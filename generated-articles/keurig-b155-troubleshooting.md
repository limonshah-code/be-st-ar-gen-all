## Keurig B155 Troubleshooting: Fix Common Coffee Maker Problems

Is your Keurig B155 commercial coffee maker acting up? A malfunctioning Keurig B155 can disrupt your morning routine or office coffee supply. You want your coffee maker to work reliably every time. Problems like the machine not turning on, refusing to brew, or leaking water can feel frustrating.

This guide provides a comprehensive approach to Keurig B155 troubleshooting. We will cover common issues and offer clear, actionable steps to get your brewer back in perfect working order. You will learn how to diagnose power problems, fix brewing failures, address leaks, and resolve frustrating error messages. We also discuss essential cleaning and descaling practices. This information helps maintain your B155 brewer for consistent, delicious coffee.

### Takeaway

*   Check power connections and reset the brewer for no power issues.
*   Ensure enough water in the reservoir and clean the exit needle for brewing problems.
*   Inspect seals and the drip tray for leaks, tightening components as needed.
*   Follow specific instructions for "Prime" or "Add Water" error messages.
*   Perform regular descaling to prevent mineral buildup and maintain performance.

To troubleshoot a Keurig B155, first confirm power supply and water levels. Then, inspect for clogs in the needles or water lines. Finally, run a descaling cycle to remove mineral buildup. These steps resolve most common operational issues with the machine.

### Solving Keurig B155 Power and Startup Problems

A Keurig B155 that does not power on is a major hurdle. You cannot brew coffee if the machine does not start. Several simple checks can help you diagnose and fix this problem. We want to ensure your brewer receives electricity properly.

First, check the power cord. Make sure it plugs firmly into a working electrical outlet. Sometimes, the cord might come loose or the outlet itself might not have power. You can test the outlet by plugging in another small appliance, like a phone charger or a lamp. If the outlet works, the issue is with the brewer.

Next, look for a tripped circuit breaker. Your Keurig B155 draws significant power, especially when heating water. An overloaded circuit can cause the breaker to trip, cutting power to the outlet. Go to your electrical panel and reset any tripped breakers. If the breaker trips again immediately after plugging in the Keurig, there might be a more serious electrical fault with the brewer itself. In such cases, professional service is advisable to avoid electrical hazards.

Finally, try a simple reset. Unplug the Keurig B155 from the wall outlet for at least 30 seconds. Then, plug it back in. This soft reset can sometimes clear minor internal glitches that prevent the machine from powering on correctly. For similar issues with other models, you might find this general guide helpful: [Keurig Coffee Makers Troubleshooting](https://beacleaner.com/keurig-coffee-makers-troubleshooting). Always ensure the brewer is clean; a build-up of residue can sometimes impact electrical connections or internal sensors. Cleaning the outside casing and base can prevent dust accumulation from affecting electrical parts.

Sometimes, the internal thermal fuse might have tripped to prevent overheating. This typically happens if the machine overheats significantly. If you suspect this, and the simple power checks do not work, the machine likely needs professional repair. Do not attempt to open the machine yourself, as this can void warranties and create safety risks. A functional power supply is the first step to brewing your favorite coffee.

### Resolving Keurig B155 Brewing Issues: No Brew or Partial Brew

When your Keurig B155 fails to brew, or only delivers a partial cup, it is very disappointing. This problem often stems from simple causes related to water flow or K-Cup detection. We need to ensure water moves freely through the system and that the brewer recognizes your coffee pod.

Begin by checking the water reservoir. Make sure it contains enough water for the selected brew size. The B155 has a minimum fill line; if water levels are too low, the machine will not initiate a brew cycle. Refill the reservoir to the MAX fill line if needed. Also, ensure the reservoir sits correctly in its base. A misaligned reservoir can prevent water from being drawn into the heating tank. The floats inside the reservoir must move freely; clean the reservoir if you notice any debris or stickiness on the float mechanism.

Next, inspect the K-Cup holder and the entry/exit needles. These needles are crucial for proper brewing. The top needle punctures the K-Cup, and the bottom needle allows brewed coffee to exit. Over time, coffee grounds can clog these needles. Remove the K-Cup holder and clean both needles with a straightened paper clip. Be careful as the needles are sharp. You can also run a water-only brew cycle without a K-Cup to flush out any remaining debris. This helps clear any blockages that prevent full water flow. For similar cleaning advice on other models, consider learning [How to Clean Inside of Keurig](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-inside-of-keurig).

Another cause for partial brews can be air in the water lines. This is common after refilling an empty reservoir or if the machine has been stored. To prime the system, lift and lower the handle several times with the reservoir full. Then, run several water-only brew cycles without a K-Cup until a full cup of hot water dispenses. This action forces air out of the system and ensures a steady water flow. If the problem persists, the pump might be struggling, which often points to a need for descaling. For more general K155 (a similar model) troubleshooting, see [Keurig K155 Troubleshooting](https://beacleaner.com/keurig-k155-troubleshooting). Ensuring these components are clear and primed solves most brewing problems.

### Addressing Water Flow and Leaking Troubles

Water leaks from a Keurig B155 can be messy and indicate underlying issues. It is important to find the source of the leak to prevent further damage and ensure your brewer functions correctly. Leaks often point to problems with seals, reservoir placement, or internal blockages.

Start by checking the water reservoir. Make sure it is seated firmly and correctly in its base. If the reservoir is misaligned, water can seep out from underneath. Remove the reservoir, clean its base and the machineâ€™s receiving area, and then reinsert it, ensuring it clicks into place. Inspect the reservoir for any visible cracks or damage. Even a hairline crack can lead to a slow leak. If you find cracks, you likely need a replacement reservoir.

Next, examine the drip tray. The drip tray collects minor drips during brewing. However, if it overflows frequently, or if water appears around the base of the machine, the issue is more significant. Check the overflow tube inside the drip tray. This tube can become clogged with coffee residue, causing water to back up and spill out. Clean this tube thoroughly. Also, ensure the drip tray is not overfilled. Empty it regularly.

Inspect the K-Cup holder area. Sometimes, leaks occur here if the K-Cup is not seated correctly, or if the top or bottom needle is damaged or clogged. A damaged seal around the brew head where the K-Cup sits can also cause leaks. Lift the handle and inspect the rubber seal that presses against the K-Cup. If it looks cracked, torn, or misplaced, it needs replacement. You can try gently pressing the seal back into place if it has just shifted. For issues with other Keurig models, you may find solutions in [Keurig K-Mini Troubleshooting](https://beacleaner.com/keurig-k-mini-troubleshooting) or [Keurig Mini Troubleshooting](https://beacleaner.com/keurig-mini-troubleshooting). These parts are critical for a watertight seal during brewing.

Finally, consider internal hose connections. If water leaks from the bottom or back of the machine, an internal hose might have come loose or cracked. This usually requires opening the machine, which is best left to qualified technicians. Do not attempt internal repairs unless you have expertise. Regular descaling also prevents internal pressure build-up from clogs, which can strain hoses and seals. Proper care helps prevent these types of leaks from occurring.

### Addressing Keurig B155 Error Messages

Your Keurig B155 can display various error messages on its screen. These messages provide clues about what is wrong with the machine. Understanding them helps you quickly resolve the issue and resume brewing. We aim to translate these digital signals into actionable steps.

One common error is "Add Water" or "Low Water." This message appears when the water reservoir does not contain enough water for the selected brew. Even if it looks full, the float sensor inside might be stuck or misaligned. First, make sure the reservoir is filled above the minimum fill line. If it is full but the message persists, remove the reservoir, clean it, and gently agitate the float inside to ensure it moves freely. Then, replace the reservoir firmly. Sometimes, simply removing and reinserting the full reservoir solves this issue.

Another frequent message is "Prime." This indicates that the brewer's pump needs to be primed, meaning there is air in the water lines instead of water. This happens after the machine has been drained, stored, or when the water reservoir runs completely empty. To clear this message, lift and lower the brewer handle several times. Then, with a full reservoir, try running a water-only brew cycle without a K-Cup. Repeat this until a continuous stream of water flows out, indicating the system is primed. This process removes air pockets and allows the pump to draw water effectively.

If your B155 displays "Brew Too Hot" or "Overheating," the internal temperature sensor might be faulty or the heating element is working too hard. Unplug the machine immediately and let it cool down for at least 30 minutes. Plug it back in and try brewing again. If the error persists, the brewer requires professional service. Do not continue to operate an overheating machine.

Less common but possible errors include "Water Heating" or "Heating Error." These suggest a problem with the heating element or its sensor. Again, unplugging and letting the machine reset can sometimes help. If not, internal components are likely failing. Remember, error messages are the machine's way of telling you what it needs. Addressing them promptly saves time and prevents further issues. For other model-specific errors, consider reviewing articles like [Keurig K-Supreme Troubleshooting](https://beacleaner.com/keurig-k-supreme-troubleshooting).

### Descaling and Cleaning for Optimal Performance

Regular descaling and cleaning are vital for the longevity and performance of your Keurig B155. Mineral deposits, primarily calcium and lime, build up inside the brewer's heating element and water lines over time. This buildup, known as scale, slows down brewing, reduces water temperature, and can eventually cause the machine to malfunction. Proper maintenance prevents many common issues.

Your Keurig B155 should be descaled every 3 to 6 months, or more frequently if you have hard water. You can use a Keurig descaling solution or white vinegar. To descale, empty the water reservoir. If using white vinegar, fill the reservoir with 10 ounces of undiluted white vinegar. If using a descaling solution, follow the product's instructions. Place a large mug on the drip tray. Begin a brewing cycle without a K-Cup. Repeat this process until the "Add Water" light illuminates.

After the initial descaling cycle, let the brewer stand for at least 30 minutes with the solution inside. This allows the descaling agent to work on stubborn mineral deposits. Then, discard any remaining solution from the reservoir. Rinse the reservoir thoroughly with fresh water. Now, fill the reservoir with fresh water. You must run at least 12 rinse cycles to remove all traces of the descaling solution. Failure to rinse properly can leave a vinegar taste in your coffee. You can find more details on this process at [How to Clean Keurig with Vinegar](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-keurig-with-vinegar) or [Keurig Duo Descaling Troubleshooting](https://beacleaner.com/keurig-duo-descaling-troubleshooting).

Beyond descaling, regular cleaning is necessary. Wash the drip tray and K-Cup holder with warm, soapy water. These parts collect coffee spills and grounds. Wipe down the exterior of the brewer with a damp cloth. Clean the water reservoir weekly. This prevents mold and mildew growth. Always remember to unplug the brewer before cleaning any parts. These simple maintenance steps ensure your Keurig B155 delivers hot, delicious coffee consistently. Neglecting cleaning can lead to poor coffee taste and operational issues.

### Coffee Quality Issues: Weak Brews & Grounds in Coffee

Are you getting weak, watery coffee from your Keurig B155? Or worse, finding annoying grounds in your cup? These issues directly impact your coffee experience. They often point to problems with coffee extraction or filtration. We want your coffee to taste strong and clean.

A weak brew often indicates that water is not passing through the coffee grounds effectively. The primary culprits are usually clogged needles or a faulty pump. As mentioned before, clean the upper and lower needles with a straightened paper clip. Even a small blockage can reduce water flow, leading to under-extraction and a weak cup. Ensure that the water reservoir is full and properly seated, and that there is no air in the lines, as these can also hinder proper water delivery to the K-Cup. Descaling also plays a significant role; mineral buildup can restrict water flow, causing weak brews over time. If your brewer is old and heavily used, a struggling pump might not be generating enough pressure for proper extraction.

Finding grounds in your coffee mug is a common and frustrating problem. This almost always means the needles are not puncturing the K-Cup correctly, or the K-Cup itself is faulty. First, remove the K-Cup holder and clean both the entry and exit needles thoroughly. Check them for any bends or damage. A bent needle might not create a clean puncture, allowing grounds to escape. Also, ensure the K-Cup is placed firmly and correctly in the holder. If it shifts during brewing, the punctures can be uneven, causing grounds to leak.

Sometimes, the issue is with the K-Cup itself. Generic or off-brand K-Cups might not fit perfectly or might have weaker seals, leading to grounds escaping. Try using a different brand of K-Cup to see if the problem persists. If the problem only occurs with one type of K-Cup, switch brands. Another cause can be using a reusable K-Cup filter that is overfilled or has too fine a grind. If you use a reusable filter, ensure the coffee grounds are not packed too tightly and that the grind size is appropriate for a Keurig (medium grind is best). For further insights into general Keurig problems, refer to this guide: [Keurig K25 Troubleshooting](https://beacleaner.com/keurig-k25-troubleshooting). By addressing these potential causes, you can improve your coffee quality.

### Advanced B155 Troubleshooting & Component Checks

When basic troubleshooting steps do not resolve your Keurig B155 problems, you might be facing more complex component issues. These situations often require a deeper understanding of the machine's parts. While some advanced checks are possible, others might necessitate professional assistance. We want to identify deeper problems.

If your brew button is unresponsive or other buttons on the control panel do not work, there could be an issue with the control board or the membrane switch. First, try unplugging the machine for a few minutes and plugging it back in to reset the electronics. This can sometimes clear a temporary glitch. If the problem continues, the control panel assembly may be faulty and need replacement. This is a complex repair that usually requires opening the machine.

A very noisy pump that is not delivering water can indicate a failing pump or a severe airlock. While basic priming steps help with minor airlocks, a persistent problem suggests the pump itself is struggling. You might hear a loud buzzing or grinding sound without any water flow. If the pump fails, the machine cannot brew. Replacing a pump is an internal repair and should only be performed by someone with technical expertise. Ensure the water intake filter in the reservoir is clean; a clogged filter makes the pump work harder.

Temperature issues, where coffee is too cold or excessively hot, point to problems with the heating element or the thermistor (temperature sensor). If the coffee is consistently cold, the heating element might be failing, or the thermistor is providing incorrect readings. Conversely, if it is too hot, the thermistor might not be signaling the heating element to shut off at the correct temperature. These components are internal and require specialized tools and knowledge for diagnosis and replacement. Always unplug the machine and allow it to cool completely before considering any internal checks.

For issues that seem beyond simple fixes, consulting the Keurig B155 user manual can provide specific diagnostic codes or advanced troubleshooting steps. Keurig also offers technical support, and they can guide you through more intricate problems. Do not attempt to repair internal components unless you are confident in your abilities. Improper repairs can damage the machine further or create safety hazards. Remember, many issues, especially with similar models like the K155, often overlap in their solutions, so guides like [Keurig K155 Troubleshooting](https://beacleaner.com/keurig-k155-troubleshooting) can be useful for context.

### FAQ Section

**Q1: Why does my Keurig B155 say "Prime" all the time?**
A: The "Prime" message means air is trapped in the water lines. To fix this, lift and lower the brewer handle several times. Then, run multiple water-only brew cycles without a K-Cup until a full stream of water comes out. This removes air pockets from the system.

**Q2: How often should I descale my Keurig B155?**
A: You should descale your Keurig B155 every 3 to 6 months. If you live in an area with hard water, descaling more frequently, perhaps every 2-3 months, is recommended. Regular descaling prevents mineral buildup and maintains optimal performance.

**Q3: Why is my Keurig B155 leaking water from the bottom?**
A: Leaking from the bottom often indicates a problem with the water reservoir seal or an internal hose connection. Ensure the reservoir is seated correctly and not cracked. If the leak persists, an internal hose might be loose or damaged, requiring professional inspection.

**Q4: My Keurig B155 coffee is too weak. What can I do?**
A: Weak coffee usually results from clogged needles or a need for descaling. Clean both the upper and lower K-Cup needles with a paper clip. Also, perform a descaling cycle to clear internal mineral buildup, which improves water flow and coffee extraction.

**Q5: What do I do if my Keurig B155 won't turn on?**
A: First, check if the power cord is securely plugged into a working outlet. Test the outlet with another device. Try unplugging the Keurig for 30 seconds and plugging it back in for a soft reset. If these steps fail, check your circuit breaker or consider professional service.
