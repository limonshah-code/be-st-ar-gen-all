## Reclaiming Freshness: How To Clean Outside Garbage Can

Do you ever hold your breath when you open your outdoor garbage can? That familiar, unpleasant odor often signals a hidden world of grime, bacteria, and maybe even unwelcome guests. An unkempt outdoor bin is not just an eyesore; it becomes a breeding ground for pests and a source of lingering smells. Cleaning your outside garbage can is a simple task that significantly improves your home's hygiene and overall outdoor environment.

This guide provides a comprehensive approach to tackling even the dirtiest bins. We will explore why regular cleaning is crucial, what tools and solutions you need, and provide clear steps for a thorough clean. You will learn how to remove stubborn odors, deter pests, and keep your bin fresh between cleanings. Let's transform that smelly receptacle into a sparkling clean container.

### Takeaway

Cleaning your outside garbage can keeps your home clean and healthy. Prioritize safety with protective gear. Use effective cleaning methods to remove dirt and odors. Maintain your bin regularly for long-term freshness and fewer pests.

### A Quick Answer

To clean an outside garbage can, first empty all contents. Rinse the inside with water. Scrub the interior and exterior surfaces using a stiff brush and a strong cleaning solution. Rinse the can thoroughly to remove all soap and debris, then allow it to air dry completely before use.

## Why Cleaning Your Outside Garbage Can Matters So Much

A dirty outdoor garbage can is more than just unsightly. It poses several problems for your home and health. Understanding these issues helps you appreciate the importance of regular cleaning. We often overlook this part of our home maintenance, but its impact is significant.

First, consider the powerful odors. Food waste, wet garbage, and decaying organic matter create a foul smell. This odor can waft around your yard, making outdoor spaces less enjoyable. It can even attract unwanted attention from neighbors. A clean garbage can eliminates these offensive smells, improving your outdoor air quality.

Next, think about pests. Raccoons, rodents, flies, and maggots are all attracted to dirty bins. Food residue provides an easy meal for these creatures. A dirty can becomes a breeding ground for flies, leading to maggots inside. Keeping your bin clean removes their food source and deters them from making your can their home. Preventing pests means a healthier environment around your house.

Finally, hygiene is a major concern. Garbage cans harbor millions of bacteria and viruses. These microorganisms can spread to your hands, tools, or even back into your home. Regular cleaning disinfects the surfaces, reducing the risk of illness. It maintains a sanitary environment, protecting your family from harmful pathogens. My home feels much safer knowing my bins are clean.

For an eco-friendlier approach, white vinegar is excellent for cutting grease and deodorizing. You can also mix baking soda with water to create a powerful scrubbing paste. This combination helps neutralize strong odors.

Consider a long-handled scrub brush or a broom dedicated to cleaning your garbage can. This keeps you from bending over too much. A bucket for mixing solutions is also helpful. ## Step-by-Step Guide: How To Clean Outside Garbage Can Thoroughly

Cleaning an outdoor garbage can seems like a daunting task, but it is simple with a clear plan. Follow these steps for a truly clean and fresh bin. Always work in an outdoor area where water can drain freely, such as a driveway or lawn. This prevents dirty water from pooling in unwanted places.

### Empty and Initial Rinse

First, ensure the garbage can is completely empty. Remove any remaining trash, bags, or debris. Turn the can on its side and shake out loose dirt. Next, use your garden hose to thoroughly rinse the inside of the can. Blast away any large chunks of food or dirt. This initial rinse removes the easy stuff, preparing the surface for scrubbing. If your can has a lid, rinse that separately as well.

### Apply Cleaning Solution

After rinsing, stand the can upright. Now, apply your chosen cleaning solution. If using a liquid cleaner, pour some inside and around the edges. For soap and water, fill a bucket and pour it into the can. If using a powder like baking soda, sprinkle it generously over the wet surfaces. Make sure the solution coats all interior surfaces. Let the solution sit for a few minutes to break down grime. This soaking time helps the cleaner work its magic on stubborn dirt.

### Scrubbing Away Grime

Put on your gloves and safety glasses. Use your stiff-bristled brush to vigorously scrub the inside of the can. Pay close attention to the bottom, sides, and any crevices where dirt accumulates. Scrub the exterior of the can as well, focusing on any stains or sticky spots. Don't forget the lid; scrub its underside and edges thoroughly. A good scrub removes stuck-on food and grime. For particularly stubborn areas, you might need to apply more solution and scrub harder.

### Final Rinse and Dry

Once you have scrubbed every part, it's time for the final rinse. Tip the can on its side and use the garden hose to rinse away all soap and dirt. Continue rinsing until the water running out is clear and free of suds. Rinse the lid thoroughly too. Make sure no cleaning solution remains. After rinsing, tip the can upside down or on its side to allow all water to drain out. Let the can air dry completely in the sun. Sunlight helps to further sanitize the can and prevents mold growth. [Mold can be a persistent problem](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-mold-with-vinegar) in damp areas, so thorough drying is essential.

## Tackling Stubborn Grime and Odors: Advanced Cleaning Tips

Sometimes, a basic wash isn't enough. Outdoor garbage cans can develop intense odors and deep-seated grime over time. These require a bit more effort and specialized techniques. My own garbage can once had a smell that just wouldn't quit. I found these advanced tips truly make a difference.

For lingering, stubborn odors, an enzyme cleaner is your best friend. These cleaners contain beneficial bacteria that break down organic matter causing the smell. Spray the inside of the can generously after the initial wash and let it sit for several hours, or even overnight, before the final rinse. This process literally digests the source of the odor. You can also use a mixture of baking soda and water. Sprinkle baking soda liberally inside the wet can, let it sit for a few hours, then scrub and rinse. It neutralizes odors effectively.

Deep-seated grime, like sticky residue from burst bags or dried spills, can be tough. For these, a degreaser cleaner works wonders. Apply it directly to the sticky areas, let it soak for 10-15 minutes, then scrub vigorously with your stiff brush. Hot water also helps loosen stubborn deposits. If you have access to a power washer, it can blast away layers of caked-on dirt and residue with incredible efficiency. Always wear protective gear when using strong cleaners or power tools.

If you are dealing with mold or mildew, especially in shaded areas, white vinegar is an excellent natural solution. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Spray the affected areas, let it sit for an hour, then scrub and rinse. Vinegar is acidic and kills most mold spores. [Cleaning mold with vinegar](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-mold-off-walls) is a safe and effective way to tackle these growths. For really tough mold, a bleach solution might be necessary, but always use caution and good ventilation.

After cleaning, consider using odor absorbers. Place a charcoal briquette or a pouch of baking soda at the bottom of the can before inserting a new liner. These items absorb lingering smells and keep the can fresher for longer between washes. I find this simple step prevents odors from building up quickly.

## Preventative Measures: Keeping Your Bin Cleaner for Longer

Cleaning your outside garbage can is a great start, but prevention is key to keeping it fresh. Implementing a few simple habits can drastically reduce how often you need to perform a deep clean. These small changes make a big difference in the long run. My own bins stay much cleaner now with these routines.

Always use heavy-duty garbage bags. Flimsy bags tear easily, leading to spills and leaks inside the can. Choose bags that are thick and durable. Double-bagging particularly wet or messy waste provides an extra layer of protection. This simple step prevents liquids from seeping onto the can's surfaces.

Another effective method is to wrap or bag all food waste. Loose food scraps are the primary cause of odors and attract pests. Wrap meat scraps in newspaper before putting them in a bag. Place all food waste in a separate, sealed bag within your main garbage bag. This minimizes direct contact with the can walls and contains smells. It also makes it less appealing for animals.

Rinse out containers before tossing them. Milk cartons, yogurt cups, and other food containers often have residue that attracts pests and creates odors. A quick rinse under the tap removes these attractive bits. This small effort goes a long way in keeping your bin clean. Similarly, drain excess liquids from food items before disposal.

Consider investing in a bin liner or deodorizer. Some products are designed to absorb moisture and neutralize odors. You can sprinkle baking soda at the bottom of the can before putting in a new bag. This helps absorb any accidental leaks and keeps the can smelling fresher. Regularly sprinkling [baking soda inside your garbage disposal](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-garbage-disposal-with-lemon) also works for kitchen odors.

Finally, place your garbage can in a shaded, well-ventilated area if possible. Direct sunlight can cook organic matter, intensifying odors and encouraging bacterial growth. Good airflow helps dissipate any smells that do escape. Regular emptying, especially of food waste, is also critical. Don't let waste sit for too long in hot weather.

## Eco-Friendly Options for a Greener Clean

Caring for our environment extends to our cleaning practices. You can clean your outside garbage can effectively without harsh chemicals. Many natural ingredients provide powerful cleaning and deodorizing properties. These options are safer for you, your pets, and the planet. I always prefer using natural solutions when possible.

White vinegar is an incredible all-purpose cleaner. Its acetic acid content breaks down grease, disinfects surfaces, and neutralizes odors. Mix equal parts white vinegar and water in a spray bottle. Spray the inside of your garbage can generously, let it sit for about 30 minutes, then scrub and rinse. For extra deodorizing power, you can sprinkle baking soda into the can before spraying with vinegar. The fizzing action helps lift dirt. [Cleaning with vinegar](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-shower-with-vinegar) is a versatile method for many household tasks.

Baking soda is another natural superstar. It is an excellent abrasive for scrubbing and a powerful odor neutralizer. Sprinkle a generous amount of baking soda inside the wet garbage can. Use a damp brush to scrub the paste onto the surfaces. Let it sit for a while before rinsing thoroughly. For ongoing odor control, sprinkle a layer of baking soda at the bottom of your clean, dry bin before putting in a new bag.

Lemon juice also offers natural cleaning and deodorizing benefits. Its citric acid helps break down grime and leaves a fresh scent. You can add lemon juice to your vinegar solution or simply rub cut lemons directly on stains and smelly areas. The fresh citrus smell is much more pleasant than chemical fumes.

When cleaning with eco-friendly solutions, consider how you dispose of the wastewater. Ideally, clean your garbage can on a grassy area where the water can naturally filter into the ground. Avoid cleaning on concrete or asphalt near storm drains if you are using even mild chemical cleaners. This prevents contaminated water from entering public waterways. Natural cleaners are safer for this kind of drainage.

For tough stains, simple hot water can be surprisingly effective. Fill your can with very hot water, add a few drops of natural dish soap, and let it sit for an hour. The heat helps loosen grime before you scrub. This method works well for softening dried-on debris.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues: Pests, Mildew, and Rust

Even with regular cleaning, you might encounter specific problems with your outdoor garbage can. Pests, mildew, and rust are common issues that require targeted solutions. Knowing how to address these helps maintain your bin's condition and functionality. I have dealt with all these problems at some point.

### Dealing with Pests

Maggots are a clear sign of flies breeding in your can. To get rid of them, first empty the can completely. Sprinkle a generous amount of salt, lime, or borax over the maggots. These dehydrate the maggots effectively. Let it sit for an hour. Then, clean the can thoroughly with hot, soapy water and a stiff brush, making sure to rinse and dry it completely. After cleaning, ensure your garbage can lid seals tightly. This prevents flies from laying eggs and keeps other pests out. You might also consider placing mothballs or a pest deterrent packet inside the can (outside the liner) to repel unwanted visitors.

Raccoons and other scavengers are attracted to food odors. To deter them, always bag and tie your trash securely. Use sturdy garbage cans with locking lids or heavy-duty latches. Some people bungee cord their lids shut for extra security. Regularly hosing down the outside of your can can also help reduce lingering food smells that attract animals.

### Battling Mildew

Mildew thrives in damp, dark environments. If you see black or green fuzzy patches, your can has mildew. To remove it, create a solution of equal parts white vinegar and water. Spray the mildewed areas thoroughly. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes to an hour. Then, scrub vigorously with a stiff brush. Rinse the can completely and ensure it dries fully in the sun. Sunlight is a natural mildew inhibitor. For particularly stubborn mildew, a bleach solution (1 part bleach to 9 parts water) can be used, but always wear protective gear and ensure good ventilation. [Cleaning mold in a shower](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-mold-in-shower) uses similar principles.

### Addressing Rust

Rust can form on metal garbage cans or components like hinges and handles. To remove light rust, use a wire brush or steel wool. For more significant rust, you can try a rust-removing product available at hardware stores. Always follow product instructions carefully. After removing rust, consider applying a rust-inhibiting primer and paint to the affected areas. This prevents future rust formation. For plastic cans, rust is less of an issue, but rust stains from metal items inside can occur. These can often be removed with a paste of baking soda and lemon juice, scrubbed and rinsed.

By knowing how to tackle these specific issues, you can keep your outside garbage can in better condition. Proactive prevention, combined with targeted cleaning, ensures a healthier and cleaner outdoor space.

## FAQ Section

### How often should I clean my outside garbage can?
Aim to clean your outside garbage can at least once every month, especially during warmer seasons. If you dispose of a lot of food waste, or notice strong odors and pests, clean it more frequently. Regular cleaning prevents grime buildup, odors, and insect infestations.

### What's the best way to get rid of maggots in my trash can?
To eliminate maggots, first empty the can. Sprinkle a generous amount of salt, borax, or diatomaceous earth over them. Allow it to sit for an hour to dehydrate them. Then, thoroughly clean the can with hot, soapy water, rinse well, and let it dry completely in the sun.

### Can I use a pressure washer to clean my garbage can?
Yes, a pressure washer is highly effective for cleaning outside garbage cans. It blasts away stubborn grime, dried-on food, and dirt quickly. Use caution and wear safety glasses. Start with a lower pressure setting to avoid damaging the can, especially if it's plastic, then increase as needed.

### Are there any natural alternatives to bleach for cleaning?
Absolutely. White vinegar mixed with water (50/50 ratio) is an excellent natural disinfectant and deodorizer. Baking soda acts as an abrasive cleaner and odor absorber. Lemon juice adds cleaning power and a fresh scent. These natural options are safer for the environment and pets.

### How do I prevent my garbage can from smelling bad?
Prevent bad smells by bagging all food waste securely. Rinse out food containers before disposal. Sprinkle baking soda at the bottom of the can before adding a new liner. Ensure your can has a tight-fitting lid to trap odors and deter pests. Regular cleaning also helps immensely.

### What should I do with the dirty water after cleaning?
If you used mild, biodegradable cleaners like soap and water or vinegar, you can typically let the dirty water drain onto a grassy area. Avoid draining it into storm drains, especially if you used harsh chemicals. For stronger chemical solutions, check local regulations for proper disposal.
