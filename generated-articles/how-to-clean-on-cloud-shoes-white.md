These materials require gentle cleaning methods. Harsh chemicals or strong scrubbing can damage them. Using the wrong cleaning agent might discolor the white mesh or foam. Understanding these materials helps you choose the right cleaning approach. My goal is to protect your shoes.

The mesh on On Cloud shoes is durable but delicate. It can trap dirt and dust easily. The CloudTec® sole units are resilient but can also accumulate grime. Standard cleaning products might be too aggressive. They can break down the adhesives or fade the white color. I always recommend testing any new cleaner on a small, hidden area first. This prevents accidental damage. Gentle care ensures the longevity and appearance of your favorite footwear.

Their specialized design also impacts cleaning. The "clouds" on the sole have unique crevices. These areas can hold mud and small debris. You need tools that can reach into these spaces effectively. A soft-bristled brush or an old toothbrush works well here. I find that a little patience goes a long way. This careful approach helps you clean every part of the shoe without harm. Knowing your shoe’s construction makes cleaning easier.

Proper material care extends shoe life. It also keeps your shoes looking new. You avoid issues like yellowing or stiffening of the mesh. On Cloud shoes are an investment in comfort and style. ### Gathering Your Gentle Cleaning Supplies

Before you start, collect everything you need. Having all supplies ready streamlines the cleaning process. You need a few basic items. These items are likely already in your home. A soft-bristled brush is essential. An old toothbrush works perfectly for small areas. You also need a mild soap. Dish soap or a gentle laundry detergent works well. Avoid harsh detergents or bleach. These can damage the shoe materials.

Next, get two small bowls or buckets. One bowl will hold clean, warm water. The other will hold your soapy water solution. Microfiber cloths are also very useful. They absorb water well and are gentle on surfaces. Paper towels can work in a pinch. However, microfiber cloths are reusable and more effective. You might also want a shoe tree or some paper to stuff inside the shoes. This helps maintain their shape while drying.

Consider specialized shoe cleaners if you prefer. Many brands offer gentle foam cleaners specifically for sneakers. These cleaners are often pre-mixed and easy to use. They are usually safe for various materials. I find them convenient for quick clean-ups. Just make sure the product specifies it is safe for mesh and white materials. Always read the instructions on the product label.

Optional tools include a Magic Eraser for rubber soles. It works wonders on scuffs. A specialized shoe protector spray is also good for post-cleaning. It helps repel water and stains. Having the right tools makes the job much easier. It also ensures you clean effectively without causing damage. Preparing your cleaning station is the first real step to success.

### Step-by-Step Pre-Cleaning Preparation

Proper preparation sets the stage for a successful clean. This step removes loose dirt and protects delicate parts. First, remove the laces from your white On Cloud shoes. Laces gather a lot of dirt. They also make it hard to clean the shoe tongue and eyelets. You can wash the laces separately. I usually soak them in a small bowl of soapy water. Then I scrub them gently.

Next, take out the insoles. Insoles absorb sweat and odors. They also prevent water from saturating the shoe’s interior during cleaning. You can clean insoles separately if needed. Often, a simple wipe down with a damp cloth is enough. For a deeper clean, sprinkle baking soda on them to absorb odors. Let them air out thoroughly. If you need to clean the inside of your shoes, you can find more guidance on [how to clean the inside of shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-the-inside-of-shoes).

Now, remove any loose dirt from the outside of the shoes. Bang the soles together gently over a trash can. This dislodges dried mud and debris. You can also use a soft-bristled brush or an old toothbrush. Brush off any remaining dirt from the upper, sides, and sole. Pay attention to the CloudTec® pods. Dirt often gets stuck in these crevices. This initial brush-off prevents dirt from spreading during washing.

This pre-cleaning step is vital. It stops dirt from turning into muddy smears once water is introduced. It also ensures your cleaning solution can work directly on embedded grime. Take your time with this part. A few extra minutes here will save you effort later. Your shoes are now ready for a deeper wash.

### Effective Hand-Washing Techniques for On Cloud White

Hand-washing is the safest and most effective method for your white On Cloud shoes. Machine washing can be too harsh for their delicate mesh and sole units. It can cause discoloration or damage the shoe's structure. I strongly advise against putting On Cloud shoes in a washing machine. If you need to know more about general shoe washing, you can explore information on [how to clean shoes in the washer](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-shoes-in-the-washer), but remember this specific advice for On Cloud shoes.

Start by mixing your cleaning solution. Add a few drops of mild soap to a bowl of warm water. Stir it gently to create some suds. Dip your soft-bristled brush into the soapy water. Begin scrubbing the mesh upper of the shoe. Use gentle, circular motions. Do not press too hard. The goal is to lift dirt from the fabric, not to rub it in. Rinse your brush frequently in the clean water to avoid spreading dirt. You can find more general tips on [how to clean white shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-white-shoes).

Next, focus on the rubber soles and CloudTec® pods. These parts often collect more stubborn dirt and scuffs. Dip your brush back into the soapy water. Scrub the rubber parts more firmly if needed. An old toothbrush is excellent for getting into the grooves of the CloudTec® elements. Pay close attention to the sides of the sole. Mud and grime often build up here. For specific advice on rubber shoes, refer to [how to clean white rubber shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-white-rubber-shoes).

Once you have scrubbed all areas, wipe the shoes clean with a damp microfiber cloth. Make sure to remove all soap residue. Soap left on the shoe can attract more dirt or cause yellowing. Repeat the wiping process with a clean, damp cloth until no suds remain. This step ensures a pristine finish. Your shoes should now look much brighter.

### Tackling Stubborn Stains on White On Clouds

Even with regular cleaning, white shoes can develop stubborn stains. Mud, grass, or oil can be tricky to remove. Do not despair. You can tackle these specific stains with targeted approaches. For mud stains, let the mud dry completely first. Then, brush off as much as possible before applying any liquid. If the mud is still wet, it will just spread. This is a crucial step when dealing with [how to clean mud off white shoes](https://beacleacleaner.com/how-to-clean-mud-off-white-shoes).

For general stubborn marks, a paste of baking soda and mild detergent can work wonders. Mix equal parts baking soda and detergent with a small amount of water. Apply this paste directly to the stain. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes. Then, gently scrub the area with a soft brush or toothbrush. Rinse thoroughly with a damp cloth. This method is effective for many types of discoloration. Remember to always rinse completely.

Hydrogen peroxide is another powerful stain remover for white materials. Mix one part hydrogen peroxide with two parts water. Apply this solution to the stain with a cotton ball or soft cloth. Let it sit for a few minutes. Then, rinse the area well. This is particularly useful for light yellowing or tough spots on white mesh. You can find more details on using this solution on [how to clean white shoes with hydrogen peroxide](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-white-shoes-with-hydrogen-peroxide).

For oil or grease stains, use a small amount of liquid dish soap directly on the spot. Dish soap is designed to cut through grease. Gently rub it in with your finger or a soft cloth. Let it sit for a minute, then rinse thoroughly. If a stain persists, you might need to repeat the process. Always be gentle, especially on the mesh. You want to lift the stain, not damage the fabric. For comprehensive advice on various marks, refer to [how to clean stained shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-stained-shoes).

### Drying Your On Cloud Shoes Safely

Proper drying is as important as proper cleaning. Incorrect drying can damage your On Cloud shoes. It can cause yellowing, shrinkage, or structural issues. Never put your On Cloud shoes in a dryer. The high heat can melt adhesives, deform the foam, or shrink the mesh. This will ruin your shoes. Air drying is the only safe method.

First, gently blot any excess water from the shoes with a clean, dry microfiber cloth. Press the cloth against the mesh and sole. This helps speed up the drying process. Next, stuff your shoes with white paper towels or clean, dry cloths. Do not use newspaper. The ink from newspaper can transfer onto the white fabric, creating new stains. The paper towels absorb moisture from inside the shoe. They also help maintain the shoe's original shape as it dries. Change the paper towels every few hours if they become very damp.

Place your shoes in a well-ventilated area. Choose a spot away from direct sunlight or artificial heat sources. Direct sunlight can cause white materials to yellow. Heaters can cause materials to warp. A spot indoors with good airflow is ideal. You can place them near an open window or a fan. Allow plenty of time for them to dry completely. This can take 24-48 hours, depending on humidity and how wet they were.

Make sure the shoes are bone dry before wearing them again. Wearing damp shoes can lead to mildew growth and unpleasant odors. It also weakens the materials over time. Being patient during the drying phase protects your cleaning efforts. It also extends the lifespan of your beautiful white On Cloud shoes.

### Maintaining Your White On Cloud Shoes Long-Term

Cleaning your white On Cloud shoes is important. Preventing them from getting too dirty is even better. Regular maintenance keeps your shoes looking fresh between deep cleans. After each wear, give your shoes a quick wipe-down. Use a dry cloth or soft brush to remove dust and light dirt. This prevents grime from setting into the fabric. It makes future cleaning much easier.

Consider using a shoe protector spray. These sprays create a barrier on the shoe's surface. This barrier repels water and stains. Apply the spray to clean, dry shoes according to the product instructions. Reapply it periodically, especially if you wear your shoes often. This protective layer is a game-changer for white footwear. It helps your shoes resist dirt and liquid.

Store your white On Cloud shoes properly. Keep them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Sunlight can cause white materials to yellow over time. Using shoe trees or stuffing them with paper helps maintain their shape. This also prevents creases. Do not store them in plastic bags. Shoes need to breathe. Proper storage prevents mildew and odors.

Rotate your shoes if you wear them frequently. Giving your On Clouds a rest allows them to air out completely. It also extends their overall lifespan. Having a few pairs of shoes to switch between is ideal. Consistent, gentle care will keep your white On Cloud shoes looking new for a long time. These simple habits make a big difference.

### FAQ Section

**Q: Can you put On Cloud shoes in the washing machine?**
A: No, you should not put On Cloud shoes in the washing machine. The machine's harsh cycles and high heat can damage the delicate mesh, foam soles, and adhesives. Machine washing can also cause yellowing or structural deformities. Hand-washing with mild soap and water is the safest and most recommended method for cleaning On Cloud shoes.

**Q: How often should I clean my white On Cloud shoes?**
A: Clean your white On Cloud shoes as needed. For light dirt, a quick wipe-down after each wear is helpful. For deeper cleaning, aim for once a month or every few weeks, depending on how often you wear them and what activities you do. Regular light cleaning prevents dirt from building up.

**Q: What can I use to remove yellowing from my white On Cloud shoes?**
A: For yellowing, a paste of baking soda and a small amount of mild detergent can be effective. Apply it, let it sit, then gently scrub and rinse. A solution of one part hydrogen peroxide to two parts water can also help. Apply it, let it sit, then rinse thoroughly. Always air dry away from direct sunlight to prevent further yellowing.

**Q: Is it okay to use bleach on white On Cloud shoes?**
A: No, avoid using bleach on your white On Cloud shoes. Bleach can weaken the shoe's materials, especially the mesh. It can also cause irreversible yellowing or discoloration, making the problem worse. Stick to milder cleaning agents like dish soap, baking soda, or diluted hydrogen peroxide for best results.

**Q: How do I protect my white On Cloud shoes from getting dirty?**
A: To protect your white On Cloud shoes, use a waterproof and stain-repellent spray designed for footwear. Apply it to clean, dry shoes. Avoid wearing them in very muddy or dusty conditions. After each wear, brush off loose dirt. Store them in a clean, dry place away from direct sunlight.

**Q: What's the best way to dry On Cloud shoes?**
A: The best way to dry On Cloud shoes is by air-drying. After cleaning, blot excess water with a clean cloth. Stuff the shoes with white paper towels to absorb moisture and maintain shape. Place them in a well-ventilated area away from direct sunlight or heat sources. Allow 24-48 hours for complete drying.
