## How To Clean Kids Shoes: Make Them Sparkle Again!

Kids' shoes face constant adventures. They jump in puddles, play in mud, and explore every corner of the park. This daily activity leaves footwear looking less than ideal. Keeping children's shoes clean presents a common challenge for many parents. Dirt, grime, and odors can build up quickly. Learning **how to clean kids shoes** properly saves money and extends the life of their favorite pairs.

A clean pair of shoes also helps maintain foot health. Bacteria and fungi thrive in dirty, damp environments. Regular cleaning prevents these issues. This guide provides simple, effective methods for cleaning all types of children's shoes. We will cover general cleaning steps, specific material care, stain removal, odor control, and proper drying techniques. Get ready to transform those muddy messes into fresh, clean footwear.

### Takeaway

*   Remove loose dirt and debris before wet cleaning.
*   Identify the shoe material to choose the correct cleaning method.
*   Use mild cleaning solutions and soft brushes for gentle care.
*   Address stains quickly for the best removal results.
*   Always air dry shoes away from direct heat or sunlight.
*   Use odor absorbers to keep the inside of shoes fresh.

### What is the best way to clean kids shoes?

The best way to clean kids shoes involves first removing loose dirt with a dry brush. Then, use a mild soap or detergent mixed with water, applied with a soft cloth or brush, based on the shoe's material. Rinse thoroughly with a damp cloth and allow the shoes to air dry completely away from heat sources.

## Getting Started: Essential Tools for Cleaning Kids Shoes

Before you begin cleaning, gather your supplies. Having the right tools makes the process smoother and more effective. You do not need many specialized items. Most things are likely already in your home. This preparation step sets you up for success.

Start with a dedicated cleaning area. An old towel or a plastic sheet protects your work surface. I usually do this outside or in the laundry room. This prevents messes from spreading. Prepare your tools based on the shoe material you plan to clean. Different materials require different approaches.

Here are the basic tools you will need:

*   **Soft-bristled brush or old toothbrush**: This helps remove loose dirt and scrub surfaces gently. I find an old toothbrush perfect for getting into small crevices.

*   **Warm water**: For cleaning and rinsing.
*   **Paper towels or old newspapers**: For stuffing shoes during drying.

Removing initial dirt is very important. Loose soil can scratch shoe surfaces during cleaning. It also makes the wet cleaning step less effective. For shoes covered in mud, let the mud dry first. Then, gently tap the shoes together or use a stiff brush to knock off large clumps. If your child's shoes are particularly muddy, you might find more specific tips helpful. ## General Cleaning Steps for All Kids Shoes

Once you have your tools ready, you can start cleaning. These general steps apply to almost all types of children's shoes. Following a consistent process helps ensure thorough cleaning. It also protects the shoes from damage. I always start with these steps, no matter the shoe style.

First, remove the laces and insoles from the shoes. Laces often hold a lot of dirt and can be cleaned separately. Insoles absorb sweat and odor. They need special attention. You can wash laces by hand with mild soap and water. For insoles, you might need to scrub them gently or even replace them if they are too worn or smelly. Understanding [how to clean the inside of shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-the-inside-of-shoes) is key for complete shoe freshness.

Next, dry brush the entire shoe. Use your soft-bristled brush or an old toothbrush. Brush away any loose dirt, dust, or debris from the outside of the shoe. Pay attention to the soles and seams. Dirt often collects in these areas. This step prevents mud or grit from turning into a muddy paste when wet cleaning starts. It also helps expose any deep stains.

Prepare your cleaning solution. Mix a small amount of mild soap (like dish soap or laundry detergent) with warm water in a bowl. Do not use too much soap. Too much soap can leave residue or damage delicate materials. A little goes a long way.

Dip a microfiber cloth or soft brush into the solution. Wring out excess liquid. You want the cloth or brush to be damp, not dripping wet. Gently wipe down the outer surfaces of the shoe. For stubborn spots, lightly scrub in a circular motion. Be gentle, especially on softer materials. Avoid saturating the shoes with water, especially if they are not designed for heavy water exposure.

After cleaning, wipe the shoes with a clean, damp cloth. This removes any soap residue. Soap left on the shoes can attract more dirt later. Rinse your cloth often in clean water. Repeat this step until all soap is gone.

Finally, prepare the shoes for drying. Proper drying is vital to prevent odors and damage. We will cover drying in a later section. Always let shoes air dry completely. This keeps them looking good and smelling fresh. If your kids' shoes have a persistent odor even after cleaning, you may need additional steps to address the smell. ## Cleaning Specific Shoe Materials for Children

Different shoe materials require different cleaning methods. What works for canvas might harm leather. Knowing the material helps you choose the right approach. This protects the shoes and ensures a good clean. I always check the shoe tag for material information before I start.

### Canvas and Fabric Kids Shoes

Canvas and fabric shoes are very common for kids. They are durable but get dirty easily. These shoes are often the easiest to clean.

*   **Supplies**: Mild soap, warm water, soft brush, microfiber cloths.
*   **Method**: After removing loose dirt, mix mild soap with warm water. Dip a soft brush or cloth into the solution. Gently scrub the entire canvas or fabric surface. For stubborn dirt, you can apply a paste of baking soda and a little water. Let it sit for a few minutes, then scrub. Rinse with a clean, damp cloth until all soap residue is gone. Do not soak canvas shoes for long periods. If your child's shoes are made of canvas, these steps are perfect. Find detailed instructions on [how to clean canvas shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-canvas-shoes) for the best results.

### Leather and Synthetic Leather Kids Shoes

Leather and synthetic leather offer durability and are easy to wipe clean. However, they need different care to prevent drying or cracking.

*   **Supplies**: Mild soap (like dish soap or saddle soap), warm water, soft cloths, leather conditioner (for real leather).
*   **Method**: Wipe off loose dirt with a dry cloth. Mix a very small amount of mild soap with water. Dampen a cloth with this solution and gently wipe the shoe surface. Do not scrub hard. For real leather, follow up with a leather conditioner after cleaning. This keeps the material supple and prevents cracks. Always avoid harsh cleaners or excessive water on leather. Learn more about [how to clean leather shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-leather-shoes) to keep them looking new.

### Mesh and Athletic Kids Shoes

Many kids' sneakers feature mesh or synthetic materials. These provide breathability but can trap dirt.

*   **Supplies**: Mild detergent, warm water, soft brush, old toothbrush.
*   **Method**: Use a soft brush to remove surface dirt. Prepare a solution of mild detergent and warm water. Dip an old toothbrush into the solution and gently scrub the mesh areas. Work in small sections. Rinse with a clean, damp cloth. Avoid harsh scrubbing that could damage the mesh. Athletic shoes often see heavy use and can accumulate a lot of grime. ### Rubber and Slides for Kids

Rubber boots, sandals, and slides are simple to clean. They are waterproof and robust.

*   **Supplies**: Mild soap, water, sponge or cloth.
*   **Method**: Simply wipe down with soapy water and a sponge. For textured soles, use a brush to remove trapped dirt. Rinse thoroughly under running water. These materials handle water well, making them quick to clean. If your child has rubber slides that need a refresh, you can also look at specific advice on [how to clean slides shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-slides-shoes).

### Suede and Nubuck Kids Shoes

Suede and nubuck are delicate materials. They require special care. Water can cause stains.

*   **Supplies**: Suede brush, suede eraser, specialized suede cleaner (optional).
*   **Method**: Never use water or harsh soaps. Use a dry suede brush to remove dirt. Brush in one direction. For stains, use a suede eraser. Gently rub the eraser over the stain. If the stain persists, a specialized suede cleaner might be necessary. Test any cleaner in an inconspicuous area first.

By tailoring your cleaning approach to the shoe material, you protect your child's footwear. This keeps shoes looking good for longer. It prevents damage that can come from using the wrong cleaning method.

## Tackling Tough Stains on Kids Footwear

Kids' shoes attract all sorts of stains. From mud to grass, and food spills, tough spots need specific treatment. Acting quickly on stains increases your chance of full removal. Different stains require different approaches. I have learned to identify the stain before trying to clean it.

### Mud Stains

Mud is one of the most common stains on kids' shoes. Do not try to clean wet mud.

*   **Method**: Let the mud dry completely. Once dry, most of it will flake off. Use a stiff brush or an old toothbrush to gently scrape or brush off the dried mud. For any remaining residue, you can use a damp cloth with mild soap, according to the shoe material's guidelines. For a comprehensive guide on mud removal, see our article on [how to clean muddy shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-muddy-shoes).

### Grass Stains

Grass stains often have a green tint due to chlorophyll. These can be tricky.

*   **Method**: For canvas or fabric shoes, try rubbing alcohol or white vinegar. Apply a small amount to a clean cloth. Dab the stain gently. Do not rub, as this can spread the stain. Rinse with a damp cloth. For tougher grass stains on fabric, you can make a paste of baking soda and a little water. Apply it to the stain, let it sit for 15-20 minutes, then scrub gently and rinse.

### Food and Drink Stains

Juice, ketchup, chocolate – kids love to spill. These stains vary in difficulty.

*   **Method**: For fresh food stains, blot immediately with a clean cloth to absorb as much as possible. Then, use a mild dish soap solution. Apply it to the stain with a damp cloth and gently blot or wipe. Rinse with a clean, damp cloth. For greasy food stains, a small amount of liquid laundry detergent can be effective. If the stain persists, a paste of baking soda and water can draw out the stain. Apply the paste, let it dry, then brush it off. If your child's shoes are particularly stained, a broader approach to stain removal might be helpful. Check out our detailed guide on [how to clean stained shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-stained-shoes).

### Scuffs and Marks

Scuff marks are common, especially on white rubber soles and synthetic uppers.

*   **Method**: For rubber soles, a magic eraser works wonders. Gently rub the eraser over the scuff. For tougher scuffs on white rubber, a small amount of toothpaste (non-gel, non-abrasive) can also work. Apply with an old toothbrush, scrub gently, and wipe clean. For scuffs on synthetic uppers, a pencil eraser can often lift the marks. Always test on a hidden spot first. If you need to refresh white shoes with persistent scuffs or marks, you might find more techniques in our article on [how to clean white shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-white-shoes). This can make a big difference on your child's light-colored footwear.

Remember to always test any cleaning solution on an inconspicuous area first. This ensures it does not damage or discolor the shoe material. Work slowly and gently. Repeated light treatments are better than one harsh attempt.

## Machine Washing Kids Shoes: When and How

Machine washing can be a convenient option for certain types of kids' shoes. However, it is not suitable for all materials. Knowing when to machine wash and how to do it correctly prevents damage. I only machine wash shoes that I know can handle it.

First, check the shoe material. Canvas, some athletic sneakers with synthetic mesh, and certain fabric shoes are generally machine washable. Avoid machine washing leather, suede, wool, or shoes with delicate embellishments. These materials can be damaged by water or the agitation of a washing machine. If you are unsure, it is safer to hand wash.

If the shoes are machine washable, prepare them. Remove the laces and insoles. Knock off any loose dirt or mud. Pre-treat any major stains before putting them in the machine. This helps the machine wash be more effective.

Put the shoes in a mesh laundry bag or a pillowcase. Secure the pillowcase with a knot or rubber band. This protects the shoes from harsh tumbling. It also protects your washing machine from shoe components. I always add a few old towels to the wash. This helps balance the load and reduces the banging noise. The towels also provide gentle abrasion to help clean the shoes.

Use a mild laundry detergent. Avoid bleach or harsh chemicals. Select a gentle cycle on your washing machine. Use cold water. Hot water can shrink or damage shoe materials. A delicate or hand wash cycle is usually best. Do not use the dryer. High heat can melt glues, warp materials, or shrink shoes.

After the wash cycle finishes, remove the shoes immediately. Air drying is essential. Place the shoes in a well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight or heat. Stuff the shoes with paper towels or old newspaper. This helps absorb moisture and maintains the shoe's shape. Change the paper every few hours if it becomes too damp. It can take 24-48 hours for shoes to dry completely. Make sure they are totally dry before your child wears them again. Damp shoes can lead to odor or mold growth. ## Odor Removal and Inside Shoe Care for Children's Footwear

Kids' shoes often develop odors. This is due to sweat and bacteria. Regular cleaning of the outside of the shoe is important. However, the inside needs attention too. Addressing odors makes shoes more pleasant to wear. It also creates a healthier environment for your child's feet.

Sweat from feet creates a warm, moist environment inside shoes. Bacteria thrive in this setting. These bacteria break down sweat, producing unpleasant smells. This is a common issue with children's footwear, especially with active kids. Ignoring inside shoe care can lead to persistent odors. It can also encourage fungal growth.

Here are some effective methods for odor removal:

*   **Baking Soda**: Baking soda is a natural odor absorber. Sprinkle a generous amount inside each shoe at night. Make sure to get it into the toe area. Let it sit overnight. The baking soda absorbs moisture and neutralizes odors. In the morning, shake out the powder completely. You can use a vacuum cleaner with a hose attachment to remove all residue. I find this method works best for general shoe freshness.
*   **Foot Powder**: Regular foot powder can help prevent odors before they start. It absorbs moisture, keeping the foot and shoe drier. Encourage your child to use foot powder daily if their feet sweat a lot. This simple step can make a big difference.
*   **Dry Tea Bags**: Unused, dry tea bags can also absorb odors. Place a few tea bags inside each shoe overnight. Black tea works well because it contains tannins. Tannins help kill bacteria. This is a simple, eco-friendly option.
*   **Air Circulation**: After your child takes off their shoes, do not store them in a closed closet. Let them air out in a well-ventilated area. Remove insoles if possible. This allows moisture to evaporate, preventing bacterial growth. I always tell my kids to put their shoes where they can breathe.
*   **Washing Insoles**: As mentioned earlier, insoles are a primary source of odor. If removable, hand wash them with mild soap and water. Scrub gently. Rinse thoroughly and let them air dry completely before putting them back in the shoes. For a deeper dive into cleaning the inner parts of shoes, consult our guide on [how to clean the inside of shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-the-inside-of-shoes). This will help ensure freshness from the inside out.

For very stubborn odors, a shoe deodorizer spray or disinfectant might be needed. Choose one specifically designed for shoes. These products often contain ingredients that kill odor-causing bacteria. Always follow product instructions carefully. By regularly addressing inside shoe care, you can keep your child's shoes smelling fresh. This prevents uncomfortable odors and promotes foot hygiene. ## Drying Kids Shoes Properly and Preventing Future Dirt

Proper drying is the final and crucial step in cleaning kids' shoes. Incorrect drying can lead to several problems. These include lingering odors, material damage, or even mold growth. Preventing future dirt also extends the time between deep cleans.

Always air dry shoes. Never use a machine dryer. High heat from a dryer can melt the adhesives that hold shoes together. It can also cause materials like rubber or synthetic leather to shrink, crack, or warp. This permanently damages the shoes. Direct sunlight can also cause fading or damage to certain materials. Find a spot with good air circulation, away from direct heat sources like radiators or sunlight. I often put shoes outside in the shade on a sunny, breezy day.

To help shoes dry faster and maintain their shape, stuff them. Use clean paper towels or old newspaper. Crumple the paper and stuff it firmly into the toe box and body of the shoe. The paper absorbs moisture from the inside. Change the paper every few hours, especially if it becomes damp. This speeds up the drying process. Make sure the shoes are completely dry before your child wears them again. This usually takes 24 to 48 hours. Damp shoes become breeding grounds for bacteria and mold, leading to bad smells.

Preventing future dirt is easier than constant deep cleaning. Here are some proactive steps:

*   **Protective Sprays**: Consider using a water and stain repellent spray. These sprays create a barrier on the shoe's surface. This makes it harder for dirt and liquids to penetrate the material. Apply the spray to new or freshly cleaned shoes. Always follow the product instructions. Test on an inconspicuous area first.
*   **Regular Wiping**: Encourage your child (if old enough) or make it a habit to wipe down shoes regularly. A quick wipe with a damp cloth after outdoor play can remove surface dirt before it sets in. This takes only a minute but saves a lot of cleaning effort later.
*   **Shoe Rotation**: Having multiple pairs of shoes allows them to air out and dry completely between wears. This reduces moisture buildup and helps prevent odors. Rotating shoes also extends the life of each pair.
*   **Proper Storage**: Store shoes in a cool, dry, well-ventilated area. Avoid piling them in a damp closet. This helps prevent moisture and odor issues. Shoe racks or open shelving work well.

By focusing on proper drying and taking preventive measures, you keep your child's shoes cleaner for longer. This reduces the frequency of intensive cleaning. It also ensures their shoes stay fresh, comfortable, and durable. This means less work for you and happier feet for your child.

## FAQ Section

### Q1: Can I put all kids shoes in the washing machine?
No, not all kids shoes are suitable for machine washing. Canvas and certain synthetic sneakers are generally safe. Leather, suede, wool, or shoes with delicate parts should never go into the washing machine. Always check the shoe material and care label first to avoid damage. Machine washing can cause materials to shrink or glues to melt. Learn more about [how to clean shoes in the washer](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-shoes-in-the-washer).

### Q2: How often should I clean my child's shoes?
The frequency depends on how often your child wears the shoes and how dirty they get. For active children, a quick wipe-down after each use is good. A deeper clean every few weeks, or when visibly dirty, is recommended. Regular quick cleans prevent dirt buildup and extend shoe life.

### Q3: What if my child's shoes have mold?
If your child's shoes have mold, address it immediately. Do not machine wash moldy shoes, as it can spread spores. Instead, use a solution of white vinegar and water (1:1 ratio) or rubbing alcohol. Scrub the affected area with a brush. Wipe clean and allow to air dry completely in a well-ventilated area. For detailed steps, refer to our guide on [how to clean mold off shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-mold-off-shoes).

### Q4: How do I get yellow stains off white kids shoes?
Yellow stains on white kids shoes, especially on rubber soles, can often be treated. Make a paste of baking soda and a small amount of hydrogen peroxide. Apply the paste to the yellow areas and let it sit for a few hours in sunlight. Then, scrub gently with an old toothbrush and rinse. This method helps lift yellowing. For more tips on restoring white shoes, read about [how to clean yellow shoes](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-yellow-shoes).

### Q5: Is it okay to use bleach on kids shoes?
Generally, no. Avoid using bleach on kids' shoes. Bleach can damage shoe materials, especially colored fabrics, causing discoloration or weakening fibers. It can also leave harsh chemical residues. Stick to mild soaps, detergents, or natural cleaners like baking soda and vinegar for safer cleaning.
