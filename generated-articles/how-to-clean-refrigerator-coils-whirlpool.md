## How To Clean Refrigerator Coils Whirlpool: A Simple Guide

Your refrigerator works hard day and night. It keeps your food fresh and safe. But did you know a tiny part of its operation often gets overlooked? I am talking about the refrigerator coils. These coils are vital for your appliance's performance.

Learning how to clean refrigerator coils on your Whirlpool unit is a game-changer. Dirty coils make your fridge work harder. This uses more energy and shortens its life. I will show you how to clean Whirlpool refrigerator coils safely. You will learn the importance of this task and simple steps to do it yourself. This guide covers everything from preparation to finishing touches.

*   **Save Money:** Less energy use means lower electricity bills.

Cleaning Whirlpool refrigerator coils involves turning off power, locating coils (usually at the back or bottom), and using a brush and vacuum to remove dust and debris. ### Understanding Whirlpool Refrigerator Coils: Why Cleaning Matters

Have you ever wondered what keeps your food cold? The evaporator and condenser coils do this important job. Evaporator coils are inside your refrigerator; they absorb heat. Condenser coils are outside; they release this heat into your kitchen. These coils are part of a closed system. Refrigerant flows through them. This substance changes from a liquid to a gas and back again. This cycle creates the cooling effect you need.

Over time, dust, pet hair, and dirt collect on the condenser coils. These coils are often found on the back or under your Whirlpool refrigerator. When coils get dirty, they cannot release heat efficiently. This forces your refrigerator's compressor to work harder. The harder it works, the more electricity it uses. This means higher energy bills for you. It also causes wear and tear on the appliance.

Cleaning these coils is not just about saving money. It is also about extending the life of your appliance. A clean refrigerator runs smoothly. It cools food properly. I find that this small effort brings big benefits. It is a key part of good home appliance care. Dirty coils can also lead to your refrigerator struggling to maintain correct temperatures, possibly spoiling food.

### Identifying Your Whirlpool Refrigerator Coil Location

Knowing where to find your Whirlpool refrigerator coils is the first step to cleaning them. Whirlpool models vary. Most refrigerators have their condenser coils in one of two places. They are either at the back of the unit or underneath it.

Older Whirlpool models often have coils visible on the back. These look like black or gray grates. They are easy to spot and reach. For these models, you will simply pull the refrigerator away from the wall. You will see the coils ready for cleaning. This placement makes the job quite straightforward.

Many newer Whirlpool refrigerators place coils underneath the unit. You access these coils from the front. You might need to remove a kick plate or grille at the bottom. This part is usually snapped on or held by a few screws. Look for vents or a removable cover. You will find the condenser coils behind this cover. They are often near the compressor. I advise checking your specific Whirlpool model's manual if you are unsure. This ensures you find the exact location and proper removal instructions. Understanding coil location is crucial for safe and effective cleaning. It helps you prepare for the task correctly. ### Essential Tools and Safety Steps for Cleaning Coils

Before you start cleaning, gather your tools. You will need a few simple items. A coil cleaning brush is essential. This brush has long, thin bristles. It reaches between the coil fins. You will also need a vacuum cleaner. A hose attachment with a narrow nozzle is best. A pair of work gloves protects your hands. Safety glasses shield your eyes from dust. Finally, have a damp cloth ready for wiping.

Safety is paramount when working with appliances. The very first step is to unplug your Whirlpool refrigerator from the wall outlet. This removes all electrical power. It prevents any risk of electric shock. Never attempt to clean coils with the power on. Next, if your refrigerator has a water dispenser or ice maker, turn off the water supply. This prevents any leaks while you move the unit.

You should also clear the area around the refrigerator. Pull the unit away from the wall if coils are on the back. Give yourself enough room to work comfortably. For bottom coils, remove any items in front of the kick plate. Proper ventilation is also a good idea. Open a window or turn on a fan. This helps clear dust from the air. Following these steps ensures a safe cleaning process. My goal is to help you clean your coils without any issues.

### Step-by-Step Guide: How to Clean Refrigerator Coils Whirlpool

Cleaning your Whirlpool refrigerator coils is a straightforward process. Following these steps ensures a thorough and safe clean. I always recommend taking your time.

#### 1. Disconnect Power and Access Coils

First, unplug your Whirlpool refrigerator from the power outlet. This is the most important safety step. If your fridge has an ice maker, turn off its water supply. Then, locate the coils. If they are on the back, gently pull the refrigerator away from the wall. Ensure you have enough space to work. If the coils are at the bottom, locate the kick plate or grille. This is usually at the bottom front. Carefully remove it. It might snap off or have a few screws. You will find the condenser coils visible behind this plate.

#### 2. Brush Away Loose Debris

Once you access the coils, grab your coil cleaning brush. This brush is designed to reach between the narrow fins of the coils. Start brushing gently. Remove any large clumps of dust, dirt, or pet hair. Work from top to bottom. Allow the debris to fall to the floor or into a tray you place underneath. Be gentle to avoid bending the delicate coil fins. I find that this initial brushing makes vacuuming much easier.

#### 3. Vacuum the Coils and Surrounding Area

After brushing, use your vacuum cleaner with a hose attachment. A narrow nozzle works best here. Vacuum the coils thoroughly. Get into all the nooks and crannies. Remove all the loose dust and debris the brush dislodged. Also, vacuum the floor area underneath and around the coils. This includes the fan and compressor area. These components often collect dust too. A clean environment around the coils helps with air circulation. This step is critical for efficient heat exchange. If your specific model is a Whirlpool, you can find more detailed instructions on how to clean coils on Whirlpool refrigerator [here](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-coils-on-whirlpool-refrigerator).

#### 4. Wipe Down and Reassemble

After vacuuming, use a damp cloth. Lightly wipe down the coils and surrounding surfaces. This removes any remaining fine dust. Do not use excessive water. The area should be just damp, not wet. Ensure everything is dry before you plug the refrigerator back in. Replace the kick plate or grille if you removed it. Push the refrigerator back into its position against the wall. Finally, plug the refrigerator back into the electrical outlet. Turn the water supply back on if you turned it off. Your Whirlpool refrigerator is now ready to run more efficiently. This task is a simple way to maintain your appliance. ### Maintaining Clean Coils: Best Practices for Whirlpool Refrigerators

Cleaning your Whirlpool refrigerator coils is not a one-time event. It is a recurring task. Establishing a regular cleaning schedule is a smart move. I recommend cleaning the coils at least once or twice a year. If you have pets that shed a lot, or if your home is particularly dusty, you might need to clean them more often. Three to four times a year is a good target for pet owners. Consistent maintenance prevents heavy dust buildup. This ensures your refrigerator runs at peak efficiency. It also helps avoid strain on the cooling system.

Prevention is also key. You can reduce how quickly dust builds up. Keep the area around your refrigerator clean. Regularly vacuum the floor around and under the appliance. This simple habit helps reduce the amount of dust and pet hair that gets drawn into the coils. If your Whirlpool refrigerator is in a high-traffic area, consider a small rug or mat. This can trap some dust before it reaches the coils. These small actions contribute significantly to long-term coil cleanliness.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid When Cleaning Coils

When cleaning refrigerator coils, some mistakes can hinder your efforts or even damage the appliance. First, never use harsh chemicals or excessive water. Coils are delicate. Chemicals can corrode them. Too much water can cause electrical shorts or rust. Stick to dry brushing and vacuuming. A slightly damp cloth is fine for wiping.

Second, avoid bending the coil fins. These fins are thin and easily damaged. Bent fins block airflow. This reduces the coils' ability to dissipate heat. Be gentle with your coil brush and vacuum nozzle. If you accidentally bend a fin, you can try to gently straighten it with a thin, flat tool, like a butter knife, but use extreme caution.

Finally, do not forget to unplug the refrigerator. This safety step is vital. Ignoring it puts you at risk of electric shock. Always ensure the unit is completely powered down before you touch the coils. Following these tips helps you clean your Whirlpool refrigerator coils safely and effectively. ### Troubleshooting Common Issues After Coil Cleaning

Sometimes, after you clean your Whirlpool refrigerator coils, you might notice issues. Do not panic. Most problems have simple solutions.

#### Refrigerator Still Not Cooling Well

If your refrigerator does not cool properly after a coil cleaning, check a few things. First, ensure the refrigerator is plugged in fully. Make sure the circuit breaker is not tripped. Sometimes, a loose plug or a tripped breaker can be the cause. Also, verify that the temperature settings are correct. You might have accidentally changed them.

Dirty coils are often the main cause of poor cooling. If you cleaned them, but the problem persists, the issue might be deeper. It could be a faulty fan, a refrigerant leak, or a compressor issue. These problems usually need a professional. My advice is to call a certified technician if basic checks do not resolve the issue.

#### Strange Noises Coming From the Refrigerator

Hearing new noises after cleaning can be alarming. Sometimes, a fan blade might hit a wire or a piece of debris. This can happen if something was dislodged during cleaning. Unplug the refrigerator again. Carefully check the area around the fan and coils for anything obstructing the blades. Remove any debris you find.

A common noise is a hum or buzz. This is often normal operation. If it is a new, loud, or rattling sound, it might indicate a loose part or a struggling component. If you cannot find a clear obstruction, and the noise is persistent or loud, consider contacting Whirlpool customer service or a repair person. It is always better to address strange noises early. This prevents minor issues from becoming major repairs.

### Frequently Asked Questions

#### How often should I clean my Whirlpool refrigerator coils?

You should clean your Whirlpool refrigerator coils at least once every 6 to 12 months. If you have pets that shed a lot, or if your home is particularly dusty, I recommend cleaning them every 3 to 4 months. Regular cleaning prevents dust buildup, which improves efficiency. This helps your refrigerator run better.

#### What happens if I do not clean my refrigerator coils?

If you do not clean your refrigerator coils, dust and debris will build up on them. This buildup traps heat. The refrigerator's compressor must work harder to cool the interior. This leads to higher energy consumption and increased electricity bills. It also shortens the lifespan of your Whirlpool refrigerator and can lead to costly repairs.

#### Can I use water to clean refrigerator coils?

I do not recommend using large amounts of water to clean refrigerator coils. Excessive water can cause electrical shorts or rust. Use a dry coil cleaning brush and a vacuum cleaner for most of the dust. A slightly damp cloth is fine for a final wipe. Always ensure the refrigerator is unplugged before cleaning.

#### How do I know if my Whirlpool refrigerator coils need cleaning?

You can often tell if your Whirlpool refrigerator coils need cleaning by its performance. If your refrigerator runs constantly, makes more noise than usual, or feels warm on the back or bottom, dirty coils are likely the cause. Visually inspect them; if you see a thick layer of dust or pet hair, it is time for a clean.

#### Is it safe to clean refrigerator coils myself?

Yes, it is safe to clean refrigerator coils yourself. You must follow proper safety precautions. Always unplug the refrigerator from the power outlet before you begin. Use appropriate tools like a coil brush and vacuum. Be gentle when working around the coils to avoid damage. This simple DIY task saves you money and improves appliance efficiency.
