## Step-by-Step Guide to Cleaning Your Air Max 97

Cleaning your Air Max 97 shoes involves a few simple steps. Following these steps helps you clean effectively without damage. Take your time with each stage. This ensures a proper clean.

### Pre-Cleaning Preparation

First, prepare your shoes for cleaning. Remove the laces from both shoes. Take out the insoles too. You will clean these parts separately later. This allows you to reach all areas of the shoe.

Next, remove loose dirt. Take your dry, soft-bristled brush. Gently brush off any mud, dust, or debris. Start from the top of the shoe. Move towards the sole. Pay attention to the grooves and seams. This step prevents rubbing dirt further into the material during wet cleaning. I usually do this outside. It avoids spreading dirt inside my house.

### Cleaning the Upper Materials

Now, prepare your cleaning solution. Mix a small amount of mild soap with warm water in a bowl. Dip your soft brush or a microfiber cloth into the solution. Wring out excess liquid. The brush or cloth should be damp, not soaking wet.

Gently scrub the upper part of the shoe. Use circular motions. Focus on soiled areas. Be extra careful with mesh or reflective parts. These are more delicate. For stubborn marks, you can apply a bit more solution directly. Work slowly to lift the dirt. Do not press too hard.

The Air Max 97 often has various materials. It includes leather, mesh, and synthetic overlays. Adjust your pressure based on the material. Leather can handle more scrubbing than mesh. For white Air Max 97s, immediate cleaning is key to prevent stains from setting. You can find specific tips for keeping [white Air Max 97](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-white-air-max-97) spotless. Remember to clean all Nike Air Max 97 shoes with care. You can use the same gentle techniques for [cleaning Nike Air Max 97](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-nike-air-max-97) as for other Nike models.

### Cleaning the Midsole and Outsole

The midsole and outsole usually collect the most dirt. These parts are more durable. You can use a stiffer brush for them. Dip your brush into the cleaning solution. Scrub the midsole vigorously. Remove scuff marks and dirt.

For the outsole, pay attention to the traction patterns. Dirt gets stuck in these grooves. Use a detail brush or an old toothbrush to dig out stubborn grime. You can also use a cloth to wipe down the rubber. Make sure to clean all visible parts. This includes the air bubble.

### Rinsing and Drying

After scrubbing, rinse the shoes. Do not put them under running water. Instead, dampen a clean microfiber cloth with plain water. Wipe down the entire shoe. This removes any soap residue. Repeat this step until no soap foam remains. Soap left on the shoe can attract more dirt.

Once rinsed, pat the shoes dry with a clean towel. Then, allow them to air dry completely. Place them in a well-ventilated area. Keep them away from direct sunlight or heat sources. Sunlight can yellow materials. Heat can warp glue or materials. Stuffing crumpled paper inside helps absorb moisture and maintain shape. This drying process can take several hours, or even a full day. Ensure they are bone dry before wearing them.

## Handling Different Materials on Air Max 97

The Air Max 97 features a mix of materials. These materials need specific care during cleaning. Understanding each one helps you clean safely. It prevents damage and keeps your shoes looking great.

### Cleaning Mesh Areas

The mesh parts on your Air Max 97 are breathable. They are also delicate. Dirt can easily get trapped in the mesh weave. Use a very soft brush for these areas. A soft toothbrush works well. Dip the brush in your mild soap solution. Gently brush the mesh in small circles. Do not apply too much pressure.

After scrubbing, wipe the mesh with a damp cloth. Make sure all soap is gone. Mesh can absorb water easily. So, avoid saturating it. Too much water can cause yellowing or damage to the fabric structure. I always wipe mesh very lightly. This protects the fabric.

### Cleaning Leather and Synthetic Overlays

Air Max 97s often have leather or synthetic overlays. These materials are more durable than mesh. You can use a slightly firmer brush on them. Dip your brush in the cleaning solution. Scrub these areas with gentle pressure. For tough stains on leather, you might use a dedicated leather cleaner. Always test it on a small, hidden area first.

Wipe leather and synthetic parts thoroughly with a clean, damp cloth. Remove all soap residue. After drying, you might consider a leather conditioner. This helps keep leather soft and prevents cracks. Synthetic overlays usually do not need conditioning. Just ensure they are clean and dry.

### Caring for Reflective Strips

The Air Max 97 is famous for its reflective stripes. These strips can lose their shine if not cared for properly. They are also sensitive to harsh scrubbing. Use only a very soft cloth or sponge on these parts. Dip the cloth in your mild cleaning solution. Gently wipe the reflective strips. Do not scrub hard.

Avoid abrasive cleaners on these strips. They can scratch or dull the reflective material. After cleaning, wipe them dry immediately with a clean cloth. This prevents water spots. Keeping them clean helps maintain their unique look. I always treat reflective parts with extra care.

## Dealing with Stubborn Stains and Odors

Sometimes, your Air Max 97s face more than just dirt. Stubborn stains or lingering odors require special attention. Do not worry. There are ways to tackle these issues effectively. You can restore your shoes' freshness.

### Spot Cleaning Stubborn Stains

For tough stains like mud, grease, or grass, act quickly. The sooner you treat a stain, the easier it is to remove. First, try your standard mild soap solution. Apply it directly to the stain. Let it sit for a minute or two. Then, gently scrub with a soft brush.

If the mild soap does not work, consider a specialized stain remover. Many sneaker cleaning kits include these. Always test the product on an inconspicuous area first. This checks for discoloration. Follow the product's instructions carefully. For oil-based stains, a small amount of rubbing alcohol on a cotton swab might work. Dab, do not rub, to lift the stain. I have used a magic eraser for scuffs on the midsole. It works wonders but use it carefully.

### Removing Bad Odors

Odors typically come from bacteria growth inside the shoe. Addressing these smells is important for hygiene. First, ensure the shoes are thoroughly dry. Moisture fosters bacteria. You can sprinkle baking soda inside the shoes. Let it sit overnight. Baking soda absorbs odors naturally. Then, shake it out.

For a stronger solution, mix water and white vinegar in a spray bottle. Spray lightly inside the shoes. The vinegar neutralizes odors. Allow the shoes to air dry completely afterwards. You can also place dryer sheets inside your shoes. They absorb odors and leave a fresh scent. Regular cleaning of insoles also helps. You can hand wash insoles with soap and water. Ensure they are fully dry before returning them to the shoes. This helps prevent future smells.

These help maintain the shoe's shape. They prevent creasing. Cedar shoe trees also help absorb moisture and odors. Avoid storing dirty shoes. Always clean them before putting them away. ## Common Mistakes to Avoid When Cleaning Air Max 97

Cleaning your Air Max 97s properly is crucial. Making mistakes can damage them. Avoid these common errors to keep your sneakers safe and looking good.

### Using Harsh Chemicals

Never use strong bleaches, harsh detergents, or industrial cleaners on your Air Max 97s. These chemicals can strip colors. They can also damage materials. They might weaken glues. This leads to parts falling off. Stick to mild soaps or specialized sneaker cleaners. Always read product labels. Ensure they are safe for your shoe materials. I once used a strong cleaner on an old pair, and it ruined the color. Learn from my mistake.

### Machine Washing Your Air Max 97s

Do not put your Air Max 97s in a washing machine. The washing machine's agitation can damage the shoe's structure. It can break down the cushioning system. It can also cause materials to shrink or warp. The heat from a dryer is also very harmful. Hand washing is always the safest method for these delicate sneakers.

### Improper Drying Techniques

Drying your shoes correctly is as important as cleaning them. Never use a dryer or direct heat source. High heat can melt glues. It can deform the shoe's shape. It can also cause plastic components to crack. Always air dry your shoes in a cool, well-ventilated area. Keep them away from direct sunlight. Sunlight can yellow white parts. It can also fade colors. Patience is key during the drying process. Ensure they are completely dry before you wear them again. This prevents odors and mildew.

## FAQs About Cleaning Air Max 97

### Can I put my Air Max 97 in the washing machine?
No, you should not put your Air Max 97 in a washing machine. The harsh cycles and high heat can damage the shoe's delicate materials, glue, and overall structure. It can cause discoloration, shrinkage, and separation of components. Always opt for hand cleaning methods to preserve your sneakers.

### How do I dry my Air Max 97 shoes after cleaning?
After cleaning, air dry your Air Max 97 shoes. Place them in a well-ventilated area, away from direct sunlight or artificial heat sources like radiators or dryers. Stuff them with crumpled paper towels or a shoe tree to absorb moisture and maintain their shape. Allow them to dry completely, which can take up to 24 hours.

### What products are best for cleaning Air Max 97?
Mild soap mixed with water is often sufficient. You can use dish soap or laundry detergent diluted in warm water. For more specialized cleaning, use dedicated sneaker cleaning solutions and kits. These products are formulated to be safe for various shoe materials found on Air Max 97s, including mesh, leather, and synthetics.

### How often should I clean my Air Max 97?
The frequency depends on how often you wear them and where. For daily wear, a quick wipe-down after each use is good. A deep clean every few weeks or once a month is ideal to prevent dirt buildup. If they get very dirty or stained, clean them immediately to avoid permanent marks.

### How do I remove scuff marks from the midsole of my Air Max 97?
For scuff marks on the midsole, a magic eraser works well. Lightly dampen the magic eraser and gently rub the scuffed areas. Be careful not to press too hard or rub on painted or delicate areas. For general dirt, a soft brush with cleaning solution effectively removes grime from the midsole's surface.
