### Mechanical Clog Removal Methods

When chemical cleaners fail, mechanical methods often succeed. These methods directly tackle the physical blockage. They do not rely on dissolving the clog. They are safer for your pipes and the environment. I always recommend trying these methods first for severe clogs.

*   **Plunging:** A simple plunger can be very effective. Ensure you have a good seal around the drain. Fill the tub with a few inches of water. This helps create suction. Pump the plunger vigorously for 10-20 seconds. Repeat this action several times. The force of the water can dislodge the clog.
*   **Drain Snake/Auger:** For deeper or tougher clogs, a drain snake is useful. These tools are available at hardware stores. Feed the cable into the drain opening. Turn the handle to advance it. When you feel resistance, you have hit the clog. Rotate the snake to break up or hook the clog. Pull it out slowly.
*   **Removing the Pop-Up Stopper:** Sometimes, hair wraps around the pop-up stopper mechanism. This creates a partial blockage. You can usually remove the stopper by twisting or lifting it. Clean any hair or debris by hand. This simple step can often fix slow drains.

Always exercise care with mechanical tools. Do not force the snake. This can damage pipes. If you feel unsure, stop. Call a professional.
