## Furrion Tankless Water Heater Troubleshooting: Simple Fixes

Having consistent hot water is a comfort we often take for granted, especially when living on the go. Furrion tankless water heaters are popular choices for RVs and mobile homes. They provide hot water on demand. But like any appliance, they can encounter issues. When your Furrion tankless water heater acts up, it can disrupt your day.

I understand the frustration of a cold shower or no hot water for dishes. This article helps you diagnose and fix common problems with your Furrion tankless water heater. We will cover troubleshooting steps for various issues. These include no hot water, error codes, low pressure, and strange noises. You will also find tips for essential maintenance. This guide aims to help you get your unit working well again.

### Takeaway

*   **Check Power & Gas Supply First:** Ensure the unit receives power and has adequate gas flow.
*   **Decipher Error Codes:** Refer to your manual to understand specific error messages and their solutions.
*   **Address Water Flow:** Clean filters and descale the unit to resolve low water pressure or inconsistent heating.
*   **Inspect Ignition System:** Verify the igniter and gas burner for proper function.

### How do I troubleshoot a Furrion tankless water heater?

To troubleshoot a Furrion tankless water heater, first check power and gas supply. Next, review the display for error codes. Clean the water filter and descale the unit if flow issues or sediment are present. Ensure proper water pressure. If problems persist, consult the owner's manual or a certified technician for specific component checks.

### No Hot Water or Inconsistent Temperature

A common and frustrating problem with any water heater is a lack of hot water. Sometimes, you get a burst of warmth, then it quickly turns cold. This issue with your Furrion tankless water heater can stem from several sources. Understanding these causes helps you find the right solution. I always start by checking the basics.

First, ensure the unit has power. Check the circuit breaker for your RV or home. Make sure it is not tripped. If the unit does not have power, it cannot heat water. Also, verify the gas supply. Ensure propane tanks are full and valves are open. A closed valve means no fuel for the burner.

Low water flow is another frequent cause of inconsistent temperatures. Tankless heaters require a minimum flow rate to activate. If water pressure is too low, the unit may not trigger. Sediment buildup in pipes or the heater itself can restrict flow. I have seen this issue many times. You can improve water flow by cleaning the inlet filter. A malfunctioning thermostat can also lead to incorrect water temperatures. The thermostat tells the heater how hot to make the water. If it sends wrong signals, you get lukewarm or cold water.

### Decoding Furrion Tankless Water Heater Error Codes

Your Furrion tankless water heater display panel shows error codes. These codes are valuable clues. They tell you what is wrong with the unit. Each code points to a specific issue. Knowing what these codes mean helps you troubleshoot effectively. I always recommend checking the owner's manual first. The manual provides a complete list of codes and their meanings.

Common Furrion error codes include:

*   **E1 (Ignition Failure):** This code means the burner failed to light. Check your gas supply. Ensure the propane tank has fuel and valves are open. Inspect the igniter and gas valve. They might be dirty or faulty. Sometimes, low gas pressure causes this. Make sure other gas appliances work correctly.
*   **E2 (Overheat Protection):** This code indicates the water temperature inside the unit got too high. This is a safety shutdown. It often happens due to restricted water flow or a faulty temperature sensor. Clear any blockages. Check for kinks in water lines. Descaling the unit can help if mineral buildup restricts flow.
*   **E3 (Flame Out During Operation):** The burner ignited but then went out. This suggests inconsistent gas supply or a faulty flame sensor. Check the gas pressure again. Ensure proper ventilation. A blocked flue can also cause the flame to go out.
*   **E4 (Water Flow Sensor Fault):** This means the water flow sensor is not working correctly. The sensor detects water moving through the unit. If it fails, the heater won't activate. Check if the water inlet filter is clogged. Cleaning it might resolve the issue. If not, the sensor may need replacement.
*   **E5 (Exhaust Temperature Too High):** This points to a problem with the exhaust system. The vent might be blocked. Ensure nothing obstructs the exhaust pipe. Proper ventilation is critical for safety and performance. This can also indicate an issue with the fan.
*   **E6 (Inlet/Outlet Temperature Sensor Fault):** These sensors monitor water temperature. If they are faulty, the unit cannot regulate heat. The water might be too hot or too cold. This usually requires replacing the specific sensor.

After identifying the error code, you can take specific steps. Many error codes relate to maintenance issues. For example, E2 and E4 often point to mineral buildup. Regular cleaning prevents these problems. Descaling removes calcium and lime deposits. This improves efficiency and prevents shutdowns. ### Addressing Low Water Pressure in Your Furrion Unit

Low water pressure affects your Furrion tankless water heater's performance. It can prevent the unit from activating or cause inconsistent heating. ### Resolving Ignition and Gas Supply Problems

Ignition issues are a primary reason a Furrion tankless water heater fails to produce hot water. The unit needs a reliable flame to heat water. If the burner does not ignite, or the flame is weak, you will have cold water. I find that gas supply is often the first thing to check.

First, ensure adequate gas supply. For RVs, check the propane tank level. A low tank will not provide enough pressure for the heater. Make sure the propane tank valve is fully open. Also, check the main gas shut-off valve to the water heater. It must be in the "on" position. If you recently refilled your propane tank, burp the lines. Turn off all gas appliances, then slowly open the main tank valve. This releases any air trapped in the lines. Test other gas appliances to confirm general gas flow.

Next, inspect the igniter. The igniter creates the spark that lights the gas. If it is dirty or worn, it may not spark consistently. Look for any carbon buildup on the igniter probe. Gently clean it with a soft brush if necessary. A faulty igniter might need replacement. You should hear a distinct clicking sound when the water heater tries to light. If you hear no click, the igniter or its control module might be the problem.

### Dealing with Leaks and Strange Noises

Leaks and unusual noises from your Furrion tankless water heater can be alarming. Addressing these issues promptly is important. Leaks can cause water damage. Strange noises often indicate underlying mechanical problems. I take these signs seriously.

**Leaks:**

*   **Loose Connections:** The most common cause of leaks is loose fittings. Check all water inlet and outlet connections. Ensure they are hand-tight, then use a wrench for a quarter turn more. Do not overtighten, as this can strip threads.
*   **Pressure Relief Valve (T&P Valve):** The temperature and pressure relief (T&P) valve can sometimes leak. This valve is a safety device. It opens if pressure or temperature gets too high. A small drip might be normal during operation. Continuous dripping indicates excessive pressure or a faulty valve. Check your water pressure. If it is too high, install a pressure reducer. If the valve is old, it might need replacement.
*   **Heat Exchanger:** A leak from the heat exchanger is serious. This component heats the water. Leaks here often mean the unit is old or has severe corrosion. Repairing a heat exchanger leak is usually expensive. Often, unit replacement is a better option. This type of leak requires professional assessment.
*   **Frozen Pipes:** In cold weather, water trapped in pipes or the unit can freeze and expand. This causes cracks and leaks. Ensure your RV or home is properly winterized. Use the heater's anti-freeze function if available.

**Strange Noises:**

*   **Knocking or Popping Sounds:** These noises often indicate sediment buildup inside the heat exchanger. As water heats, it boils trapped sediment. This causes popping sounds. Descaling the unit can remove these deposits. Regular descaling prevents this problem. Learn more about descaling your tankless unit at [how to clean tankless water heater](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-tankless-water-heater).
*   **Whistling or Squealing:** This typically points to restricted water flow. It can be a partially closed valve or a clogged inlet filter. The sound comes from water being forced through a small opening. Check all shut-off valves and clean your water filter.
*   **Grinding or Vibrating:** These sounds suggest a problem with the fan or pump. The exhaust fan might have a failing bearing. The water pump might be struggling. These issues usually require professional repair or part replacement.
*   **Gurgling:** This sound can indicate air trapped in the system. Bleeding the air out of your water lines can resolve this. Run hot water at all faucets until the gurgling stops.

Addressing these issues protects your investment. Regular descaling is crucial for avoiding noise from sediment. ### Essential Maintenance for Furrion Tankless Heaters

Proper maintenance is the cornerstone of a reliable Furrion tankless water heater. Regular care prevents most common issues. It extends the lifespan of your unit. I cannot stress enough how important preventive maintenance is. It saves you time, money, and frustration in the long run.

**1. Descaling (Flushing):**
This is the most critical maintenance task. Hard water leaves mineral deposits inside your heater. These deposits build up in the heat exchanger. They reduce efficiency. They can even cause damage. Descaling involves circulating a descaling solution or white vinegar through the unit. This breaks down the mineral buildup. I recommend descaling at least once a year. If you have very hard water, do it every six months. You can find detailed instructions on [how to clean tankless water heater](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-tankless-water-heater). The process for various tankless heaters is similar. You might also find helpful steps in our specific guides like [how to clean a rinnai tankless water heater](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-a-rinnai-tankless-water-heater) or [how to clean rinnai tankless water heater](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-rinnai-tankless-water-heater).

**2. Cleaning the Inlet Water Filter:**
Your Furrion tankless heater has a small filter at the water inlet. This filter catches sediment and debris from the water supply. It prevents them from entering the unit. This filter can become clogged. A clogged filter restricts water flow. This leads to low pressure or inconsistent heating. Inspect and clean this filter regularly. I typically clean it every few months. More often if your water source is known to be dirty. Our guide on [how to clean water heater filter](https://beacleaner.com/how-to-clean-water-heater-filter) shows you how.

**3. Checking the Exhaust Vent:**
The exhaust vent expels combustion gases. It must be clear and unobstructed. A blocked vent can lead to dangerous carbon monoxide buildup. It also causes the unit to shut down or run inefficiently. Periodically inspect the vent for blockages. Look for bird nests, leaves, or debris. Ensure the vent termination is clear. Proper airflow is essential for safe operation.

**4. Inspecting Gas and Water Connections:**
Regularly check all gas and water connections. Look for any signs of leaks. Tighten any loose fittings. Ensure gas lines are not crimped or damaged. A small leak can waste energy. A large leak poses a safety hazard. I always use a soapy water solution to check for gas leaks. Bubbles indicate a leak.

**5. Testing the T&P Valve:**
The temperature and pressure relief valve is a crucial safety device. It releases pressure if it gets too high. Test it annually. Carefully lift the lever on the T&P valve. A burst of water should discharge. This confirms it is working. If no water comes out, or it drips continuously, the valve might be faulty. It needs replacement.

**6. Winterization (for RVs/seasonal use):**
If you live in an RV or use your Furrion heater seasonally, proper winterization is vital. Draining the unit prevents water from freezing inside. Freezing water causes pipes to burst. Follow your manual's specific winterization steps. This often involves draining all water and sometimes introducing RV-safe antifreeze.

Following these maintenance steps keeps your Furrion tankless water heater running smoothly. It helps you avoid unexpected breakdowns. It also ensures you always have hot water when you need it.

### When to Call a Professional for Your Furrion Unit

While many Furrion tankless water heater problems have DIY solutions, some issues require professional help. Knowing when to call a technician is important. It ensures your safety and prevents further damage to the unit. I always recommend caution when dealing with gas and electrical systems.

**1. Gas Leaks:** If you suspect a gas leak, turn off the gas supply immediately. Evacuate the area and call your gas company or a certified plumber. Do not try to find or fix a gas leak yourself. Gas leaks are extremely dangerous. They pose fire and explosion risks. I never attempt to fix gas issues.

**2. Persistent Error Codes:** You have tried the basic troubleshooting steps for an error code. But the code keeps reappearing. This indicates a deeper problem. It might be a faulty sensor, a control board issue, or a complex system failure. Professionals have diagnostic tools. They can accurately identify the root cause. This prevents guessing. Working with electricity can be hazardous. Incorrect repairs can cause further damage or pose a shock risk.
